Episode 910: Regression Cook Four Beats

Grizzly Knights

Wade Wilson and Felicia Hardy spend a Tuesday night doing four small bad decisions in a row. Nobody calls it that. Everybody knows.

* * *

Time.

Space.

Reality.

It is more than a linear path. It is a prism of endless possibility,
where a single choice can branch out into infinite realities, creating
alternate worlds from the ones you know.

In every one of those realities, the same people live with the same
minds. The same hungers. The same scars. The same compensatory
mechanisms they would never name out loud.

I am the Watcher. I am your guide through these vast new realities.

Follow me, and ponder the question — not "what if?"

The question, in this universe, is simpler.

What do they do when they think the mic is off?

These are their stories.

* * *

“That depends on how he’s looking at me when he does it. Wade’s a walking Rorschach test—every gesture means something and nothing, and half the art is deciding which one it is that day,” Felicia said.

“Alright, Wade—cards on the table. What's your play tonight?” Felicia asked.

“Bluff? Me? Oh, honey, my entire existence is the poker table, the cards, the chips, the dealer, and the strategically placed mirror over your shoulder. Hell, even the felt’s got my name on it,” Wade said.

“Careful with your next step, Wade—depends who wants to come out of this room on top,” Felicia said.

“takes action, leaning forward and propping my chin on my hand like we're both about to get a pop quiz on each other's tells,” Wade said.

“Your turn, Wade—be very careful where you land,” Felicia said.

“takes a deep breath, looks at her like the tequila just stopped burning and left all the raw behind,” Wade said.

“So, Wade—what exactly did you think would happen if you let me see that?” Felicia asked.

“takes action, gripping the edge of the table with both hands like a lifeline, the humor stripped bare for once in my goddamn life,” Wade said.

The olive stem snaps between her teeth.

* * *

“Well, isn't this poetic,” Felicia said.

“Poetic, huh? That what we’re calling my endless tap-dance on a landmine of misery and mirth? Sure, Felicia, let’s Victorian novel this shit,” Wade said.

“Careful, darling. You might find I play better in the deep end,” Felicia said.

“What, like our little collision course of chaos and charm isn't Shakespearean already? Careful, kitty—all this tension and I might start soliloquizing…” Wade said.

“This whole dance? It's not about the cards, the jokes, or who's holding their ground. It's about us trying not to fucking blink first,” Felicia said.

“You wanna go bare? Fine. Let’s see if the poetry still holds up when the rhymes start breaking,” Wade said.

“Then let's see who can hold the heat, Wade,” Felicia said.

“You wanted raw? Here it is. No punchlines, no get-out-of-pain-free cards. Just me,” Wade said.

Smoke curls from the end of the Camel, catching briefly in the streetlight's sickly halo before dissolving. His free hand hangs loose at his side, fingers twitching once like they might close around something that isn’t there. The cards remain where he’s left them—face up, unflinching.

* * *

Eastbound.

“Silence has its own weight when you know how to use it, but the waiting—the pause—says you’re unwilling to break first. If he wants me to talk, he’ll have to earn it,” Felicia said.

“Tough crowd. Thought I’d go with ‘soft touch of lunatic sincerity.’ You bite first, or should I?” Wade asked.

“Fine,” Felicia said.

“Quiet? Oh, honey, this is my sultry, brooding antihero mode. Works better in moonlight, but I’ll make do. Want me to add a tortured sigh or is this already doing it for you?” Wade asked.

The cab swerves.

“The cab jerks, and my shoulder, one hand catching the seat to steady myself. It's instinct—something between keeping control and leaning into the moment just enough to keep it alive,” Felicia said.

“So, is this the part where we talk about the elephant in the room, or do we just make out and leave the emotional epiphanies for after taco night?” Wade asked.

“The thing, huh? Bold of you to assume this moment doesn’t have a little more finesse than that, sweetheart.’,” Felicia said.

Her nails tap the leather.

* * *

“Alright, Wade—if the boots are off and I’m barefoot in your world of carefully curated chaos, I’m not just visiting, am I?” Felicia asked.

“Trouble is the appetizer, tequila’s dessert, and that Switzerland thing you’re oh-so-curious about? The five-course meal everyone regrets but nobody forgets. Flask’s top left, Swiss secrets under lock, and good luck trying to win, sugar. This game’s rigged, and the house always…” Wade said.

“Wade, you talk a big game for a man who clearly thinks 'subtle' is a four-letter word. But let's get something straight—if I take what I want, it's not because you let me win. It's because you never saw me switch the pieces,” Felicia said.

“Your move, Black Cat. Hope you've got claws sharp enough and wit quick enough to stick with me, ‘cause once we start this dance, I'm not exactly known for tucking anyone in early,” Wade said.

The whiskey stays untouched. Felicia leans back against the counter, bare feet on cold linoleum. Wade’s fingers linger on the rim of his glass, tapping once, twice — a rhythm too careful to be casual.

“Nice try, princess, but if you’re starting a fire, better be ready to burn,” Wade said.

Felicia’s eyes flick down, then back up. Slow. Measuring. Wade doesn’t look away.

* * *

As for me, these are my stories.

I observe all that transpires here. But I do not, cannot, will not
interfere.

I have watched. I will continue to watch.

For I am the Watcher.
