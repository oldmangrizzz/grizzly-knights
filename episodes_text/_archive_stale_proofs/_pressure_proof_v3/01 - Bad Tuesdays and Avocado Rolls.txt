Episode 1: Bad Tuesdays and Avocado Rolls

Grizzly Knights

Felicia and Wade plot a scheme to unravel Peter Parker's tightly-wound web.

* * *

Time.

Space.

Reality.

It is more than a linear path. It is a prism of endless possibility,
where a single choice can branch out into infinite realities, creating
alternate worlds from the ones you know.

In every one of those realities, the same people live with the same
minds. The same hungers. The same scars. The same compensatory
mechanisms they would never name out loud.

I am the Watcher. I am your guide through these vast new realities.

Follow me, and ponder the question — not "what if?"

The question, in this universe, is simpler.

What do they do when they think the mic is off?

These are their stories.

* * *

Peter Parker.

“Oh-ho-ho, Felicia. That smile's got secrets dripping off it like Peter had some kind of sticky, slinger-related effect on you. Spill. This Spidey-dar never fails,” Wade said.

“Wade, sweetheart, if you're trying to crawl under my skin, you're picking the wrong cat. I’m an expert in pressure, remember? Emotional, physical, metaphysical. Peter’s a string I’ve played before—and beautifully, if I do say so myself. So the idea that I still want him or that little schoolboy angst of his is turning me inside out? Cute. But not really my tempo, darling,” Felicia said.

“Oh, Felicia, how dare you! Me? Secrets? Soft spots? With Peter? Why, I’d sooner propose to his mask than—wait, bad example—I'd sooner... uh... let my chimichanga get cold! Damn you, Catwoman Adjacent, you found my only weakness!” Wade said.

Wade's boot taps the metal leg of the table, rhythmic, impatient. Felicia’s fingers trace the rim of her glass, the condensation slick beneath her touch. Her other elbow rests firmly on the table, leaning closer, the scent of her Chanel Chance teasing the air between them.

“Gah! You've got me, Felicia! I’m stuck in the Parker Pull—his radioactive charm is like a black hole for my better judgment! But let’s not pretend you don’t enjoy a little spin in his orbit too. Admit it, we’re two satellites, caught in a webbed tango,” Wade said.

“A coalition, huh? You and me, tangled in schemes, messing up the spider’s plans… Now, that’s got the kind of spark only a felonious felidaen-orbiting-freakshow alliance could bring. Imagine the damage—no, the artistry. Intrigued yet, partner?”

Time holds. Her face is inches from his, the curve of a smirk threatening, pulling at the lines of her cheek. His mask folds slightly as he tilts his head, the unfiltered gaze behind it somehow softer, somehow sharper. No one breathes.

“Sharing stakes is tricky business, Felicia. If I drag Peter’s name here, it’s not just web fluid we’re tangling. So tell me—how sharp are your claws in this game? Because mine? They cut both ways,” Wade said.

Her nails click softly against the glass, a slow metronome to his silence. Wade leans in now, a hitch in his breath audible beneath the mask. Between them, the table feels smaller, tighter, as his hand drifts towards the expanse of avocado-streaked white. A gamble, already cast. Neither moves further.

“Alright, Felicia, deal me in. Cards on the table, claws and katanas out—what’s the play that knocks Peter off his sticky little pedestal?” Wade asked.

Their heads dip closer, a conspiracy sealed in the space where her whisper meets his low laugh. Her fingers finally brush his gloved hand, a calculated gesture met with stillness. The booth, electric now, hums with the weight of what they're about to do.

Two margaritas, one empty plate, and a name hanging between them like a loaded gun. The booth holds its breath.

“Let’s cut to the chase—why talk around Peter when we can pull him right into our little dance? Felicia, care to bait the bug?” Wade asked.

The phone glows between them, her message sent. Wade's laugh cuts the air, sharp and quiet. Their eyes meet—current seething under the surface. Peter is already in orbit.

* * *

The unopened bottle of tequila is still on the table. Neither of them has touched it.

Trust sits between them, as fragile and unspoken as the seal on the bottle.

“So, who wants to clarify before I regret asking?” Peter asked.

“That depends on the exact question and whether Peter's mask is up or off. Specifics. Lay it out for me,” Felicia said.

“Alright, Peter, what's it going to be? Clarity about me or clarity about you? Because those don’t tend to live in the same place.”

“Felicia, go claws-out head-on. I want a front-row seat for the Peter Parker Clarity Hour, with you slicing straight through his “but maybe.” I'll stir later—your strike first means I can torpedo whatever’s left standing. Go on, make him squirm; I'll grab snacks.” Wade said.

“True enough to keep him from spiraling, but just sharp enough so he remembers who he's dealing with. "Peter, honey, if you're waiting for me to spell out what you're too afraid to ask... I'm not exactly the type to do your homework for you,” Felicia said.

“Cards on the table, you two. I didn't sign up for couples trivia night,” Peter said.

“Felicia, escalate and make it sting—rub the raw nerve. I'll back your play and pitch fastballs while Peter buckles. Keep pressing; I'll enjoy every single twitch,” Wade said.

“Easy there, tiger. I'm not here to claw you open, but if you keep flinching every time we scratch the surface, maybe it’s not me you’re scared of,” Felicia said.

The bottle looms, unbroken. A silent dare. Its weight presses heavier than the words they are not saying.

“Alright, your move. Keep playing chicken, or let’s finally lay down the damn cards,” Peter said.

“Fine, Peter. You want it straight? I loved the mask, the danger, the myth. You. But Peter Parker came after, and I didn’t sign up for an ordinary life. You want me to want both? That’s never been the deal,” Felicia said.

“Oh, Pete! You're like a live-action telenovela, except with way less shirtless scenes and way more guilt spirals. Come on, big guy, Felicia’s already stripped you down to your narrative boxers—time to let it all out. We’re listening,” Wade said.

“Because if this turns into tequila confessions, someone better start with actual facts before we drown in all this atmosphere,” Peter said.

“Here, Pete—confession juice. Made from 100% agave and 200% bad ideas. Go on, take a swig. Liquid courage never killed anyone—me, specifically, but you're way less durable,” Wade said.

“Someone's gotta call it. So... do I take the lead, or are we just dancing in circles here?” Peter asked.

“Got it, Peter. We can sit here tripping over our own webs, or we can figure out whose bottle this really is. Your call,” Felicia said.

Her words fall like the crack of a whip, sharper than the promise trapped in glass. The tequila waits, its seal unbroken, a challenge none of them can ignore now.

* * *

Harsh white light. Mirrors, everywhere. A jungle of testers and acrylic shelves, islands of color and gloss arranged in military precision. The fragrance wall looms like a neon temptation, each bottle a promise or a lie. Somewhere in the distance, a low hum of bubblegum pop filters through the air, blending with the sharp tang of misplaced samples. Lipstick smeared on the edge of an unclaimed tissue. An overstepped puddle of shimmering primer on the ground near Aisle 3. This is no haven; it is a battlefield of insecurities sold as elevation.

“Mirrors can be such liars, don’t you think? They promise you truth, but all they really know are surfaces. Fascinating, in their own way, but never enough,” Felicia said.

“You know, sometimes I think the only difference between deep thoughts and naval gazing is a sexy French accent saying 'existential,” Wade said.

“Right, Wade. That’s one way to put it, or maybe just a cry for attention wrapped in a metaphor only he gets. Felicia, you and your entrances — ever the headliner. Should we just pass out playbills with glitter? Or is this my cue to run inventory on my excuses before the Spider-signal gets lit again?” Peter asked.

“Boys, boys—if I wanted a stand-up routine and a socially awkward Q&A, I’d have scheduled brunch. Capes down, focus up,” Felicia said.

Felicia, poised on the razor's edge of a stiletto, leans just enough to make Peter her anchor. Her hand finds his shoulder, fingernails faint against the worn cotton of a Parker hoodie, the gesture practiced and unthinking. Wade, half-slouched on the fragrance wall, adjusts the katanas strapped to his back—an audible shift of leather and steel—and lets the moment hang between them like a joke he hasn’t said out loud yet.

“Do you ever think smoke signals are wasted on rooms with no one left to read 'em?” Wade asked.

“Wade, buddy, your suggestive metaphor game is hitting a solid 8 out of 10 today. Honestly, it's almost inspiring—and mildly terrifying. But hey, group goals, right? We’re playing high-stakes poker with the ‘responsibility’ deck every day, so, uh, let’s maybe not reshuffle it by drawing extra chaos cards just yet?” Peter asked.

“take_action: My grip tightens, just the slightest pull of fingertips against Peter’s sleeve—like I’m not sure if I’m holding him in place or holding myself steady. The shift in his voice, the ache underneath… damn him. Damn me for hearing it,” Felicia said.

Felicia’s grip tightens, the faint flex of muscle visible in her wrist. Peter doesn’t move, but there’s a strain now—his body locked just enough to hold her weight and her words without breaking. Wade lingers at the edge of it all, head tilted just so, a stark stillness that betrays how ready his body is to make the jump from silence to noise.

“Sure, sure, we’re spiraling toward something deep and meaningful, but can we leave enough crumbs for a horny raccoon to follow us out? Asking for a friend. The raccoon is me. I want tacos,” Wade said.

“Wade, it’s… yeah, it’s funny. You’re funny. Always. Just—maybe let it breathe for a second. Not everything’s a punchline, okay?” Peter asked.

“take_action: I let go of Peter’s sleeve. But not fully—just a slow drag down his wrist until my hand falls away entirely, deliberate enough that the air doesn’t forget it,” Felicia said.

Felicia pulls back, slow, deliberate—a predator conceding ground but never intent. Her fingers trail from Peter’s shoulder, the absence colder than the hold. Wade shifts, straightening from his lean—just enough to let the weight shift in the room. Tension doesn’t dissolve; it shifts, rewired through the triangulation of her retreat, Parker’s stillness, and Wilson’s waiting grin.

* * *

Time.

Listen.

“So, is this where you hit me with some "cat's got your tongue" line, or do I just preemptively apologize for whatever I probably did this time?” Peter asked.

“Oh, Peter, let's not pretend you don't find the tilt as deliberate as the neckline. Should I add a little garnish just to keep you distracted?” Felicia asked.

“Casually throwing Peter off-kilter? Felicia's basically got the WWE belt in that sport. I mean, does not leaving a webhead slightly un-webbed count as cardio for her? Pretty sure it’s her default setting. I imagine she’s all draped over his arm like he's a musketeer and she's the damsel needing constant rescuing—except, ya know, she's the one plotting which pocket to pick next,” Wade said.

“Wade, I get it—you're jealous because your milkshake didn't bring the rooftop boys to the yard. But don't worry, we can't all have a web-slinging after-hours highlight reel,” Peter said.

“Boys, boys—if I wanted to watch posturing, I’d go sit ringside at a pro-wrestling match. Unless one of you is planning to take your shirt off, let’s skip to the part where you realize I’m the only one winning here,” Felicia said.

Tension.

“Oh, don’t let me stop the show. I’ll just be over here, trying to figure out if you’re going for Lady Catbeth or Ham-let-me-steal-your-dignity. Peter, buddy, you doing okay over there? Wanna tag out before this hits the soliloquy phase?” Wade asked.

“Alright, Wade, what’s it gonna be this time? A bad pun, a questionable anecdote, or are you just here to steal Felicia’s thunder like the human wrecking ball you are?” Peter asked.

“I lean in close, just enough to make them both refocus. "Gentlemen, if I wanted a dick-measuring contest, I’d hire a tailor, not a referee. Now, play nice—or I’ll show you how sharp this cat’s claws really are,” Felicia said.

“That’s it! That’s the closing number. Bravo, Felicia, bravo. Now, where's the part where Peter bursts out into heartfelt song about how conflicted he feels? Or wait—do you skip to the part where the audience forgets the stakes because the cat’s got his tongue and his balls? Stage directions unclear,” Wade said.

“Come on, guys—save the claws and the... whatever Wade's version is... for the bad guys. Or at least give me a five-second head start to grab some popcorn, because I feel like I’m about to watch performance art,” Peter said.

Noise.

“There—that's better. Are we talking about me now, or are you both still too distracted by your own egos to notice the masterpiece on your table?” Felicia asked.

* * *

Concrete.

“Too quiet. Wade's waiting on calamity, Peter's waiting on dread, and here I am... enjoying the cigarette like it's the actual thief in the night,” Felicia said.

“Alright, now that I've provided the grace of adding just enough macho-whimsy to this touching moment, what's next, Kitty Kat?” Wade asked.

“God, between the news-cycle-length summary from Wade and Uatu's "Deep Thoughts™ by Jack Handey," I'll wing it. Felicia thrives on control. Wade thrives on testing it. If I engage him right now—chaos. So, better to steady her footing,” Peter said.

“Peter’s playing at subtlety. Says he told me to distract Wade with the “Felicia Hardy Special.” You know, my usual charm offensive. But hey, if I owe him another one, it’s not like it’s newsworthy. Always me pulling off the heist and untangling these webs.” Felicia said.

There.

“Slippery and sharp—well, Wade, aren’t you just the bard of razor-edged compliments. Guess it’s a good thing I like my edges admired. Keeps things interesting,” Felicia said.

“Alright, sharp claws, your move. Do we banter, or do we brawl? Because honestly, both are kinda foreplay,” Wade said.

“Hey Wade, the mask does not filter smells, I promise you that. So unless your face wants to play flammable with my web fluid, keep escalating. Felicia, you ready to drop the saber-rattle or joust? God help me if we do both at once,” Peter said.

Close.

“You know, Wade, sharpness comes with responsibility... and an audience. So, are you watching closely, or looking for the next line in your sonnet?” Felicia asked.

“Alright, Felicia, you cutting in for the duet, or is this where you try and claw me outta step?” Wade asked.

“Felicia, Wade, look—I'm not saying "this table is the dangerous triangle." I'm saying, with Wade’s mouth and your claws, it’s less ‘table’ and more ‘triangular warzone.’ Play nice, or at least creative, yeah?” Peter asked.

“See, this is why I adore the web-head; he’s all in on the slapstick but can’t totally dodge sincerity, which is just mwah perfect victim material. Alright, Felicia, your teaching moment awaits!” Wade said.

“Your move, Wade. But remember—this stage belongs to me,” Felicia said.

Control.

* * *

Time.

Time.

“Wade narrating me narrating you, Peter. Charming. You boys going to keep thinking out loud, or should I let you figure out who the cherry was for?” Felicia asked.

“Oh, come on, Felicia. The cherry thing? Low-hanging fruit—and trust me, I know a thing or two about low-hanging, overripe situations. But you wanna talk about popping something? How 'bout I pop outta the ceiling vent into Peter’s bedroom again, see how red he gets this time? Real cherries blush less,” Wade said.

“Wade, buddy, you’re like a rejected Hallmark card—sharp, tasteless, and somehow making me embarrassed for both of us. You wanna try that again, or should I just send you a postcard from a better mood?” Peter asked.

“Felicia, I swear, with the way you toss bait, you’re trying to captain the S.S. Tension. You’re practically gift-wrapping the room’s energy. Next thing, you’ll be asking if any of us have been stretching for the next set of complicated entanglements. I stretch before I disappoint and after… because recovery matters. Now tell me, are we jumping to footnotes or just slapping another layer of subtext on this mess?” Wade asked.

“So are we tying knots, Wade, or are you just playing string games hoping we won't notice who's tangled where?” Felicia asked.

Surface.

* * *

Half a Mai Tai, crushed ice melting. A plate of Tex-Mex egg rolls pushed to the side, grease pooling beneath them. Felicia leans back, second margarita in hand, silver heels hooked onto the edge of her seat. Wade polishes off his third Jack and Coke as Peter picks at the remains of his untouched Cajun Jambalaya Pasta. The overhead lighting is soft, but not forgiving; no shadows to hide the look Wade just shot over his straw.

“Wade's sharp looks are like dogs barking in the distance—you don’t flinch unless they start coming closer. So, I just raise my glass and smirk at Peter,” Felicia said.

“Oh, the betrayal! First, I can’t make a yeast joke near Aunt May without the door hitting me on the ass on the way out, and now you’re telling me Precious Parker makes moans in his sleep that even you’ve heard? Is this… like, a cat-woman-super-hearing thing, or are you and Spidey actively comparing me to his wet-web-dream fodder?” Wade asked.

“Oh, yeah, great, like this is the hill I want to die on right now—Felicia and Wade comparing field notes on my sleep sounds. Absolutely normal and fine conversation topic. Totally not deeply horrifying or mortifying in any way,” Peter said.

“Oh, that’s cute. Blame-shifting while not actually saying anything—classic Parker playbook. I lean in, tilting my head just enough to give him the full VIP tour of a slow, unimpressed smile,” Felicia said.

“Oh, a deciding vote! Democracy is alive and well but still depressingly horny. I vote for... guilty with Spider-Induced Side Effects! I mean, c'mon, that guy? Parker's got Catholic guilt without being Catholic, super strength but close enough to losing every moral tug-of-war, and angst so thick you could spread it on toast. Even his deflections have layers,” Wade said.

“A burrito? A psychological burrito? Great, now I’m a late-night Chipotle order with unresolved trauma as the guac. Thanks, Wade. Real Nobel Prize-level analysis there,” Peter said.

“Peter trying to verbally spar with Wade is like bringing a slingshot to a bazooka fight, but adorable effort deserves acknowledgment. I rest an elbow on the table, propping my chin on my hand, and watch him spin like he’s on one of his own webs,” Felicia said.

“Oh, so we're playing Musical Deflections now? Fine, deal me in! My version of ‘dinner and a show’ depends entirely on the company:,” Wade said.

“Keep it pointed at me, Wade. Focus right here on this sad, guilt-wrapped burrito, and leave Felicia out of your Rated-M-for-Messy game of escalation. Seriously,” Peter said.

The corner of Felicia’s mouth curls—half-smirk, half-shield. Her nails, black lacquered, trail the rim of her glass without rhythm. Wade’s laugh cuts sharp, like a sudden gust, while Peter leans forward, voice low, a hand brushing hers on the table. She doesn’t pull away, but her posture shifts, one leg crossing over the other slow, deliberate. Body language as stilettoed as her heels.

“Oops—look, it slipped! Or maybe, just maybe, we’re all dancing around the spider in the room so hard that the music stopped and someone had to kill the silence. Now that I've got your attention—Felicia, Peter’s spins are cute, but my question is: are we playing dodgeball, or just seeing who cracks first? Because I’m not the guy you want holding the quiet card,” Wade said.

“And guess who swings in right on cue—Front-Row Parker! You test a theory hard enough, the universe sends you its webbed-up mascot. Peter, you're just in time to enforce the "Deadpool isn't allowed to redecorate mid-spiel" ordinance. Or, you know, take the heat for the messy tension I've now generously escalated from 'smoldering' to 'explosive.' You're welcome.”

“Wade, I swear, if this is your version of some bad beat poet trying to light the fuse just to watch it blow, pick another room. You might thrive on the chaos, but some of us still have to clean up the shrapnel,” Peter said.

Felicia’s drink pauses mid-lift, condensation slipping down her wrist. Wade grins sharp, too wide, the kind of smile that holds. Peter exhales slow and uneven, his hands tightening around the edge of the table, the one thing he can control. The air hums—three distinct weights pressing into the same fragile corner of reality. Tension here, laughter there, silence creeping between it all.

* * *

As for me, these are my stories.

I observe all that transpires here. But I do not, cannot, will not
interfere.

I have watched. I will continue to watch.

For I am the Watcher.
