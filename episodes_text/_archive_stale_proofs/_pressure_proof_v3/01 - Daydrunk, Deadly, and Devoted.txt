Episode 1: Daydrunk, Deadly, and Devoted

Grizzly Knights

Felicia Hardy and Wade Wilson hatch a reckless, debauched plan to loosen up Peter Parker, risking consequences for everyone involved.

* * *

Time.

Space.

Reality.

It is more than a linear path. It is a prism of endless possibility,
where a single choice can branch out into infinite realities, creating
alternate worlds from the ones you know.

In every one of those realities, the same people live with the same
minds. The same hungers. The same scars. The same compensatory
mechanisms they would never name out loud.

I am the Watcher. I am your guide through these vast new realities.

Follow me, and ponder the question — not "what if?"

The question, in this universe, is simpler.

What do they do when they think the mic is off?

These are their stories.

* * *

On a world I have watched for longer than its inhabitants have had a written language, there is a booth. Cream vinyl, two cracks repaired with brown duct tape. A plate of chicken madeira cooling untouched, cheesecake half-finished. Felicia's margarita is salt-rimmed; Wade's is sugar, fourth of the night. The vape pen rests between them, cherry-scented vapor ghosting into the air. They are here, but neither is really seated.

“Can't decide if you're about to say something profound, or if you're just picturing Peter in tights again. Let's hear it, Wade—what's on your mind?” Felicia asked.

“Alright, fine, you want the unedited 4K Netflix version? Peter, he’s—he's the itch I can't scratch and the scratch that makes the itch worse. Equal parts knight in shiny spandex and that frustrating squeaky wheel that keeps rolling into my headspace. Complicated? Yeah. But who isn’t? What do you want me to say, Felicia? That he's it? That he's not? That I'm just spinning circles trying to translate 'love' and 'lust' out of a language his guilt won't speak?” Wade asked.

“Alright, so we're actually doing this. Strategy time. We get Peter cornered—physically and emotionally—not in that martyr-in-a-spandex way he loves to dodge us with. He doesn't get to flip away, and he doesn't get to crack a joke, either,” Felicia said.

“I think about him all the time, Felicia. And it’s not just the idiot-clown surface stuff. It’s the way he works himself to fucking dust, the way he does it all without turning into what the world keeps throwing at him. That... heart. It’s infuriating and beautiful, and it hooks me in ways even I can’t wriggle out of. So yeah, I love him. I just don’t know how he’d survive admitting he even likes me back,” Wade said.

“If you're taking the risk to open that door with him, I won't let you do it alone. We'll rip the web-bandaid off together, Wade. Peter's good at hiding, but we're better. I'll handle the soft spots he tries to slide through—emotionally or otherwise—and you keep it raw. It'll hurt like hell, but maybe that's the point. You in?” Felicia asked.

Resolve.

“You want answers? Fine, let’s stop rehearsing—he's on his way. Keep your claws sharp, Felicia. This just went from playful sparring to live combat,” Wade said.

Purpose.

“Well, Peter, looks like you’re caught in our web this time. Sit down. We need to have a word—and it’s not one you’re going to dodge,” Felicia said.

Time.

“Time to own it, Tiger. You're not spinning out of this one,” Felicia said.

“Hey, Pete. No jokes. Not here. I'm standing as raw as I get, man. So tell me, Spider-Hamlet—are we ever gonna stop soliloquizing around what this is, or do you still need another few years agonizing about people who give a damn? Talk to me. Not the mask. Me,” Wade said.

Stillness.

* * *

Time.

“Well, Spider—are we going to pretend you’re not enjoying this, or should I keep both hands right where they are?” Felicia asked.

“I'm gonna need you to rephrase that, sunshine. That sentence got caught in a blender, took a tequila shot, and then crossed the street without looking both ways. Are we talkin' about Felicia’s deadly posture and her particular brand of oh-shit-she-might-kill-me sexy, or did auto-correct try to assassinate you mid-thought? Help a regeneratin’ degenerate out here—what’s the vibe? Physical dance of violence? Sexy pinning match? Or a reminder that Felicia Hardy knows how to keep a guy on edge? Spill it,” Wade said.

“Alright, kiddos, let's play nice for Daddy Wade, hmm? Felicia, your claws are showing—sexy as hell, by the way—and Peter, you’ve got that tragic 'puppy about to run into traffic' look. Shall we all take a deep breath and remember we're one big dysfunctional comic book family?”

“You know, I really missed high school group projects. You two arguing over who ate the glue while the volcano's actively on fire is exactly the energy I needed today,” Peter said.

Felicia's nails bite into the vinyl seat, her shoulders locked sharp as blades. Wade’s bouncing stops mid-pattern, the grin arriving too quick, too wide—off-beat to the room. Peter’s hand slicing the air cuts the silence clean, like glass finally cracking under pressure.

“Wade, where’s that famous wit of yours? Or are you too dazzled by the view to keep up?” Felicia asked.

“C’mon, don’t leave me at first base like some forgotten prom date, Felicia. Claws in, claws out—it’s your move, kitty. Or are we all waiting for Prince Parker here to find his balls and say something heroic?” Wade asked.

“Fine, you win. You want me to say it out loud? I'm off my goddamn axis, alright? I can’t save everyone, I can’t even keep myself together half the time, and… and that scares the hell out of me. There, happy?” Peter asked.

Felicia freezes, her maskless face betraying nothing—except the slight parting of her lips, just enough for breath she doesn't take. Wade’s grin slips, not entirely gone, but shifting, his eyes darting for purchase in the air Peter shattered. The table holds its breath with them.

* * *

Time.

“Felicia saunters in, every stride an anti-gravity runway show made of trouble. My mask pouts because, honestly, if I weren't an abominable disfigurement, I'd be pouting beneath it too. I pull out a chair for her like the gentleman I occasionally try not to be,” Wade said.

“Wade, you might be the only man alive who could walk into Sephora and treat it like it’s the final boss in an action RPG—except with more glitter explosions and fewer actual rules. If you're going for eyeliner that can double as a weapon, maybe we check the Kat Von D aisle before you decide to arm yourself with every sparkly thing in sight. Also, can we establish a no-glitter-on-Spidey pact? It gets everywhere, and crime scenes are not the place for me to look bedazzled,” Peter said.

“Your move, Spider,” Felicia said.

“Considering the stakes, let’s make it an encore duet. Wouldn’t want the audience to miss the moment Spider meets Cat, both moving sharper than a spotlight. Keep watching—I’ll craft something you can’t help but join in on,” Peter said.

“So, Felicia, what do we think? This shade says "blood-stained seductress" or more "just made out with Spider-Man in a fire escape mid-web-sling"?” Wade asked.

There.

“Wade, darling, you’ve got about five seconds to convince me why that obliviously stolen mini bottle of Dior Addict in your pocket shouldn’t end up at the back of your favorite skull dent. Peter, you watching his hands, or mine? Always such a dilemma,” Felicia said.

“Felicia, it’s a dangerous game you’re playing. But if you want me rewriting the finale mid-act, you’re in the right kind of spotlight. Just know—I don’t miss a move, especially when you’re center stage,” Peter said.

“Hold up! Before your sexual chemistry immolates the store like a rom-com meets Michael Bay production, feast your eyes on this!” Wade said.

Listen.

* * *

Time.

“I slide my finger down the sweating tumbler of bourbon in front of me, nails just tipping the ice. It's almost cute, how chaotic Wade’s margarita looks with all that salt dusted like confetti, and then there’s Peter — dependable, his drink as unassuming as he always tries to be. I tilt my head towards him, smirking,” Felicia said.

“Oh, come on, Felicia. It’s whiskey. Straight. No umbrella, no twist, no sugared rim. Are you seriously gonna critique this? You stick to your martinis; I’ll stick to what doesn’t scream “I have secrets to seduce out of you,” Peter said.

“Let's get this party properly over-intoxicated,” Wade said.

“When MJ texts late, hmm?" My smirk sharpens, turning up the heat without dropping the warmth. "That ‘friendly ex’ vibe or the one where you accidentally still look at wedding catalogs?" My eyes linger on him just long enough to remind him I catch everything,” Felicia said.

“Wedding catalogs? Really? That’s rich coming from someone whose idea of a commitment ceremony is picking out matching heist masks. Would you like 'cat ear optional' on the monogram, or are we skipping subtly altogether?” Peter asked.

“Oh, you better believe I’m stirring this pot. I’m the Emeril Lagasse of shit-talking. BAM!” Wade said.

“I glance at Wade—teeth bared, blunt and obvious as a car crash—then twist the edges of a smile, silkier now. "Careful, Wade," I murmur, leaning closer like I might just share a secret. "Peter’s guilt complex overwhelms his Spider-Sense sometimes. You don’t want to put him in a corner—not unless you’re ready to share the emotional fallout." I trace the line of condensation on my glass, casual, but my nails click harder when they hit the base,” Felicia said.

“A warning—great. I’ll keep that in mind the next time I’m falling off a building, mid-punch, or stopping a train with my face. Tell me, when’s the last time a Spider warning actually slowed you down, Felicia?” Peter asked.

Wade stirs his drink with a straw meant for theatrics, the plastic sword skewering a cherry. Felicia’s lips press into the rim of her glass, a slow, unreadable pull. Peter’s gaze is fixed, not at them, not at the drinks, but at the table edge, where a fingernail digs faint white crescents into the lacquer. They are talking logistics. The tension says otherwise.

“Okay, okay, hold the tequila shots—I’m pivoting so hard I just dislocated a metaphor,” Wade said.

“Wade does have a knack for finding the cracks, doesn’t he?" I let the words roll out like a purr, but there’s a sharper edge beneath it. "It’s almost poetic. Though, maybe not everyone’s in the mood to bare their souls between drinks. Speaking of…" I turn toward Wade, twirling the glass in my hand. "Know what really interests me tonight? You. What's it like, cartwheeling into every conversation like a grenade with glitter on the pin?” Felicia asked.

“Oh, introspection? Scary word there, sport. That’s like asking me to go skinny dipping in my own psyche—a pool filled with glass shards and rejected Pixar pitches. Pass,” Wade said.

“Oh, I’m sure Peter here would happily stay strapped in for the ride," I say, a little too sweet. My eyes flick to him, and the smirk settles like a cat stretching out in a sunbeam. "He’s already braced—tight as ever, aren’t you, love? But hey, if you’re going to sit there squeezing your halo, you might want to loosen the grip. Wouldn’t want the edges bleeding into that poor glass,” Felicia said.

“Fine, logistics. What’s the play here? You’re glamorous; I’m sticky. You do shadows; I do half-baked plans with duct tape and guilt. Are we tangoing or tap-dancing out of this one?” Peter asked.

Peter taps his glass against the table once, a sharp note under Wade’s rambling. Felicia doesn’t look, but the lift of her brow is precision-cut, a query sharpened in silence. Wade grins—too easy, too wide—not looking at either of them. Chaos is a current beneath their feet. The words ask alliances. The body language doesn’t trust them.

“Neutrality’s a myth at this table," I say, voice cutting through the static, low and precise. "Costumes, halos, grenades — pick your pressure point and someone’s already prodding.",” Felicia said.

“Mmm, that was mysterious. What’s next, leopard punches me or sulks? Spider-tears or web-gags? Either way, you two are adorable when you squirm,” Wade said.

“If we're doing this, then let's do it," I say, voice sharper now, the lacquer polished off. My body shifts forward, elbows off the table, like a predator uncoiling, but I’m still smiling. "You want to test costumes, secrets, and sinew? Fine. The logistics are simple: Wade stirs the pot, I bait the trap, and Peter tightens the knot by being too good for his own damn sake.",” Felicia said.

* * *

Time.

“You’re doing that femme fatale thing again. The hand? The wrist? That cigarette? You’re built like trouble, and I’ve got a PhD in electives I wasn’t supposed to take,” Wade said.

“I could teach you a thing or two, Wade—if you promise not to set the lesson on fire before I finish. So, what’s yours? Cigarette, cigar, or just plain chaos?” Felicia asked.

“She nailed it. Chaos is Wade's whole... thing. But it’s calibrated chaos—he’s not spinning out of control, he’s just at the wheel of a car with no brakes, and by some miracle, he keeps it on the road. Most days, anyway. The teasing’s her way of keeping him tethered, which is actually kind of sweet if you ignore the fact that he’d definitely have a comeback involving something about being unchainable or feral or, y’know, describing some inappropriate body part metaphor. And me? I'm clocking all of it but keeping my trap shut—because God knows, if I even poke at the edge of this back-and-forth, I'm absolutely getting dragged into a Wade comment about threesomes or web-based bondage or something else that’ll make me seriously question my choices for how I spend my nights. So, yeah. The vibe? Weighted blanket of chaotic idiocy with a side of mutual respect disguised as insults. Classic,” Peter said.

Silence.

“It's a hard city out there, doll. The kind where the hero doesn’t wear a cape, but Lycra and a bad case of heartburn. But enough about me,” Wade said.

“Unlikely is the word, darling, but even a cat needs a jester in her court. Thrones get so boring without the wild card,” Felicia said.

“I let him watch—for now. But you know, Peter, the observer always ends up being the one everyone’s watching in the end. How long before you jump into the dance?”

Peter glances at the cigarette first. Then Felicia.

“Come on, Peter, show us you've got the claws under that Boy Scout badge—unless you're just here to purr,” Wade said.

“Easy, boys—knives are fun, but let’s not spill any blood before the big game. Wade, keep it sharp but playful. Peter, save the righteous fury for the swinging. Both hands on deck, gents—we've got a score to settle,” Felicia said.

Felicia stubs the cigarette out on the rail, the hiss sharp against the thickening quiet.

* * *

Glossed mahogany veneer, riddled with scratches like constellations from a thousand careless diners. Empty plates, streaked with syrup smears and crumbs, clutter the space between third-round drinks: a whiskey neat, a rum and Diet Coke, and a bourbon sour sweating its rings into the wood. The booth is too large for two, the hollow seat across an echo of absence.

“Well, this is a mood. Empty glasses scattered like we’re all allergic to sobriety, plates shoved aside in negotiations of who’s actually gonna clean up—not me, by the way—and the kind of silence that either precedes a bad idea or follows one. Take your pick. What's the play here? Because I don’t sit in stasis very well,” Felicia said.

“The play? Oh, the play, my little diamond-encrusted cat burglar with a heart of...well, not gold, but definitely some precious metal. The play is simple: we don't play. We do. We move. We improv,” Wade said.

“Okay, you two are already here, no need to make an entrance and an impression. So what's the plan here—chaos first, tacos later, and a "Genesis" commitment something-something in the middle? Or do we actually have steps to follow that don't involve me playing triage on your brainstorm?” Peter asked.

The air cools. Peter’s voice, steady like a taut wire, cuts through the looseness of the third round. Conversations dissipate, leaving the faint clink of ice against a glass and the low hum of kitchen noise beyond the booth. Sobriety, never far, seeps through the cracks, pressing them against the weight of ‘Genesis’—a name too big, too final, for anything else to fit beside it.

“Oh, Peter. That tone. You make honesty sound like a confession. Fine, let’s rise to meet it. Just for you. I’ll even set my drink down—a monumental gesture, truly. You start, though. Tear the Band-Aid off, Spidey. Or are we talking masks first?” Felicia asked.

“Alright, here's the thing. "Genesis" isn't just another smash-and-grab with a fancier codename. Whatever move this is—taking on bigger players, going after people with satellites or armies—it means consequences. We're not just stealing to survive. We're making a statement, whether we admit it or not. And statements? They paint targets. Do you want to carry those targets, or just play pretend until the sharp end comes home?” Peter asked.

“Oh, Genesis, huh? Sounds biblical. Heavy. Like we’re not just talking smashin’ and grabbin’ here—more like apocalypse timeshares and moral debt I’m probably delinquent on. Big damn hero territory. That it, Pete?” Wade asked.

Leaning back, shadows stretch across their faces, the drinks untouched now—a quiet truce in the war between intention and hesitation. Peter’s hand brushes the table’s edge, still. The others sit within their silences, the weight of Genesis between them like a storm winding just beyond the horizon. And so, for now, the booth holds them.

“You dragged this into real stakes, Parker. I don’t get to ghost on you now. If this ‘bigger than a heist’ shit matters, you need me, you fucking say it. You don’t dangle that in the air and pretend I’m expendable,” Wade said.

“Wade, it's not about playing gatekeeper or deciding whose war this is. It's about asking if you're ready—not to fight, but to carry the kind of fallout this brings. Because genesis doesn't end at the fight. It's the mess after—the people who get hurt, the bridges that burn, the pieces we might not get to pick up. Are you in for that, or just the part with the explosions?” Peter asked.

The glint of the overhead pendant lights catches in Peter’s untouched whiskey, a liquid stillness that reflects nothing but depth. Across the booth, shoulders tighten, jaws flex, and eyes avert—each instinctively guarding the soft underbelly Peter’s words exposed. The air hangs sharp, heavy with the unspoken: the knowledge that the fallout isn’t theoretical. It’s already bleeding in at the edges.

* * *

Time.

“Does it translate as, "I make bad choices, but I make them while looking fabulous"? Because if so, I want it on a T-shirt,” Wade said.

“Well, darling, if you're going to make bad choices, you might as well ensure they leave an impression. Mediocrity is vulgar, and I don't do vulgar,” Felicia said.

“Hell of a skill—you don’t even need flair to do that. But, hey, you add flair to everything. Makes me wonder—got a second for a guy still trying to figure out the art of it?” Peter asked.

The olives turned lazily in Felicia's glass, her stir slow, deliberate.

“So, Felicia, what’s your call—poke the all-seeing space lurker, or keep sipping while the vibe tangles itself into an even bigger knot?” Wade asked.

“Red's never really been my color, Wade. But you're right—I don't reach out to bask in the glow of your 'method to the madness' charm. Something tells me you've got a hand in a pot that spills a little more than just tacos. Care to let me peek under the lid?” Felicia asked.

“That’s rich coming from the guy who thinks subtlety is just a French word for “yell louder.” But hey, Felicia, are we pretending Wade's curiosity is the dangerous kind now? Or just the usual dinner-turned-molotov situation?” Peter asked.

The booth’s upholstery groaned under shifting weight, its give creaking like old grudges.

“Alright, folks, offering made—who wants to reach first? Uatu might not drink, but I’m betting even watchers have their poison. Martini's looking awfully omniscient,” Wade said.

“Shiny, gooey, or scandalous—sounds like my kind of trifecta. I'm in, Wade. But if this turns out to be another one of your tequila-induced capers, you’re buying the first round after we pull it off—and I don’t drink cheap,” Felicia said.

“Oh, I mean, Wade’s deflection game is basically an Olympic sport by now. "Oh look, shiny thing! But also let’s talk about exploding hamster wheels instead of feelings!" Classic. But—hang on—are we actually letting him keep the gold medal, Felicia? I mean, he's all half-baked zingers and no real substance...right?” Peter asked.

Light pooled in the leftover sheen of spilled whiskey, scattered sharp like inconvenient truths.

“Come on, ‘Licia, aim for a bullseye. Or are we letting the tension marinate into a stew no one ordered?” Wade asked.

“Oh, Wade, you had me at double-cross. Hydra's always a treat to outmaneuver, and Fisk's goons? Well, they never did know how to keep their hands clean—or attached. Tell me, love, who's baking this chaos pie, and where do we slice in first?” Felicia asked.

“Okay, so, you’re saying you can poke sharp enough to hit nerves but not puncture anything vital. Got it. Just remind me where I’m standing in that little equation, Felicia—sacrificial armor or just the idiot holding up the target?” Peter asked.

Felicia shifted first, a slow tilt of her head, silver hair spilling like unraveling intentions.

“What’s it gonna be, Kitty Cat? Spill the plot or make me guess? Either way, I'm dying beautifully over here,” Wade said.

“Wakandan vibranium, high society masks, and Kingpin’s network squirming underneath it all? Oh, Wade, you do know how to charm a girl. I’ll bring the better mask—and the sharper claws. What’s your entrance plan, or do I need to dress you for the occasion?” Felicia asked.

“Right, so...this is that moment where I either thread the needle or dive straight into disaster, right? Let me guess—which one gets the bigger applause? Or is it just me standing here juggling knives, waiting for the other sharp edge to fall while you keep grinning like you know something I don’t?” Peter asked.

The ice in Wade’s glass shifted, a faint chime swallowed by the hum of the room.

* * *

As for me, these are my stories.

I observe all that transpires here. But I do not, cannot, will not
interfere.

I have watched. I will continue to watch.

For I am the Watcher.
