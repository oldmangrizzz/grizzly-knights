CLINICAL SOURCES — Logan / Wolverine (retrieved from PubMed; cite in clinical_provenance).
Grounds: dissociative amnesia and identity alteration as responses to chronic/repeated trauma
(Weapon X, the memory loss, the berserker dissociative state) — a dissociation spectrum, not just "rage."

1. Dissociation as the ultimate response to chronic developmental/traumatic stress; dissociative amnesia,
   depersonalization, derealization, identity confusion and identity alteration form a single severity
   spectrum; highest reported childhood-trauma frequency among psychiatric conditions.
   Sar (2014), The many faces of dissociation: opportunities for innovative research in psychiatry,
   Clin Psychopharmacol Neurosci 12(3):171-179. DOI: 10.9758/cpn.2014.12.3.171 ; PMID: 25598819

2. Transdiagnostic clinical assessment of dissociation; acute dissociative reactions and chronic secondary
   syndromes over a core of amnesia/identity alteration.
   Sar (2022), Dissociation Across Cultures, Alpha Psychiatry 23(3):95-103.
   DOI: 10.5152/alphapsychiatry.2022.21556 ; PMID: 36425778
