CLINICAL SOURCES — Victor von Doom (retrieved from PubMed; cite in clinical_provenance).
Grounds the structure: grandiose narcissistic armor as a DEFENSE over shame and a Complex-PTSD core
(the grandiosity is the defense against the C-PTSD, not its absence).

1. Grandiose narcissism co-occurs with vulnerability and correlates negatively with self-esteem and
   positively with shame — the grandiose surface is a defense over a vulnerable, shame-driven interior.
   Pincus et al. (2009), Initial construction and validation of the Pathological Narcissism Inventory,
   Psychol Assess 21(3):365-379. DOI: 10.1037/a0016530 ; PMID: 19719348

2. Grandiose and vulnerable narcissism interrelate via self-esteem and emotion dysregulation; improvement
   is gradual and slow (the maintenance is sticky).
   Weinberg & Ronningstam (2022), Narcissistic Personality Disorder: Progress in Understanding and
   Treatment, Focus (Am Psychiatr Publ) 20(4):368-377. DOI: 10.1176/appi.focus.20220052 ; PMID: 37200887

3. Complex PTSD via affect dysregulation, psychoform dissociation, and identity alteration — distinct from
   borderline personality disorder. Grounds the C-PTSD core beneath the narcissistic armor.
   van Dijke, Hopman & Ford (2018), Eur J Psychotraumatol 9(1):1400878.
   DOI: 10.1080/20008198.2017.1400878 ; PMID: 29410773
