CLINICAL SOURCES — Magneto / Erik Lyncher (retrieved from PubMed; cite in clinical_provenance).
Grounds the genocide-survivor / collective-trauma core and the "only power guarantees survival" doctrine.

1. "Survival mode": intergenerational transmission of genocide trauma — a constellation of fear, mistrust,
   social hostility, decreased self-worth, hypervigilance, and trauma-based coping passed to later
   generations. Directly grounds Magneto's doctrine that the world is permanently dangerous to his people.
   Bezo & Maggi (2015), Living in "survival mode": Intergenerational transmission of trauma from the
   Holodomor genocide, Social Science & Medicine 134:87-94.
   DOI: 10.1016/j.socscimed.2015.04.009 ; PMID: 25931287

2. Intergenerational trauma from collective trauma (war, genocide, systemic oppression): biological
   embedding of stress, identity shaped toward mistrust and emotional restraint, elevated distress;
   parental PTSD a key predictor. Grounds the inheritance and the racial-terror modernization.
   El-Khalil, Tudor & Nedelcea (2025), Impact of intergenerational trauma on second-generation
   descendants: a systematic review, BMC Psychology 13(1):668.
   DOI: 10.1186/s40359-025-03012-4 ; PMID: 40597246

3. Cumulative childhood trauma under persecution (hidden Jewish children, WWII): specific traumas,
   intra/intergenerational disturbance, affiliation disturbance, and coping/protective factors.
   Feldman, Taieb & Moro (2010), Jewish children hidden in France 1940-1944, American Journal of
   Orthopsychiatry 80(4):547-556.
   DOI: 10.1111/j.1939-0025.2010.01059.x ; PMID: 20950295
