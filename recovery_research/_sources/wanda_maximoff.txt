CLINICAL SOURCES — Wanda Maximoff / Scarlet Witch (retrieved from PubMed; cite in clinical_provenance).
Grounds: prolonged/complicated grief and the denial-reality-rejection response to catastrophic loss
(the lost children; "No More Mutants"; the constructed reality) — grief weaponized by power.

1. Prolonged Grief Disorder (ICD-11): normal grief symptoms that remain too intense for too long;
   grief-related cognition and depression as core features; an empirically defined, treatable disorder.
   Bryant et al. (2024), CBT vs Mindfulness in Treatment of Prolonged Grief Disorder: An RCT,
   JAMA Psychiatry 81(7):646-654. DOI: 10.1001/jamapsychiatry.2024.0432 ; PMID: 38656428

2. Grief aetiology/symptoms: shock, DISBELIEF or DENIAL, high anxiety, anger; complicated grief as a
   superimposed process that alters and worsens the course; trauma intensified by violent/sudden loss.
   Grounds the denial-of-reality response.
   Pop-Jordanova (2021), Grief: Aetiology, Symptoms and Management, Pril (Makedon Akad Nauk) 42(2):9-18.
   DOI: 10.2478/prilozi-2021-0014 ; PMID: 34699700
