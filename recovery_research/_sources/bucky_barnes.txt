CLINICAL SOURCES — Bucky Barnes (retrieved from PubMed by the guide; cite in clinical_provenance).

1. Moral injury: exposure to acts that violate one's moral values produces severe distress, shame,
   social isolation, and self-injurious sequelae — even when the acts were not freely chosen.
   Griffin et al. (2019), "Moral Injury: An Integrative Review," Journal of Traumatic Stress 32(3):350-362.
   DOI: 10.1002/jts.22362 ; PMID: 30688367

2. Childhood/relational trauma produces complex PTSD via affect dysregulation, psychoform dissociation,
   and identity alteration — statistically distinct from borderline personality disorder.
   van Dijke, Hopman & Ford (2018), European Journal of Psychotraumatology 9(1):1400878.
   DOI: 10.1080/20008198.2017.1400878 ; PMID: 29410773
