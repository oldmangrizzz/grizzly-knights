CLINICAL SOURCES — Reed Richards (retrieved from PubMed; cite in clinical_provenance).
Grounds the core refutation: ASD (systemizing) is NOT alexithymia. He feels; the deficit is in the
output/interoceptive channel and co-occurring alexithymia where present — not an absence of feeling.

1. Empathy differences in autism track CO-OCCURRING ALEXITHYMIA, not autism itself: ASD adults WITHOUT
   alexithymia showed intact cognitive and affective empathy; alexithymia mediated the empathy-interoception
   relationship (an "alexithymic subgroup"). Grounds: autism != emotionally absent.
   Mul, Stagg, Herbelin & Aspell (2018), The Feeling of Me Feeling for You: Interoception, Alexithymia and
   Empathy in Autism, Journal of Autism and Developmental Disorders 48(9):2953-2967.
   DOI: 10.1007/s10803-018-3564-3 ; PMID: 29644587

2. Even where alexithymia co-occurs in ASD, emotional empathy "should NOT be characterized as an absence
   of a capacity for emotional empathy" — it is experienced differently, often with greater personal
   distress, tied to reduced interoceptive (bodily) signal. The deficit is the channel, not the feeling.
   Butera et al. (2022), Relationships between alexithymia, interoception, and emotional empathy in autism
   spectrum disorder, Autism 27(3):690-703.
   DOI: 10.1177/13623613221111310 ; PMID: 35833505
