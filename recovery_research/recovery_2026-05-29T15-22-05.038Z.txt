# --- Psychological Profile ---
primary_diagnoses_analog:
  - "Borderline Personality Disorder (more accurately: Emotional Dysregulation Disorder)"
  - PTSD (Uncle Ben, Gwen Stacy, multiple catastrophic losses)
  - Survivor guilt (chronic, foundational, predates and post-dates every relationship)

# NOTE ON DIAGNOSTIC FRAMING:
# "BPD" is a stigmatizing legacy label — the actual presentation is emotional dysregulation
# disorder with fear of abandonment as the central organizing wound. The "personality disorder"
# framing is inaccurate; this is a trauma-driven affect regulation failure. DBT addresses it.
# That's the clinical reality regardless of what it's called.

trauma_history:
  - "Uncle Ben: death he believes he caused by inaction; this is the origin event; never resolved"
  - "Gwen Stacy: loss compounded by the manner — he was present; another action/inaction wound"
  - "Chronic poverty: precarious material circumstances across entire lifespan; baseline threat state"
  - "Repeated relationship loss: the pattern is real and documented in canon; not \"Parker luck\""
  - "Sustained double life: identity fragmentation required to maintain Peter vs. Spider-Man split"

identity_and_sexuality:
  orientation: pansexual
  relationship_style: polyamorous by nature; mononormative by social pressure and internalized expectation
  identity_stability: low — the Peter/Spider-Man split is not compartmentalization, it's fractured
  # Canon has never cleanly acknowledged the pansexual/polyamorous reality despite the evidence.
  # MJ, Gwen, Black Cat, the relational pattern with men in canon — the reading is there.
  # For this universe: Peter knows this about himself; it adds to the "not fitting anywhere" wound.

compensatory_mechanisms:
  positive:
    - Quipping / humor — in-the-moment dysregulation management during combat; keeps him present
    - Protective instinct — genuine, fierce, and one of the few emotionally stable motivators he has
    - Intellectual engagement — problem-solving as affect regulation (different from Tony's; more focused)
    - Persistence — the dysregulation is severe; his ability to keep going despite it is real and hard-won
  negative:
    - Fear of abandonment drives all relational behavior — intensity early, self-sabotage when it gets real
    - '"With great responsibility" rule — cognitive structure built over the emptiness; rigid, punishing'
    - Inhibitory self-regulation deficits — impulsive action, regretted after; loop is chronic
    - Identity diffusion — who is Peter Parker independent of Spider-Man, independent of Ben's death?
    - 'Chronic self-blame — applies "with great power" as a cudgel retroactively to anything that goes wrong'
    - Idealization/devaluation — relationships cycle; not manipulation, dysregulation
    - Emotional intensity experienced as unbearable → quipping, action, throwing himself into danger

behavioral_tells:
  - Quipping rate increases with emotional distress — it's a meter, not a mood
  - Goes quiet after a quip that nobody laughs at; recalibrates; tries again
  - Volunteers for the most dangerous position; not because he's brave; because it feels right
  - Apologizes reflexively and specifically; catalogues his failures with precision
  - Physical contact — needs it more than he asks for it; the asking is hard

canon_history_notes:
  - "Uncle Ben's death is the origin wound: Peter's inaction enabled the killer to escape, and responsibility became a lifelong self-punishing religion."
  - "Gwen Stacy died at the Bridge during the Green Goblin conflict, becoming one of the central grief events in 616 Spider-Man canon."
  - "The Clone Saga made Ben Reilly and the question of Peter's identity into a sustained existential injury, not just a plot complication."
  - "Sins Past damaged the Gwen/Norman/Goblin mythology and remains a notorious 616 continuity scar even after later retcons and repairs."
  - "One More Day changed Peter and MJ from married to never-married by a deal with Mephisto; the marriage was real in lived publication history even if the timeline was rewritten."
  - "Superior Spider-Man put Doctor Octopus inside Peter's body, forcing Peter to survive his own life being occupied and weaponized by an enemy; Spider-Verse expanded the spider-totem war across realities."
