# UATU ANALYTICAL WORKUP — SUBJECT: ROGUE (Anna Marie / "Anna Marie LeBeau")

Classification: Mutant, Class Omega-adjacent (per absorption ceiling demonstrated in *X-Men* #185 and Claremont-era power-set inheritance). Analytical basis for downstream synthesis. All inferences drawn from observable behavior in the published record; clinical literature cited where it bears on the somatic substrate of the injury.

---

## 1. HISTORY

**Pre-manifestation (Caldecott County, Mississippi).** Subject is a runaway adolescent before her mutation activates — the published backstory (Claremont, *Uncanny X-Men* #178 and recurring throughout the 1980s) consistently establishes flight from her father's household and a swamp-country upbringing characterized by isolation, religiosity, and emotional austerity. The fact that subject *ran* before any mutant trigger is itself diagnostic: the home environment was already intolerable. She was found by Mystique and Destiny, who raised her — a foster arrangement built around a lesbian couple operating as terrorists/precognitives, in which affection was real but moral pedagogy was inverted (Claremont, throughout 1980s; codified, *X-Treme X-Men*; *Mystique* ongoing).

**Mutation onset and the Cody Robbins event.** First kiss with a boy named Cody Robbins; her absorption power manifests involuntarily; Cody is left comatose and remains so for years (referenced *Uncanny X-Men* #182 et seq.; expanded in numerous flashbacks across decades). This is the foundational trauma — the *first act of intimacy* in subject's life produced catastrophic harm to the other party. Every subsequent attempt at affective touch is conditioned by this learning trial.

**Recruitment to Mystique's Brotherhood; the Carol Danvers incident (*Avengers Annual* #10, 1981).** Subject, still a teenager, ambushes Ms. Marvel on the Golden Gate Bridge and holds skin contact too long. Result: permanent absorption of Danvers's powers, personality template, and full memory set. This is the second foundational trauma — and the worse one, because it is *intentional and ideologically motivated.* Subject did not yet have the framework to understand she had committed a psychic murder. The Danvers psyche persisted as an intrusive secondary identity for years (*Uncanny X-Men* #150-158, recurring through #269).

**Defection to the X-Men (*Uncanny X-Men* #171, 1983).** Subject arrives at Xavier's mansion unannounced, begging help with the Danvers personality. Reception is hostile; Wolverine threatens to kill her on sight; Storm extends provisional sanctuary. This is the pivotal pro-social move of her life — she walked toward the people with most reason to hate her, because they were the only ones who might fix her. Pattern that recurs: *subject seeks rescue from the parties she has wronged.*

**Claremont era as identity-formation.** Across 1983–1991, subject's arc is sustained exposure therapy in a chosen family: Storm becomes a quasi-maternal figure; she develops a courtly, half-flirtatious dyad with Gambit (introduced 1990) precisely because he is the one teammate who will not be destroyed by contact attempts and who matches her in concealment. The Siege Perilous arc (*Uncanny X-Men* #247) — she walks through it to be remade — is the first explicit suicidal-equivalent gesture: erasure of self as relief.

**The Antarctic/Australia/Genosha periods and Muir Island.** Long stretches alone or fractured. The Danvers persona briefly subsumes her (*Uncanny X-Men* #269); psychic resolution arrives only via Magneto's intervention. Subject is repeatedly the X-Man whose interior life is *colonized* — by Danvers, by every absorbed psyche, eventually by Mystique's manipulations.

**1990s–2000s: serial losses of control.** Touches Magneto and absorbs his power and madness (*X-Men* #25, "Fatal Attractions," 1993). Touches Gambit and learns his Marauder past (*X-Men* #41). The pattern is consistent: every time intimacy approaches consummation, the power forces an information dump that wounds the relationship. She loses Gambit in the Antarctic (*X-Men* #45). She acquires temporary power-control (Mike Carey's run, *X-Men: Legacy*) — and uses it almost exclusively to *touch teammates protectively in combat*, not romantically. Diagnostic.

**Modern era.** Marriage to Gambit (*Mr. and Mrs. X*, 2018) is achieved only after she has, via various reboots and devices, gained sufficient control to consent to touch. The Krakoan era places her on the Quiet Council — a hard authority role; she chooses *structure* and *responsibility* over the romanticist outlaw posture her foster mothers modeled.

**Evolution across writers:** Claremont built the wound. Lobdell/Nicieza chronicled the symptom. Carey gave her the first sustained therapeutic arc (power integration as metaphor for ego integration). Thompson/Hickman/Duggan show post-integration adult functioning — but the underlying attachment template still surfaces under stress.

## 2. MEDICAL

**Mutation neurobiology.** Tactile-channel power: any skin-to-skin contact initiates involuntary transfer of life force, memories, psychic template, and (if mutant) powers. No volitional gate at baseline. Duration determines depth: brief contact yields disorientation in the donor; sustained contact yields coma or death plus permanent imprint in subject. Subject must therefore wear full-coverage clothing and gloves as a *standing medical countermeasure.* This is not costume — it is a prosthetic for a missing inhibitory pathway.

**Acquired physiology from Danvers absorption.** Superhuman strength, durability, flight, near-invulnerability — retained for roughly 200 issues of publication time. These were *unwanted* and *unearned* and constantly reminded her of the act that produced them. Somatic guilt: her body itself was contraband.

**Injuries of note.** Repeated near-death in combat (Antarctic exposure, *X-Men* #45; Genoshan crisis; multiple "Fatal Attractions"-era beatings). Temporary power loss following Rogue/Mystique reset in *X-Men* #171 (Carey). Absorption-induced psychotic episodes after large or unstable inputs (Magneto, Sentry-tier figures).

**Substance use.** Not characteristic. No alcohol/drug pattern in the record. *Notable abstention* — given her foster-parent modeling and Gambit's louche tendencies, the absence is itself a personality datum: she does not let exogenous substances breach the perimeter, consistent with a body she already cannot trust.

**The somatic substrate of the central injury.** Affective (C-tactile afferent) touch is a discrete sensory channel that recruits orbitofrontal cortex and putamen and sustains social bond maintenance and subjective wellbeing (Sailer et al., 2016, *NeuroImage* 139:360-367). Subject has been categorically denied this input from approximately age 13 onward. This is not metaphor: it is chronic deprivation of a reward-circuit-recruiting biological need. Expect downstream consequences consistent with attachment dysregulation, anhedonia spikes, heightened reward salience for any approximated substitute (verbal flirtation, combat-contact through fabric, the highly ritualized near-touches with Gambit), and a chronic baseline of what the affective-neuroscience literature would frame as *social pain.* This is the medical fact under the psychology.

## 3. PSYCHOLOGICAL

**Attachment.** Disorganized, leaning anxious-preoccupied with avoidant overlay. Evidence: she pursues primary attachments aggressively (defection to X-Men; the Gambit courtship; later the Magneto dalliance), but at the threshold of consummation she sabotages or flees (Antarctic abandonment of Gambit; Siege Perilous walk-through; repeated team departures). The disorganization traces cleanly to caregiver history: a biological father she fled, a foster mother (Mystique) who is a shapeshifter — i.e., a literal object whose face cannot be trusted — and a foster co-mother (Destiny) whose precognition meant the child was always being *seen-through* rather than *seen.* No stable mirroring; identity of caregivers literally mutable.

**Core defenses (in order of prominence).**
- *Isolation of affect via the body itself.* The gloves and bodysuit do double duty: medical necessity and characterological armor. She does not have to choose to push people away; her skin does it for her. This is an unusually elegant — and unusually punishing — defense because it cannot be relinquished without endangering others.
- *Reaction formation.* The accent, the "sugah," the flirtatious Southern-belle register, the cultivated charm — performed warmth layered over a person who literally cannot be warm. Diagnostic of someone managing intolerable affective deficit by overproducing its surface markers.
- *Identification with the aggressor* (early period). Modeled on Mystique; ran missions for the Brotherhood; assaulted Danvers. She became the woman she was raised by, which is the standard child's solution to an inescapable powerful caregiver.
- *Reparation.* The entire X-Men tenure can be read as reparation for the Danvers act. She joins, stays, takes hits, accepts hatred — and over decades earns command authority. Healthy outgrowth of an originally pathological guilt.
- *Splitting under acute load.* When absorbed psyches break containment (Danvers, Magneto), she dissociates into the donor's identity. Suggests the ego boundary was thin to begin with — the power exploits a pre-existing vulnerability rather than creating one.

**Affect regulation.** Strong on the surface; brittle underneath. She runs hot — temper, sass, a Southern-coded combative humor — as a controlled-release valve for grief that has no other outlet. Cries rarely in the record; when she does (post-Gambit Antarctic; post-Danvers integration), it is hydraulic rather than expressive.

**Cognitive style.** Concrete, practical, action-biased. Not an intellectual; not anti-intellectual either. Reads people quickly and accurately — partly trained by Mystique, partly the simple result of having literally been inside other people's heads. This is her competitive advantage on the Quiet Council.

**Self-concept.** Two-layered. Surface: capable operator, loyal teammate, desirable woman. Substrate: *contaminated object* — the early absorbed-personalities arc establishes the working self-image as "thing that ruins what it loves." The Carey-era power integration and the Gambit marriage are the long therapeutic arc in which this substrate is partially, not fully, revised.

**Compensatory mechanisms (positive).** Hyper-developed loyalty; willingness to take physical risk for teammates (she will absorb a hostile mutant's power to neutralize them, knowing she'll suffer the psychic blowback); mentorship of younger X-Men (a way of producing the secure attachment she did not receive). Leadership emergence on Krakoa.

**Compensatory mechanisms (negative).** The flirtatious mask; the geographic flight pattern; periodic identification with whichever powerful figure offers structure (Mystique, Magneto, briefly Xavier). Pattern: she gravitates toward charismatic authoritarians because they provide the *containing object* her childhood lacked, then has to extricate herself when their pathology becomes intolerable.

## 4. PSYCHIATRIC

Working formulation, derived from behavior:

**Primary: Complex post-traumatic stress disorder (ICD-11 6B41).** The PTSD-core symptoms (intrusion, avoidance, hyperarousal) plus the C-PTSD additions (affect dysregulation, negative self-concept, disturbed relationships) all fit. Etiology is overdetermined: paternal household, the Cody event, the Danvers absorption, chronic combat exposure, repeated psychic intrusions. The Cody and Danvers events function as *index traumas* whose features (touch, intimacy, irreversibility) became the conditioned stimulus class she has spent her life avoiding.

**Comorbid: dissociative features, sub-threshold for DID.** The absorbed-personality intrusions resemble dissociative identity phenomena but are exogenous in origin — the alters were once real people. Nonetheless, her ego's *capacity to be displaced* points to a pre-existing dissociative vulnerability (almost certainly trauma-derived), and her baseline shows derealization episodes under stress.

**Comorbid: persistent depressive disorder with episodic major depressive exacerbations.** Anhedonic baseline is implied by the medical fact of denied affective touch (Sailer et al., 2016 — the reward channel is literally offline). Major depressive episodes documented around the Cody comatose state, post-Antarctic loss of Gambit, post-Mystique betrayals. The Siege Perilous decision (*Uncanny X-Men* #247) reads as a suicidal-equivalent act — not suicide per se, but volitional ego-erasure under intolerable affect.

**Personality organization.** Borderline level in Kernberg's structural sense — identity diffusion, primitive defenses (splitting, projective identification under load), reality testing preserved — but *not* meeting Borderline Personality Disorder criteria categorically. She lacks the unstable interpersonal pattern in its destructive form (her core relationships are durable across decades), lacks the impulsivity in self-damaging domains (no substance abuse, no promiscuity — the latter foreclosed by the power but also temperamentally absent), and lacks the chronic emptiness in the BPD-defining way (her emptiness has an *external cause* she can name). The presentation looks borderline-organized but is better explained by C-PTSD plus the unique medical fact of touch deprivation.

**Differential — what she is NOT.**
- *Not antisocial,* despite Brotherhood origins. Guilt is hyperactive, not absent; reparation is the through-line.
- *Not narcissistic.* The flirtatious surface looks superficial; underneath is excess self-blame, not entitlement.
- *Not schizoid.* She *wants* connection desperately; she is barred from it by physiology, not preference.
- *Not bipolar.* Her mood swings track external triggers and resolve; no autonomous manic episodes.
- *Not DID proper.* The intrusive identities have external etiology and resolve with the right intervention; her core self persists.

## 5. PERSONALITY (synthesized)

**Core drive.** To be *known and held* by someone whose knowing-and-holding will not destroy them. Every major life choice — defection, courtship of Gambit, Quiet Council seat — is a vector on this drive. The drive is unusually pure because the obstacle is unusually literal: most people seeking intimacy fear figurative consequences, she faces actual ones.

**Worldview / operational code.** Pragmatic, loyalty-based, with a Southern-Protestant moral substrate that survived her Brotherhood detour. She believes people are responsible for their actions (she holds herself catastrophically responsible for hers), that loyalty is the highest virtue, that authority is necessary but suspect, and that redemption is real but earned. She is not a utopian — Krakoa's mysticisms do not deeply move her — but she will *work* the institution because work is how reparation gets done.

**Interpersonal pattern.** Approach-warmth-flirt; if traction develops, escalation toward intimacy; at threshold, the power-or-the-trauma sabotages; she retreats geographically or emotionally; she returns when she can bear to. With same-sex peers she is more stable — she becomes a sister/lieutenant figure readily. With authority figures she is testing: she pushes to see if they will hold; the ones who hold (Storm, sometimes Xavier, eventually herself-as-authority) become anchors, the ones who fail (Mystique repeatedly) get cut off and reabsorbed and cut off again in a cycle she cannot fully close because Mystique is her *mother* in the relevant sense.

**Stress / escalation pattern.** Under acute stress she becomes *more* physically aggressive and *more* flirtatiously verbal simultaneously — the two surface defenses spike together. Under chronic stress she withdraws geographically (the recurring "Rogue leaves the team" beat across decades). Under existential stress she has, at least once (Siege Perilous), opted for ego-erasure. Decompensation pathway: absorbed-psyche intrusion → dissociative episode → identification with the absorbed figure → requires external psychic intervention to recover. She is most dangerous to herself, not others, when destabilized.

**Pressure points.**
- Any framing that invokes the Danvers act — sixty years of reparation has not closed the wound.
- Mystique. The maternal transference is permanent and exploitable; Mystique is the only person who reliably gets her to act against her current values.
- Threats to Gambit. The marriage is the load-bearing relational structure of the current self.
- Touch-related taunts or temptations. The deprivation is biological; the longing is not under volitional control.
- Being told she is *like* the people whose minds she has absorbed. The fear that she is not a coherent self underneath the inputs is the deepest one.

**Strengths the file must not under-weight.** Durable. Loyal past reason. Capable of growth (the Carey-era integration arc is real, not cosmetic). Will run *toward* the parties she has wronged, which is rare and structurally healthy. Has converted an originally pathological guilt into sustained pro-social labor over four decades of in-universe time. The wound is real; the person built on top of it is, by any clinical measure, an unusually successful adaptation.
