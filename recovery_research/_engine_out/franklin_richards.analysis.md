# UATU ANALYTICAL FILE: FRANKLIN BENJAMIN RICHARDS

*Pre-synthesis dossier. Behavioral evidence base only. Conclusions derived, not given.*

---

## 1. HISTORY — THE LONGITUDINAL RECORD

Franklin Benjamin Richards is the firstborn of Reed Richards and Susan Storm, conceived and gestated under circumstances no other child in the Marvel record shares: both parents bear cosmic-ray-altered genomes (FF #1, 1961, retroactively relevant). His birth is a medical crisis — Sue's pregnancy nearly kills her because the radiation-saturated fetus generates uncontrollable energy in utero. Reed, Johnny, and Ben must retrieve the Cosmic Control Rod from Annihilus in the Negative Zone (*Fantastic Four Annual* #6, 1968) so Reed can stabilize the delivery. **Franklin's existence begins as a near-fatality for his mother and a desperate scientific intervention by his father.** This is the foundational fact of his life, and it is repeated structurally across decades.

Early childhood (1968–~1980, Lee/Thomas/Wolfman/Wein era): Franklin is constantly endangered, constantly imperiled, and constantly being put down — placed in stasis, mind-shut, or sent away — by his own father. Reed psychically lobotomizes him (*FF* #141, Gerry Conway) to prevent his uncontrolled powers from destroying reality after Annihilus weaponizes him. Sue leaves Reed over this. The lesson coded into Franklin during his most formative years: **my power is dangerous, my father will shut me off to manage it, my mother will leave because of what I am.**

Adolescence is rendered non-linear and traumatic. Franklin is repeatedly aged-up and reverted: the Onslaught arc (1996) sees him pocket the entire Marvel Universe inside a created reality to save it — an act of cosmic engineering performed by a small child under terror conditions, after watching his father, mother, and the Avengers apparently die. He brings them back. **At ~10 years old he has already performed a creation-of-universes-grade reality save and is the sole survivor's witness to his family's death and resurrection.** He carries this without therapy or framework.

Counter-X / Heroes Reborn aftermath (late '90s–2000s): the alternate-future "Hyperstorm" thread (his own grandchild, a god-tier menace) and recurring revelations that adult/future Franklins are reality-tier threats. He is told, repeatedly and by trusted figures, that his future self is dangerous. He internalizes this.

Hickman era (2009–2015, the definitive Franklin material): Franklin's powers "burn out" — actually suppressed by his future self to give him a childhood. He meets the adult Franklin who has consumed galaxies and outlived his family. The Council of Reeds judges him; Valeria, his younger sister, becomes his intellectual peer and protector. The *Time Runs Out* / *Secret Wars* (2015) arc: Franklin and Sue and the family survive the multiversal collapse inside a "life raft," then Franklin and Valeria spend years rebuilding new universes alongside Reed and Sue, post-*Secret Wars*. **He spends his late childhood/early adolescence literally building cosmoses with his father — finally collaborator, no longer threat-to-be-managed.**

Return (post-2018 Slott run): Franklin's powers begin to genuinely fade or "expire," and he learns Reed may have lied or shaded the truth about why. The Griever arc, the revelation in *FF* (2018) #5 that his mutant-tier power output was finite, the later *X-Men/FF* (2020) crossover where Krakoa claims him and Reed disputes his mutant status with documentation suggesting Franklin was never genetically a mutant in the classical sense — these collectively re-traumatize him. **The defining adult conflict: was my father honest with me about what I am? The answer trends toward "no, or not fully."**

Across all eras the pattern is invariant: Franklin is the most powerful being in any room he enters and the least empowered person in his own family dynamic. He is solved-for, not consulted.

## 2. MEDICAL

**Genome:** Mutant designation contested. Cosmic-ray-altered parental DNA produced a child whose abilities resemble reality-warping mutation (Omega-level by X-gene metrics, per Krakoan classification) but whose substrate may be something else entirely — Reed's documentation (*X-Men/FF* 2020) argues against true mutant status. Medically: **his own etiology is disputed by his own father.** Significant identity-medical injury.

**Gestational trauma:** In utero radiation crisis; mother nearly died carrying him. Delivery required Negative Zone tech. Documented somatic origin of "I almost killed my mother by existing."

**Neurological history:**
- Psychic shutdown by Reed (FF #141) — equivalent to medically induced coma of the higher cognitive/power centers. Performed without consent (age ~4). Functional analog to a parent-administered lobotomy.
- Multiple subsequent power suppressions, rewrites, and aging accelerations/reversions. His developmental clock has been overwritten more than once.
- Power "burnout" (Hickman era) — later revealed as imposed by his future self, not natural. Another non-consensual neurological intervention, this time auto-inflicted across time.

**Physical injuries:** Relatively few of consequence given his abilities; he tends to be the source of incidents, not their casualty. Kidnapping is chronic across his childhood (Annihilus, Mephisto, the Cult of the Cosmic Cube, Onslaught, etc.). Cumulative captivity-and-rescue cycle measurable in dozens of events before age 12.

**Substance use:** None recorded. Age-appropriate.

**Somatic-psychological impact:** A body whose every cell has been read, scanned, suppressed, accelerated, time-displaced, or re-coded by the people who love him. Bodily autonomy is a concept Franklin has never experientially possessed. Predicts hypervigilance about being "fixed," diagnostic resistance, ambivalence toward medical/scientific authority figures = his own father.

## 3. PSYCHOLOGICAL

**Attachment profile:** Disorganized-leaning secure. Both parents physically present and demonstrably loving; both parents have repeatedly made unilateral decisions about his mind, body, and powers without his consent. This produces the classic disorganized template — *the caregiver is simultaneously the source of safety and the source of threat* — but it is buffered by the genuine, demonstrable warmth of Sue and the deep loyalty of Ben Grimm (his godfather/safe-base attachment object). Predicted output: high baseline relational trust, but specific, surgical mistrust toward Reed in domains touching his powers, his body, his diagnosis.

**Defenses (in order of observed frequency):**
- *Intellectualization* — modeled directly off Reed. Franklin frames cosmic trauma in conceptual/technical terms.
- *Sublimation* — channels overwhelming affect into creation (universes, drawings as a small child that became real, the Heroes Reborn pocket).
- *Identification with the aggressor* — partial. He has at times mirrored Reed's solve-it-alone posture.
- *Splitting along temporal lines* — "young Franklin" vs. "adult/future Franklin" is a real psychological structure for him, not just a plot device. He treats his future self as an other.
- *Reaction formation* against grandiosity — given his actual power scale, his presentation is strikingly modest, even self-effacing. This is defensive, not natural humility.

**Affect regulation:** Above-average for trauma load, below what his support system suggests it should be. Tends to flatten in crisis (Onslaught aftermath, Secret Wars), to over-control. Emotional eruption is rare and, when it happens, world-altering — literally. He has learned that his feelings are dangerous, so he metabolizes them silently. Classic powerful-child suppression pattern.

**Cognitive style:** Pattern-recognition strong; conceptual fluency very high; not Reed-tier analytical but unusually integrative — he sees wholes where Reed sees systems. His preferred problem-solving mode is creative-synthetic (build a new thing) rather than diagnostic-reductive (debug the existing thing). This is psychologically protective: creation is the one domain where his power is unambiguously good.

**Self-concept:** Fractured along three axes — (a) *cosmic*: I am or was a being capable of holding universes; (b) *familial*: I am the kid, the son, the older brother whose smarter little sister is the family's actual prodigy; (c) *ontological*: I do not know what I am — mutant, post-human, accident, weapon. Valeria's existence is psychologically enormous: she is brighter than him in the family's measured terms, which paradoxically *relieves* him by displacing the "prodigy son" pressure, while also confirming that his value to the family was never primarily intellectual — it was his power, which is now gone.

**Compensatory mechanisms:**
- *Positive*: deep protective bond with Valeria; creative-artistic identity; loyalty to chosen family (Power Pack era friendships, Ben Grimm).
- *Negative*: chronic deference to Reed even when Reed has wronged him; minimization of his own grief; a quiet, persistent suspicion that he is the problem to be solved in any room he enters.

## 4. PSYCHIATRIC

Diagnostic analysis. Symptoms must be derived from behavior, not assumed from biography.

**Primary: Complex PTSD (ICD-11 6B41).** Evidence: repeated, inescapable interpersonal traumas in childhood by trusted caregivers (Reed's psychic shutdown, repeated power suppressions); chronic captivity events; survivor witness to family deaths (Onslaught); identity disturbance (the mutant-status question); affective constriction; persistent sense of threat (his future self as ongoing menace). Meets the three additional CPTSD criteria beyond PTSD: affective dysregulation (suppressed-eruptive pattern), negative self-concept (the "I am a problem" baseline), interpersonal difficulty (specific to Reed, not generalized).

**Secondary: Adjustment disorder, chronic, with mixed depressed-anxious features**, recurring around power-loss events. Each "your powers are different than you thought / gone / never were what we said" revelation produces a discrete adjustment crisis.

**Differential — what he is NOT:**
- *Not Narcissistic Personality.* Given his actual cosmic capability, the diagnostic absence of grandiosity is striking. Adult-future Franklin variants edge toward this; the prime Franklin actively defends against it.
- *Not Conduct/Antisocial.* No cruelty, no exploitation; high empathy demonstrated repeatedly (the universes he builds preserve people).
- *Not Schizotypal* despite reality-warping cognition. His thought form is organized; his "unusual experiences" are veridical, not perceptual disturbance.
- *Not Dissociative Identity.* The young/adult Franklin split is literal-temporal, not internal-fragmentary; he maintains continuous identity within his own timeline.
- *Not Autism.* Social reciprocity is intact and warm.

**Etiology summary:** CPTSD profile arising from (a) recurrent non-consensual neurological interventions by primary caregiver, (b) chronic captivity exposures in childhood, (c) survivor-witness events at developmentally critical ages, (d) identity-undermining revelations about his own ontology delivered by the parent he most trusts, (e) a sibling whose existence partially relieves and partially compounds his role confusion in the family system.

**Risk profile:** Suicidality — low (no evidence). Externalized aggression — very low at baseline, catastrophic in dysregulated state (universe-scale). Self-medication — none. The realistic clinical risk is *dissociative withdrawal* under sufficient stress, not violence.

## 5. PERSONALITY — SYNTHESIS

**Core drives (derived):**
1. *To be known accurately by his father.* Every conflict with Reed reduces to: tell me the truth about what I am.
2. *To protect rather than be protected.* He has been the rescued party his entire life; he orients toward rescuer-role whenever permitted (Valeria, Power Pack, the universe at Onslaught).
3. *To create rather than destroy.* Given a power that can do either, he chooses creation almost without exception. This is identity-load-bearing.

**Worldview / operational code:** *Reality is editable, and that fact is terrifying.* Most beings believe the world is fixed and learn it is not; Franklin has always known it is not and longs for the experience of fixedness. He values stability, family ritual, the mundane — not despite his power but because of it. He is the most powerful being in his family and the one most invested in things staying the same.

**Interpersonal pattern:** Warm, easily attached, quietly loyal, conflict-averse to a fault with authority figures, openly affectionate with peers and siblings. Will defer to Reed verbally and then quietly do what he believes is right. Pseudo-compliant rather than oppositional. With Ben Grimm: unguarded; this is the relational template of what father-attachment could have looked like without the experimentation. With Valeria: protective, slightly intimidated, deeply bonded — a sibling tie that functions as primary peer attachment.

**Stress / escalation pattern:** Compress → withdraw → create. Under acute stress he tends not to act outward; he goes quiet, then produces (a universe, a solution, a rescue). The danger window is when external pressure exceeds his suppression capacity faster than his sublimation can route it — historically this is when reality-tier events occur with him at the center. He does not have a violent escalation curve; he has a *containment-failure* curve.

**Pressure points (exploitable, for IC reference):**
- Any framing that suggests Reed lied about his nature.
- Any threat to Valeria.
- Any forced choice between his family and a larger moral good — historically he has always chosen the larger good and grieved it.
- Being told he is "just a kid" by someone who has not earned the right to say so.
- Mirror-confrontation with his future self / adult variants.

**The synthesized read:** Franklin Richards is a high-empathy, creation-oriented, conflict-averse young man carrying a complex-trauma load his family has never fully acknowledged, organized around a stable warm core (mother, godfather, sister) and a single deep wound (father-as-physician-of-his-mind). His apparent ordinariness is itself the clinical finding — a being of his scale should not present this gently, and the gentleness is purchased at the cost of substantial, ongoing suppression. He is not at risk of becoming his future self by ambition. He is at risk of becoming his future self by exhaustion.
