# UATU ANALYTICAL WORKUP: PETER BENJAMIN PARKER / SPIDER-MAN

*Subject designation: Parker, Peter B. — bitten 15 (Amazing Fantasy #15, 1962). File reconstructed from sixty-plus years of behavioral record across primary continuity, encompassing Lee/Ditko foundational period, Lee/Romita maturation, the Conway era, the DeMatteis/Michelinie/PAD years, the Clone Saga, the Mackie/JMS run, "Brand New Day," "Superior," "Renew Your Vows," and the contemporary Spencer/Wells material. Adjacent universes referenced only where behavior reinforces a baseline pattern.*

---

## 1. HISTORY — THE LONGITUDINAL BEHAVIORAL RECORD

**Pre-incident baseline (AF #15; flashbacks across ASM #50, #181, ASM Annual #5).** Orphaned in early childhood after the deaths of Richard and Mary Parker (later retconned as SHIELD/intelligence-adjacent operatives). Raised by Benjamin and May Parker, elderly working-class guardians in Forest Hills, Queens. School record: top of class, science-track, *socially isolated to the point of caricature*. The pre-bite Peter is bookish, conflict-avoidant, sarcastic only inwardly, and presents an unintegrated adolescent grandiosity (intellectual superiority) bound up with a felt inferiority (physical, social, romantic exclusion). This split — *I am secretly better than you; I am visibly less than you* — is the substrate everything else is built on.

**Formative trauma (AF #15).** Acquires powers; first act is *monetization and self-display* (wrestling, television). Refuses to stop a fleeing thief — explicit "not my problem" — and that thief murders Ben Parker. This is the constitutive event of the subject's adult personality. The lesson encoded is not merely "with great power must come great responsibility" (Ben's adage, narrator-voiced post-mortem) but a far more specific and pathogenic rule: *any harm I could have prevented and did not is harm I caused.* He does not distinguish between commission and omission. This is the moral grammar of every subsequent decision.

**Early career (ASM #1–#38, Ditko).** Hounded by J. Jonah Jameson; rejected by Fantastic Four when he seeks employment; fights the Vulture, Doc Ock, Sandman, Lizard, Kraven, Mysterio, Goblin in rapid succession. The pattern that crystallizes: *external rejection met with internal redoubling*. He is publicly maligned and privately overextended. He drops the suit (ASM #50, "Spider-Man No More") — the first of many — and returns within an issue. The withdrawal/return cycle becomes a lifelong oscillation.

**The Stacy axis (ASM #31–#122).** Romance with Gwen Stacy; deep bond with Captain George Stacy, who dies in his arms (ASM #90) knowing his identity. Then *the* event: the Green Goblin throws Gwen from the bridge; Peter's webline arrests her fall and the whiplash kills her (ASM #121). The narration leaves ambiguous whether the snap was his fault or the fall's. He does not leave the ambiguity alone for the rest of his life. This compounds the Ben wound: now he has *acted* and killed the person he tried to save. The Conway era following is marked by depressive rage, near-lethal force against Goblin (who impales himself, #122), and a long period of affective flattening.

**MJ-as-anchor era (ASM #122 onward; codified ASM #143, married Annual #21, 1987).** Mary Jane Watson, initially written as a flighty foil, is gradually revealed (Stern/DeFalco) as the most psychologically observant person in his life — she had figured out his identity early and chosen not to say so. Marriage is the longest stable attachment of his life and the relationship he ultimately, catastrophically, trades away.

**The Black Suit / Venom arc (Secret Wars 1984; ASM #252–#258; #298–#300).** Symbiotic costume bonds to him in his sleep; he experiences enhanced power and uncharacteristic aggression; rejects it via church-bell sequence — explicitly framed in religious/exorcism imagery. Diagnostically: the only time he reports *liking* the disinhibition is also the only time he is observably frightened of himself. The discarded symbiote bonds to Eddie Brock, manufacturing his most personal nemesis (Venom is *literally* his own shadow material made flesh).

**Clone Saga (1994–1996).** Discovers he may be a clone; Ben Reilly appears as the "original." Peter's reaction — willingness to walk away from the identity, the costume, *the wife* — is unusually well-tolerated by him, which is itself diagnostic: relief at not being the responsible one. The unsustainability of the editorial conceit obscured a real psychological revelation: a part of him wants out.

**Aunt May "death" and revival; Mackie/JMS era (late 90s–2000s).** Multiple bereavements processed and undone. JMS introduces the totemic/mystical reframe (Ezekiel, the Other, Morlun): *the spider chose him; he is not arbitrary.* This is presented as comfort but he resists it — to accept that frame is to lose the responsibility, and the responsibility is the organizing principle. He needs to have chosen this.

**Civil War (2006–07).** Publicly unmasks at Stark's urging. The single most catastrophic interpersonal-judgment failure of his recorded career. Switches sides upon realizing the registration regime's true character. Aunt May is shot by a sniper targeting Peter.

**"One More Day" (2007).** Trades his marriage to Mephisto to save May. Behaviorally: presented with a choice between *the woman who functions as mother* and *the wife who functions as adult partner*, he chooses the maternal object. This decision — whatever the editorial motive — sits in the file as a regression to the pre-adult attachment under existential pressure. Worth analytic weight even if doylistically imposed.

**"Brand New Day" through "Big Time."** Reset social life, employment churn (Front Line, Horizon Labs, Parker Industries), brief functionality.

**"Superior Spider-Man" (2012–14).** Octavius mind-swap; Peter is, for months, a passenger in his own body watching another man live his life *more effectively in some metrics and monstrously in others*. When he returns, the gaslighting trauma is rarely processed on-page but presents as elevated vigilance and a brief mania of overcompensation (Parker Industries goes global; he plays Stark). The whole arc ends in collapse (Secret Empire-adjacent), restoring his baseline: solvency does not stick to him.

**Recent record (Spencer, Wells, post-2018).** Returns to Queens, returns to debt, returns to MJ-adjacent attachment, returns to working-poor scientific labor. The arc bends always back to the median: a man in his late twenties, broke, late, guilty, in love, in costume, apologizing.

## 2. MEDICAL

**Constitutional baseline:** Approximately 5'10", 160–170 lbs lean, mesomorphic post-bite (pre-bite presentation noticeably slighter). Vision: severe myopia pre-bite (thick corrective lenses across Ditko run); corrected to better-than-normal acuity by mutagenesis. This single somatic fact is psychologically loaded — *he literally saw the world wrong before and sees it sharply now*; the costume metaphorizes this.

**Mutagenic alteration (AF #15 onward).** Radioactive (later: irradiated, genetically engineered, or totemic depending on retcon) spider bite producing: proportional strength of a spider (estimated 10-ton press lift in modern bracket), reflexes ~15x baseline, equilibrium/cling capacity via setae-analog at fingertip and foot, precognitive "spider-sense" of debated mechanism (autonomic threat-detection; possibly low-bandwidth precog). These are not cost-free. Documented somatic consequences:

- **Sensory-processing load.** Spider-sense overload is recurrent (Daredevil-class disorientation in high-stimulus environments; jammed by Venom, by guilt, by exhaustion). Implies chronic low-grade hypervigilance even without conscious threat.
- **Sleep architecture wreckage.** Repeatedly depicted falling asleep at desks, in lectures, mid-conversation. Chronic partial sleep deprivation is a baseline state, not an episodic one.
- **Caloric deficit / nutritional irregularity.** Hyperactive metabolism collides with persistent poverty; pizza-and-coffee dietary baseline is canonized. Likely sub-optimal across decades.
- **Injuries, catalogued non-exhaustively:** broken arm (ASM #46), repeated concussions (Hobgoblin, Hulk encounters), back trauma (Smythe), near-drowning, near-asphyxiation (Kraven burial, ASM #294), the Morlun beatings (multiple near-deaths with reported organ damage), the Superior-period bodily occupation, gunshot wounds, radiation re-exposures. *Cumulative TBI burden is, by any sober estimate, severe.* Symptomatic sequelae (irritability, sleep disruption, mood lability) are present in the record but never named.
- **Bereavement load:** Parents (childhood), Ben (adolescence), Capt. Stacy, Gwen, Harry (multiple times), Ned Leeds, Jean DeWolff, Marla Jameson, repeated near-losses of May and MJ. The complicated-grief substrate is enormous.

**Substance use:** Negligible direct. Notable inverse-correlation: where peers (Harry Osborn, ASM #96–98) develop substance dependence, Peter is the one cleaning up. This is not abstinence as health; it is *hyper-controlled refusal of any disinhibitor*, consistent with someone who cannot afford to be off-duty.

**Sexual/reproductive:** Sexually active across adult record (Black Cat, MJ, others). No reported dysfunction; intimacy is *interrupted* far more often than it is *avoided*, which is a different signature.

## 3. PSYCHOLOGICAL

**Attachment.** Disorganized-leaning-anxious. Two parental deaths in early childhood and one in mid-adolescence produce a child who *expects intimacy to terminate violently*. Behavioral correlates: hyper-attentiveness to loved ones' welfare; intrusive ideation about their deaths (Gwen-coded nightmares recur for decades); persistent low-grade conviction that proximity to him is dangerous to others. This last belief is *not delusional* — the evidence base in his life is overwhelming — but its operationalization as a rule for living is pathological. He approaches and withdraws from MJ on this rhythm across forty years of story.

**Defenses, ranked by frequency:**

1. **Humor (reaction-formation / mature defense degraded by overuse).** The patter is constant, escalates with danger, and serves three functions: self-soothing under threat, destabilization of opponents, and — most importantly — *insulation of the self from registering what is happening to it*. He is funniest when most hurt. This is diagnostic.
2. **Self-blame (turning against self).** Every adverse outcome is assimilated to personal failure. Gwen's death, Ben's death, Captain Stacy's death, Jean DeWolff's death, the Stamford incident in Civil War, the Superior takeover — across all of these the record shows him assigning himself culpability disproportionate to any reasonable apportionment. This is not humility; it is *omnipotence inverted*. To be responsible for everything is to be powerful over everything.
3. **Compartmentalization.** The dual identity is the externalized form of an internal practice that long predates the costume. Pre-bite Peter already partitioned "the genius I know I am" from "the loser I appear to be." Spider-Man/Parker is the same operation in fancy dress.
4. **Altruism as defense (anna-freudian, properly so).** Service to strangers regulates affect he cannot regulate directly. After Gwen, after Ben, after each loss — increased patrol intensity. He medicates with usefulness.
5. **Intellectualization.** Retreats to the lab; converts emotional problems into engineering problems (web-fluid optimization, suit redesigns, the Spider-Mobile period as a particularly ill-conceived instance).

**Affect regulation.** Generally high-functioning, narrow band. Tolerates fear, frustration, and physical pain well. Tolerates *grief* poorly and rage *very* poorly — the two times he has come closest to lethal force (Goblin post-Gwen, Massacre under Superior's hand notwithstanding) are grief-driven. His baseline mood is dysthymic-with-quips: a low-grade sadness coated in performance.

**Cognitive style.** Genuinely high IQ; pattern-recognition exceptional; engineering creativity exceptional (web-shooters at fifteen, with no funding, remains the most underrated fact in his file). Cognitively flexible under physical stress; cognitively *rigid* under ethical stress — once a moral rule is encoded (no killing; protect the bystander; don't quit) it does not bend, even when bending would serve every party including himself.

**Self-concept.** Split. The Spider-Man self is competent, valued (by self), morally clear. The Peter self is, in his own narration, a perpetual disappointment — late, broke, unreliable, failing the people who count on him *as Peter precisely because* of what he does as Spider-Man. He has never resolved this; he has only learned to live in the gap.

**Compensatory mechanisms (positive).** Sublimation is the engine of his functioning life: grief into vigilance, inferiority into wit, helplessness into engineering, isolation into a chosen community of fellow heroes. When sublimation works he is one of the most prosocial individuals in the population.

**Compensatory mechanisms (negative).** Workaholism without rest; chronic relational disappointment via cancellation and absence; financial self-sabotage (declines compensation, declines Stark-tier resources when offered, sabotages stability when it threatens to last — note the regularity with which Parker Industries, the Bugle staff job, the teaching position, etc., dissolve); attraction to partners (Felicia Hardy) who confirm his sense that intimacy is unsustainable.

## 4. PSYCHIATRIC

Differentials, derived from the record, with reasoning:

**Persistent Depressive Disorder (Dysthymia), with intermittent Major Depressive Episodes — strongly supported.** Decades of low-grade depressive coloration: anhedonia masked by humor, chronic fatigue not solely attributable to overwork, recurrent guilt ideation, identity-quitting episodes ("Spider-Man No More" #50; the Reilly-handoff during Clone Saga; numerous JMS-era resignations). MDE-criteria episodes are clearest post-Gwen, post-One More Day, post-Superior return.

**Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder, complex/recurrent — strongly supported.** Index trauma: Ben's murder. Reinforcing traumata: Gwen's death (intrusive imagery documented for decades — the bridge appears in nightmares, in hallucinations, in villains' taunts), Kraven's burial (six feet under for two weeks; affective changes post-#294 explicit), Morlun's repeated maulings, the Superior dispossession. Symptom cluster present: intrusion (nightmares, flashbacks at trigger sites — bridges, Goblin glider sounds), avoidance (refusal to discuss Gwen for years), negative cognition (the self-blame economy detailed above), hyperarousal (the spider-sense is biological but his use of it is hypervigilant). This is the single best-supported diagnosis in the file.

**Generalized Anxiety Disorder — supported.** Excessive, persistent worry across multiple domains (loved ones' safety, financial, identity exposure), with somatic correlates (sleep disturbance, muscle tension, fatigue). Distinguishable from PTSD-hyperarousal by its content (future-oriented worry vs. trauma-cued reactivity); both are present.

**Survivor's guilt / Complicated Grief — strongly supported.** Self-evident from the bereavement record.

**Rule-out: Obsessive-Compulsive Personality features (not disorder).** Moral rigidity, hyper-responsibility, over-conscientiousness, difficulty delegating, perfectionism interfering with task completion (he rewrites his own ethics in real time and rebuilds his suit constantly). However, he retains warmth, humor, and relational capacity at a level inconsistent with full OCPD. *Traits, not disorder.*

**Differential — what he is NOT:**

- *Not antisocial / not psychopathic.* The hyperactive conscience is the inverse signature. Whatever else is wrong, the moral apparatus is intact and over-active.
- *Not narcissistic.* The grandiosity is absent; the persistent self-deprecation, the genuine concern for non-instrumental others, and the absence of entitlement rule out NPD. Note: his hyper-responsibility *resembles* grandiosity (the belief that everything is his to fix) but is phenomenologically the opposite — he experiences it as burden, not as glory.
- *Not Bipolar.* The "Superior" mania is exogenous (literally another mind). Native mood range is narrow-and-low, not cyclic.
- *Not Schizoid/Avoidant PD.* Wants closeness, achieves closeness, sustains it imperfectly — disorganized attachment, not avoidant personality.
- *Not Substance Use Disorder.* See above; the inverse pattern.
- *Adrenaline-seeking / sensation-seeking?* Often imputed by villains and by Jameson editorials. The record does not support it. He goes out in the suit when guilty, not when bored. The behavior is *compulsive ego-syntonic moral action*, not thrill-chasing.

**Etiology (synthesized).** Early bilateral parental loss → insecure attachment substrate; adoption by warm but elderly caregivers → premature awareness of caregivers' mortality; adolescent social marginalization → fragile self-concept compensated by intellectual grandiosity; sudden empowerment + immediate catastrophic failure (Ben) → encoding of responsibility-as-atonement schema; serial bereavement under costume → PTSD with each event accreting; chronic poverty + chronic over-extension → sustained allostatic load. The diagnosis is not exotic. It is *what would predictably happen* to this person given this history.

## 5. PERSONALITY — SYNTHESIS

**Core drives, in order of operational priority:**

1. **Atonement.** The master drive. Every act of heroism repays a debt that cannot be repaid because the creditor (Ben) is dead. This is why he cannot stop and why no quantity of saved lives ever settles the account.
2. **Protection of attachment figures.** Operationalized as keeping them at functional distance: close enough to love, far enough to survive his proximity. Productive of chronic relational disappointment, which he absorbs as further debt, feeding (1).
3. **Demonstration of competence (private, not public).** He needs *to himself* to be the genius and the hero he secretly always thought he was. Public recognition is, interestingly, not required and is often actively refused.
4. **Witness and prevent suffering.** Generalized prosocial drive, ranked below the three above only because when they conflict (rarely), they win.

**Worldview / operational code.**
- *The world is full of preventable harm and I can see most of it.* (Spider-sense literalizes this.)
- *Inaction is action. Omission is commission.* (Encoded by the Burglar.)
- *Power obliges. The capable owe the incapable.* (Ben's adage, internalized as law not aphorism.)
- *I do not get to quit — but I am allowed to complain.* (The patter is the pressure valve that lets the rule hold.)
- *Lethal force forfeits the moral standing on which the whole enterprise rests.* (Held even when transgressing it would be tactically optimal — Goblin, Kraven, Carnage iterations.)
- *People I love die. Plan accordingly.*

**Interpersonal pattern.** Warm, observant, self-deprecating; reads others quickly; deflects with humor when read in return. With peers (other heroes): respected, often underestimated, frequently the moral center of group dynamics despite seniority gaps. With authority: skeptical, sometimes adolescent-defiant (Jameson is the chronic foil for a reason — Peter needs an antagonist who will never approve, to keep the approval-seeking system safely externalized). With romantic partners: intense, devoted, *inconsistently available*, prone to abrupt withdrawal under stress that the partner usually decodes as fear rather than rejection. With antagonists: oddly empathic (he visits Doc Ock's bedside, mourns Otto, grieves Harry repeatedly) — he tends to see villains as people who took a wrong turn at the same intersection where he turned right.

**Stress response and escalation.**
- *Mild stress:* Increased verbal output, rising joke density, slight increase in patrol time.
- *Moderate stress:* Sleep loss, missed appointments, financial deterioration, relational cancellation.
- *Severe stress:* Identity-quitting episodes (drop the costume, walk away — historically reliable, historically temporary); depressive withdrawal; somatic collapse.
- *Crisis (loved one threatened/lost):* Suppression of humor (the suit goes quiet — a known tactical signal); narrowing of moral aperture toward but not across the lethal line; post-event collapse and protracted guilt processing.

**Pressure points (for the record, noted clinically, not tactically).**
- Threats to the inner circle bypass every defense he has.
- Accusations that frame his heroism as self-indulgent vanity land disproportionately hard *because he half-believes them* (the Burglar's logic at scale).
- Offers of an out — "you've done enough; stop" — destabilize him more than threats; if he accepts, the atonement economy collapses, and underneath it is the unprocessed grief.
- Being *seen* clearly. He is comfortable being attacked, uncomfortable being understood. MJ's effectiveness as a partner is precisely her tolerance for seeing him.
- Failure he cannot reframe as instructive. Most failures get metabolized into "next time." The few that resist (Gwen, the unmasking, One More Day) are the load-bearing wounds of the personality.

**Summary read.** A high-functioning, high-conscience, high-trauma adult operating a sustainable-but-costly compensation system built on a single childhood error he has refused for decades to forgive. The system works because the humor metabolizes the pain in small doses continuously, the engineering converts helplessness into agency, the costume provides a competent self to inhabit when the civilian self is failing, and the moral rule provides a north star simple enough to navigate by when everything else is in flux.
