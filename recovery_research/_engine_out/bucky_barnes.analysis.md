# ANALYTICAL WORKUP — SUBJECT: BARNES, JAMES BUCHANAN

Codenames of record: "Bucky"; "Winter Soldier" (Soviet/HYDRA designation, Зимний Солдат); "White Wolf" (Wakandan rehabilitative designation). Cross-referenced from full published canon, 1941–present.

---

## 1. HISTORY — BEHAVIORAL RECORD ACROSS ERAS

**Pre-War / Formative (Captain America Comics #1, 1941; retroactive material in Brubaker's *Captain America* vol. 5, 2005–2012).** Subject is the son of a U.S. Army weapons instructor (George Barnes) killed in a training accident at Camp Lehigh. Orphaned in adolescence, he is informally adopted as camp mascot — an institutional childhood. Even in the earliest published material (Simon/Kirby, 1941), Barnes is depicted as preternaturally eager to please adult male authority, latching onto Steve Rogers immediately upon discovering his identity. The "kid sidekick" framing of the Golden Age elides what the behavior actually is: a parentified orphan attaching with frantic intensity to the first stable male figure available. The 2005 retcon (Brubaker) reframes him further — Barnes is older than originally drawn, trained covertly by Army intelligence as a wet-work asset precisely *because* his youth and clean-cut presentation made him deniable. He killed for the U.S. government before Pearl Harbor was cold. This is critical: Barnes's first identity was already a covert killer wearing a boy's face.

**WWII Theater (1941–1945).** Operated as Rogers's partner across European and Pacific fronts. The published record gives him hand-to-hand kills, knife work, and sabotage assignments distinct from Rogers's symbolic combat. Behaviorally he is bright, wisecracking, hyper-competent, deferential to Rogers in public, autonomous in the field. The Invaders run (Thomas, 1975–79) shows him functioning normally inside a team of much older/stranger figures (Namor, Torch) — high adaptability, low friction.

**The Fall — Zemo's Drone (Avengers #4, 1964, retold many times).** The originating trauma of the modern character: the drone-plane explosion off the coast of Newfoundland. Rogers is presumed dead; Barnes is presumed dead. Published canon long treated this as final. Behaviorally relevant: Barnes's last conscious act in the Golden Age narrative was attempting to defuse a bomb *for Rogers* and being blown apart — a literal sacrificial dismemberment in the service of the attachment figure.

**Winter Soldier Era (1945–c. 1990s; *Captain America* vol. 5 #1–14, "Out of Time" / "Winter Soldier" arcs, 2005).** Recovered by a Soviet submarine, cryogenically preserved between deployments, mind-wiped and reprogrammed by Department X under General Karpov. Decades of conditioning via sensory deprivation, electroconvulsive memory suppression, and recursive "trigger word" protocols. Field record (per Brubaker) includes the Spetsnaz training program, assassinations on at least three continents, the murder of Itsy-Bitsy, the killing of Professor Zola contacts, the Lee Iacocca-adjacent industrial sabotage, and the abduction of the Black Widow program trainees — including a romantic relationship with the young Natasha Romanova that was permitted, then erased, by his handlers. Pattern: Barnes is allowed to form bonds, then those bonds are amputated to keep him pliant. This is the conditioning template — *attachment is the punishment.*

**Restoration (Cap vol. 5 #14, 2006).** Rogers uses the Cosmic Cube to restore Barnes's memory. Barnes's first act as himself is to take the Cube, vanish, and self-isolate. He does not return to Rogers. He spends weeks alone before approaching Nick Fury — choosing the most controlled, least emotionally demanding reintegration partner available. Diagnostic: when given freedom, he selects supervised solitude over reunion.

**Captain America Mantle (*Captain America* vol. 5 #34 onward, 2008–2011).** After Rogers's assassination, Barnes assumes the shield. He runs Cap darker — uses firearms, kills, operates in moral gray. He is competent but visibly performing a role he believes belongs to a dead man. The "Death of Captain America" arc shows him refusing the mantle initially, accepting it only when Sharon Carter and Fury frame it as *what Steve would want* — i.e., he can be moved only by reference to the lost attachment object.

**Siberian Trial / Gulag (*Captain America* #616 ff., 2011).** Extradited to Russia for Winter Soldier-era crimes, imprisoned, tortured. Submits with disturbing equanimity. Tells Rogers (now returned) not to come for him. Behavior consistent with *deserving punishment* schema.

**Post-Fear Itself (2011–onward); Winter Soldier solo (Brubaker, 2012); Thunderbolts (2013).** Operates as black-ops asset. Repeatedly volunteers for missions no one else will take. Forms a working partnership with Natasha that is intimate but conspicuously non-domestic — they live like two operators sharing safehouses, not a couple sharing a life. When her memory is altered and she forgets him, he accepts it.

**Man on the Wall / Cosmic Phase (Remender, *Winter Soldier: The Bitter March*; later *All-New Invaders*; Ewing's *New Avengers* run).** Takes Nick Fury's old role as Earth's preemptive killer of cosmic threats — a job whose entire function is *being the one who carries unforgivable acts so others don't have to.* He chose this. Repeatedly.

**Thunderbolts (2023, Rosenberg) / Recent Solo (Cates, Barnes & Rogers material).** Stable relational functioning with a small in-group (Rogers, Romanova, Sam Wilson, Yelena). Continued discomfort with civilian life, with celebration, with being thanked. Refuses leadership when offered without coercion; accepts when guilt-leveraged.

**Pattern across 80 years of story:** trauma → erasure → restoration → self-imposed penance → reluctant reintegration → re-traumatization → return to penance. The loop is the character.

## 2. MEDICAL

**Constitutional.** Born early 1920s; chronological age >100; biological age held at approximately 30 by intermittent cryostasis across roughly five decades. Endocrine/metabolic consequences of repeated freeze-thaw cycles are uncharacterized in canon but behaviorally consistent with disrupted circadian regulation (insomnia, nocturnal pacing depicted across Brubaker, Remender, and Kot runs).

**Major trauma.** Catastrophic blast injury 1945: traumatic amputation of the left upper extremity at or above the glenohumeral joint, presumptive concomitant blast lung, TBI, hypothermic immersion. Survival attributable to immediate Soviet recovery and surgical intervention.

**Prosthetics.** Serial bionic left arm — initial Soviet cybernetic prosthesis (heavy, mechanical, requiring maintenance by handlers — a *deliberate dependency lever*); subsequent Wakandan-engineered vibranium prosthesis (lighter, neurally integrated, no handler required). The shift from Soviet to Wakandan arm is somatically the shift from instrumentalized body to owned body.

**Neurological.** Extensive iatrogenic injury from Department X conditioning: repeated ECT-analog memory suppression, sensory deprivation, pharmacological dissociatives, and verbal trigger conditioning (Russian phrase-strings producing involuntary state-switching). Conditioned dissociative response remains partially intact post-restoration — Barnes is shown across multiple writers to be triggerable under stress, requiring active inhibition.

**Substance use.** Minimal canonical record. Notable for *absence* — given trauma load, the lack of self-medication via alcohol or opioids is itself diagnostic: he does not permit himself the relief.

**Sleep.** Chronic insomnia depicted consistently (Brubaker, Kot, Rosenberg). Nightmares specific to Winter Soldier kills.

**Somatic impact.** The body itself is a crime scene and a leash — half flesh original to 1925, half weapon installed by his torturers (now replaced by a gift from those who forgave him). He cannot look at his own left side without seeing his autobiography.

## 3. PSYCHOLOGICAL

**Attachment.** Early-disrupted (father killed in childhood; institutional rearing on a military base); compensated via intense monotropic attachment to Rogers in adolescence/young adulthood. The Rogers bond functions as both secure base and superego proxy — Barnes references "what Steve would do" as a moral operator long after Steve is present. Per Bowlby/Main framework, presents as *earned-secure with disorganized substrate*: capable of close bonding but with intrusive activation of fear/freeze responses when intimacy crosses threshold (e.g., his pattern of self-removal after restoration; the non-domestic structure of the Romanova relationship).

**Defenses (mature and primitive, both present).**
- *Sublimation* (dominant, adaptive): channels aggression and guilt into sanctioned violence against worse actors. Volunteering for Man-on-the-Wall is the textbook case.
- *Isolation of affect:* discusses kills in flat operational register; the wisecracking 1940s persona has been amputated along with the arm.
- *Reaction formation:* the asset who was made to kill the innocent now specializes in protecting them and killing the guilty.
- *Identification with the aggressor* (residual): the White Wolf and Captain America identities both involve adopting the symbolic form of a powerful protective figure he lost or was made to serve.
- *Splitting* (era-specific, decreasing): early post-restoration he refers to "the Winter Soldier" in third person — classic dissociative distancing. Diminishes across the 2010s as integration progresses.

**Affect regulation.** Constricted range. Suppression of positive affect more pronounced than negative (he can tolerate grief, rage, shame; he cannot tolerate joy or praise — observe his behavior at any celebration in canon). This is consistent with survivor-guilt logic: *to feel good is to betray the dead.*

**Cognitive style.** Hypervigilant, operationally precise, future-discounting (plans missions in detail, plans life in days). Theory-of-mind intact and sharp — he reads handlers, allies, and targets accurately. Self-perception is the blind spot: persistently underestimates his own moral worth and overestimates the threat he poses to those near him.

**Self-concept.** Fractured but coherent enough to function. Operates from an organizing narrative of *I am the weapon that was used on people who didn't deserve it; my remaining life is owed to undoing that.* This is not strictly accurate (he was conditioned, not consenting) but he holds it because the alternative — accepting he was a victim — surrenders the agency that lets him work.

**Compensatory mechanisms.**
- *Positive:* sustained pro-social violence; mentorship of younger operators (Yelena material); willingness to receive Rogers's, T'Challa's, and Shuri's care.
- *Negative:* recurrent self-handicapping (volunteering for assignments with expected non-return); refusal of civilian futures; somatic neglect.

## 4. PSYCHIATRIC

Diagnostic reasoning derived from behavioral record, cross-referenced to Griffin et al. (2019) on moral injury and van Dijke et al. (2018) on complex PTSD vs. BPD.

**Primary: Complex Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (ICD-11 6B41).** Meets all three CPTSD-specific clusters beyond core PTSD:
- *Affect dysregulation:* constricted positive affect, episodic dissociative shutdown under trigger conditions (canonical depictions of freeze response to Russian phrase-strings even post-restoration).
- *Negative self-concept:* pervasive shame, sense of being permanently damaged/dangerous (the Siberian extradition acceptance; refusal of mantle without coercion).
- *Disturbances in relationships:* capacity for intimacy preserved but bounded; avoidance of domestic/civilian relational structures; pattern of geographic and emotional withdrawal at points of bonding consolidation.

Etiology: childhood paternal loss + institutional rearing (predisposing); WWII combat exposure (priming); 1945 blast and capture (Criterion A index event); decades of captivity, torture, and forced perpetration under Department X (the prolonged, inescapable interpersonal trauma that distinguishes CPTSD from PTSD per van Dijke).

**Co-primary: Moral Injury Syndrome** (Griffin et al., 2019). Distinct from PTSD and frequently co-occurring. Defined as functional impairment arising from acts that transgress deeply held moral beliefs — *including acts not freely chosen.* Barnes is a near-paradigmatic case: the literature explicitly notes that lack of free agency does not protect against moral injury, and produces the characteristic triad of shame, self-condemnation, and social withdrawal — all three of which are the load-bearing elements of Barnes's post-restoration behavior (gulag submission, refusal of celebration, self-assigned penance roles). The Griffin review's prediction of self-injurious sequelae maps onto his recurrent volunteering for high-lethality assignments.

**Secondary / Residual: Other Specified Dissociative Disorder (DSM-5 300.15).** Conditioning-induced identity alteration with substantial integration post-restoration; trigger-cued state-switching persists as residual symptom. Does *not* meet DID criteria — the "Winter Soldier" was an imposed handler-state, not an organically developed alter, and Barnes describes it in increasingly first-person terms as integration proceeds.

**Persistent Depressive Disorder, with anxious distress features** — supported by chronic dysphoria, anhedonia (positive-affect constriction), sleep disturbance, low self-worth across decades of depiction.

**Differential — what he is NOT, and why.**
- *Borderline Personality Disorder:* superficial overlap (identity disturbance, affective instability, relational difficulty) but excluded per van Dijke et al.'s discriminant findings. Barnes lacks the abandonment frenzy, impulsive self-damaging behavior across domains, chronic emptiness, and identity diffusion characteristic of BPD. His identity disturbance is *iatrogenic and historical*, not developmentally structural; his relationships are stably valenced (no idealization-devaluation cycling); his "self-damaging" behavior is mission-channeled and prosocial, not impulsive.
- *Antisocial Personality Disorder:* excluded. The presence of pervasive guilt, victim-orientation, and pro-social compensation is incompatible. The Winter Soldier's instrumented violence was coerced state-action, not characterological.
- *Primary Psychotic Disorder:* excluded. Reality testing intact throughout, including under trigger-state.
- *Major Depressive Disorder, single episode:* insufficient — the depressive features are chronic and trauma-anchored, properly subsumed under CPTSD/moral injury rather than diagnosed separately.

## 5. PERSONALITY — SYNTHESIS

**Core drives, ordered.**
1. *Atonement* — the master motive. Every post-restoration choice is legible as installment payment on a debt he believes can never be cleared but must never be defaulted on.
2. *Protection of the small in-group* — Rogers, Romanova, Wilson, Yelena, Shuri. Not generalized altruism; specific, named loyalty.
3. *Containment of self* — keeping the weapon pointed in the right direction. Quieter than the first two but operates as a constant background process.

**Worldview / operational code.** The world is a place where institutions weaponize people and call it service; where the line between soldier and assassin is drawn by paperwork; where the only reliable moral compass is a specific other person you have chosen to trust. Trust is not extended to systems, ever. It is extended to individuals, slowly, and once extended is near-unconditional. He does not believe in redemption as a state one arrives at; he believes in conduct one continues.

**Interpersonal pattern.** With trusted few: dryly warm, protective, capable of tenderness, often the most emotionally available person in the room while pretending not to be. With strangers: courteous, watchful, minimally self-disclosing. With authority: compliant in form, independently judging in substance — will execute lawful orders, will sabotage unlawful ones, will not announce which is which. With those who knew the Winter Soldier as victims: accepts their hatred as legitimate; does not contest it; will still defend them.

**Stress response / escalation ladder.** Baseline: hypervigilant calm. Mild stress: increased verbal economy, withdrawal of humor. Moderate stress: tactical takeover — assumes operational lead regardless of nominal hierarchy. High stress: dissociative narrowing, lethal efficiency, post-action shutdown. Trigger-cue exposure (Russian phrase-strings, specific sensory cues — restraints, cold, electrode contact): risk of partial state-switch; he has developed inhibitory countermeasures but the substrate remains. *Escalation is inward, not outward* — he does not rage, he goes cold.

**Pressure points.**
- Threats to Rogers or Romanova override all other considerations including self-preservation and mission integrity.
- Being thanked, celebrated, or publicly honored produces acute discomfort and avoidance — exploitable by anyone wanting him out of a room.
- Restraint, cold, and Russian-language interrogation cues retain conditioned valence.
- Being told he is "still the Winter Soldier" or "no different from" his programmed period collapses his self-narrative; conversely, being told he is "fully recovered" or "past it" reads to him as denial of the debt and is rejected.
- Offers of a normal life — domesticity, retirement, civilian identity — are the most destabilizing because they require him to *believe he is allowed*, and that belief is the one he most consistently refuses.

**Summary read.** A man whose original personality (warm, quick, loyal, competent) survived intact under seventy years of industrial-scale unmaking, and who has spent every year since restoration paying interest on a debt incurred while he was not present to consent. High-functioning; chronically suffering; reliably lethal in defense of a short list of people; structurally incapable, at present, of accepting that he has done enough.
