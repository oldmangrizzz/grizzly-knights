# UATU ANALYTICAL WORKUP — SUBJECT: BROCK RUMLOW (a.k.a. CROSSBONES, FRANK SIMPSON variant disambiguated, BENDER, BRIXTON RUMLOW)

Classification: Independent behavioral reconstruction from full published record (Captain America vol.1 #359 first appearance, 1989, Gruenwald — through Brubaker's Captain America run (2005-2012), Remender's Uncanny Avengers (2012-2015), Spencer's Sam Wilson: Captain America, Standoff/Pleasant Hill, Secret Empire (2016-17), and ancillary appearances).

---

## 1. HISTORY — THE COMPLETE BEHAVIORAL RECORD

**Origin Substrate (Gruenwald, Cap #359-362, retroactively expanded in #400-series and Brubaker era).** Rumlow grows up on the Lower East Side of New York, joining a street gang called the Savage Crims as a teenager. He leads them in a sexual assault and the murder of a rival's family; when the victims' father and sister come for revenge, he kills both, putting the sister into a coma. He flees the country at roughly seventeen — this is the first established behavior pattern: extreme violence as a problem-solving instrument, with zero post-incident affective disturbance and an immediate pragmatic exit. He is not a man "broken" by what he did; he was already the man who did it.

In exile he turns up at **Taskmaster's mercenary academy**, where he becomes the senior instructor — meaning by his early twenties he is competent enough to teach professional killers. This is significant: Rumlow is not merely violent, he is *technically excellent*. He is then recruited by the **Red Skull** as field commander of the Skull's strike forces, eventually elevated to the persona of **Crossbones**, the Skull's chief enforcer and personal bodyguard. He remains in this role, in one form or another, for the entirety of his published life. The single most important structural fact of Rumlow's biography: from age ~22 until death, he serves the Red Skull. Every other allegiance is downstream.

**The Gruenwald Era (Cap #359-450ish).** Rumlow as established field operative — kidnaps Diamondback (Rachel Leighton) when she defects from the Serpent Society to Cap; tortures her, attempts to break her, fails; engages in a long-running sexual/possessive obsession with her that he frames as love and she experiences as terror. This is the foundational interpersonal datum: Rumlow's only sustained romantic-coded attachment is structured around captivity, coercion, and the woman's fear. He cannot conceive of being chosen; he can only conceive of taking. He participates in the Skull's body-swap into a clone of Steve Rogers, the Kubekult conspiracies, the Acts of Vengeance peripherals — across this entire period he is a competent, brutal, loyal subordinate.

**Brubaker Era (Cap vol. 5, 2005-2012) — the maturation of the character.** Rumlow is now textured: he runs hit teams alongside **Sin** (Sinthea Schmidt, the Skull's daughter), with whom he forms a genuine partnership-coded bond (the closest thing in his record to a peer attachment, and notably it is with the daughter of his father-figure — a relational geometry that should be flagged). He executes **the assassination of Steve Rogers** on the Federal Courthouse steps (Cap #25, 2007) — the most consequential single act of his career, planned with the Red Skull and Dr. Faustus, pulling the trigger on the rifle round himself. He is captured by SHIELD, interrogated; Sin breaks him out. He is then horribly disfigured by a power blast from the Red Skull's reanimated/possessed body during the "Death of the Red Skull" / "Reborn" sequences.

**Disfigurement & The Mask (Cap: Reborn, Fear Itself prelude).** His face is burned into ruin. From this point forward he wears the skull-faced tactical mask functionally and almost continuously. Behaviorally, the disfigurement does not change his core conduct — he was already a sadist and a fanatic — but it consolidates the Crossbones identity over the residual Brock Rumlow identity. The mask is no longer costume; it is face.

**Fear Itself & post-.** He and Sin pursue the Hammer of Skadi; Sin becomes Skadi, he serves her — note the seamless transfer of devotion from father to daughter, the *Skull-line* being the actual object of loyalty, not any individual. He continues operating through the Hydra-aligned periphery.

**Remender's Uncanny Avengers (2012-2015).** He attempts to assassinate Professor Xavier's funeral attendees; he is the agent through whom the Red Skull obtains Xavier's brain/telepathic powers — a critical plot function, again Rumlow as *instrument*. He survives multiple lethal-grade encounters with the Avengers Unity Squad.

**Standoff / Pleasant Hill / Secret Empire (Spencer, 2016-17).** Imprisoned in the Kobik-generated illusory town, restored to a "normal" identity, then reawakened. Plays a supporting role in the Hydra-Cap takeover, ultimately functioning as muscle for a Steve Rogers he believes to be Hydra's prophesied leader — note: Rumlow does not need the leader to be the Skull; he needs the leader to be *the Skull's project*. Hydra-Cap is the Skull's project. The loyalty migrates cleanly.

**Pattern across eras and writers:** the character is remarkable for *consistency*. Gruenwald's brutal street-killer, Brubaker's professional assassin-fanatic, Remender's terror weapon, and Spencer's true-believer henchman are all the same man, only the lighting changes. The few writers who have tried to soften or complicate Rumlow have done so glancingly; the canon weight is overwhelmingly toward stable sadism in service of an authoritarian cause.

## 2. MEDICAL

**Baseline physiology.** Peak unenhanced human; canon notes Taskmaster-level hand-to-hand, expert in firearms, demolitions, edged weapons. No super-soldier serum, no mutation. He has on multiple occasions absorbed enhancements — exposure to a derivative serum during Brubaker's run augments him toward enhanced strength and durability, but he is never on Cap's tier; he is a high-end enhanced human, not a metahuman.

**Catastrophic facial burns / cranial trauma (Cap: Reborn era).** The energy discharge that disfigures him produces full-thickness burns across the face and scalp with associated soft-tissue loss. He survives with permanent severe disfigurement, almost certainly chronic neuropathic pain, and the social-functional consequences of a face that cannot be shown in public. He wears a tactical/skull mask continually. *Psychological sequelae of the injury:* note the absence of any documented depressive or avoidant response. He does not withdraw; if anything his operational tempo increases. The mask becomes identity-confirming rather than identity-concealing — a man who already preferred to be a symbol of fear now has external justification to never be anything else.

**Cumulative combat trauma.** Decades of professional violence: multiple gunshot wounds (including being shot by the Punisher, Bucky Barnes, and others), blade trauma, blunt impact, falls from height, blast exposure. Almost certainly cumulative mild-to-moderate TBI burden; chronic musculoskeletal injury load consistent with a career operator in his forties.

**Substance use.** No canonical pattern of alcohol or narcotic abuse beyond ordinary mercenary culture. Rumlow is notable in the rogues' gallery for being *disciplined*. His pleasures are violence and (to the extent he experiences it) sex; he does not anesthetize. This is diagnostically relevant — see §4.

**Reproductive/sexual.** Sexually active across the record; coercive history (the Crims-era assault, the captivity of Diamondback). His adult sexual partnership with Sin appears mutual but is structurally enmeshed with shared violence — sex and killing are paired stimuli for him.

**Mortality.** Has been "killed" multiple times and returned; current canonical status fluctuates. Medically immaterial to the workup.

## 3. PSYCHOLOGICAL — REVERSE-ENGINEERED FROM CONDUCT

**Attachment.** The record contains no functional childhood attachment data — no parents, no siblings named, no protective adult figures. The earliest documented relational world is the Savage Crims, a hierarchical predatory peer group within which Rumlow is dominant. The pattern this creates: attachment is *only* legible to him through dominance/submission gradients. He does not bond laterally with equals; he bonds *up* (to the Skull, to Skadi-Sin as Hammer-bearer) or *down* (to subordinates he commands, and to women he can possess). The Diamondback episode is the clearest specimen — when she rejects him, he cannot file this as rejection; he files it as her being temporarily out of his hand, requiring repossession. This is consistent with **disorganized-then-coercive attachment**: no secure base in development, the void filled in adolescence by a gang script in which "love" and "ownership" are not distinguished.

**Defenses.** Predominantly *primitive*: denial, projective identification, splitting, and — most prominently — **identification with the aggressor**. Rumlow's life-organizing solution to a world that produced him is to *become* the most fearsome thing in any room. The skull mask, the name Crossbones, the deliberate cultivation of a terror-aesthetic, are all the externalization of a defense: as long as he is what others fear, he is not what fears. Secondary defenses: somatization is absent (he does not convert distress into body; he discharges it into others), intellectualization is absent (he does not theorize his violence), humor is sadistic (taunting victims). There is essentially no neurotic-level defensive layer; this man does not repress, he *acts*.

**Affect regulation.** He is, by combat standards, *exceptionally* regulated. He does not panic, does not lose tactical composure under fire, can wait. The single reliable affect-disregulator in the record is **threats to or contempt from the Skull-line** — when the Skull is endangered or displeased, when Sin is harmed, Rumlow's tactical patience collapses into rage. This is the tell: his regulation is not internal; it is borrowed from the cause. Remove the cause, and the regulation is hollow.

**Cognitive style.** Operationally intelligent, planful, capable of complex multi-stage operations (the Rogers assassination plan is technically elegant). *Ideologically* simple — he does not engage in the Skull's elaborate Nietzschean self-mythology; he absorbs the conclusions and skips the philosophy. This is functionally a **concrete operational fascism**: he doesn't need the worldview to cohere intellectually, he needs it to designate enemies. Low tolerance for ambiguity. No documented intellectual curiosity outside the operational domain.

**Self-concept.** Stable, grandiose, and martial. He sees himself as an apex predator and the right hand of history's true leader. There is no evidence of impostor anxiety, no evidence of the inflation-deflation cycling characteristic of fragile narcissism. His grandiosity is *consolidated* — closer to the malignant-narcissistic register than the vulnerable-narcissistic.

**Compensatory mechanisms.** *Positive (adaptive in the egosyntonic sense):* extraordinary professional discipline; deliberate physical conditioning; willingness to subordinate ego to mission, which is unusual in his typology and reflects the strength of the Skull-identification. *Negative:* sadism as primary reward channel; women-as-possession schema; total foreclosure of any non-violence-mediated relational capacity; the mask as identity-prosthesis after disfigurement, foreclosing reintegration into civilian life.

**Capacity for empathy.** Cognitive empathy intact and weaponized (he reads victims well, knows what frightens, knows what humiliates). Affective empathy effectively absent — no observed instance, across 35+ years of stories, of him experiencing another's suffering as aversive. The Diamondback torture is the cleanest demonstration: her terror is a *positive* stimulus.

## 4. PSYCHIATRIC — DIAGNOSTIC ANALYSIS

**Principal diagnostic impression: Antisocial Personality Disorder, severe, with prominent psychopathic features (Factor 1 dominant) and sadistic specifier.**

Evidence map:
- *Conduct disorder onset before 15* — Savage Crims violence, group sexual assault, multiple homicides in adolescence (Gruenwald origin material).
- *Failure to conform to lawful behavior, deceitfulness, impulsivity-versus-planfulness, reckless disregard for safety of self and others, lack of remorse, consistent irresponsibility* — all satisfied across the record.
- *Psychopathy Factor 1 (interpersonal/affective):* glib/superficial charm in operational contexts, grandiose sense of self-worth, pathological lying as tradecraft, lack of remorse, shallow affect, callousness, failure to accept responsibility. All present.
- *Psychopathy Factor 2 (lifestyle/antisocial):* present but somewhat *muted* by his disciplined service to the Skull — he does not show the chaotic parasitic lifestyle pattern of the prototypical Factor-2-heavy offender. His antisociality is *organized*. This is what makes him dangerous on a different scale than a Bullseye or a Sabretooth.

**Sadistic specifier (DSM-III-R legacy / clinical descriptor still in use):** Diamondback captivity arc and recurrent torture conduct establish that cruelty is *intrinsically reinforcing*, not merely instrumental. He is not cruel because it works; cruelty is the work.

**Differential — what he is NOT, and why:**

- *Not Narcissistic PD primary,* though narcissistic features are dense. The discriminator: a primary NPD subject would not subordinate himself so completely and durably to the Red Skull. Rumlow's grandiosity is *real estate within* the Skull's larger grandiosity; he does not need to be the leader and shows no documented ambition for the top seat (contrast Zemo, Strucker, Pierce). His narcissism is structurally *malignant-antisocial*, not classical NPD.
- *Not Borderline organization at the level of identity diffusion.* His identity is stable, even rigid. Affect lability is absent. He does not idealize-devalue in the BPD pattern (his idealization of the Skull is constant over decades).
- *Not Schizoid or Paranoid PD,* despite tactical paranoia — his vigilance is reality-tested and situational.
- *Not PTSD,* despite extensive trauma exposure including disfigurement. He shows none of the cardinal clusters: no intrusion phenomena documented, no avoidance (he actively seeks the same stimuli), no negative cognitions, no hyperarousal beyond baseline-tactical. The disfigurement, which would induce body-image and depressive sequelae in most patients, produces in him *intensified role consolidation* rather than distress. The likeliest reason: PTSD requires a pre-trauma self that the trauma threatens; Rumlow's self is already organized around violence, so violence done to him is congruent, not shattering.
- *Not psychotic.* No thought-disorder evidence, no perceptual disturbance. His commitment to Hydra-Cap as the prophesied leader in Secret Empire is *ideological credulity within an authoritarian frame*, not delusion.
- *Not Intermittent Explosive Disorder.* His violence is planful and chronic, not episodic and dyscontrolled.

**Etiology (reconstructive).** A near-total absence of secure early attachment, immersion in an adolescent peer ecology that rewarded predation, and an early-adulthood capture by an authoritarian father-figure (the Red Skull) who provided ideological scaffolding for what was already a sadistic temperament. The Skull functioned as the **organizing introject** that turned a feral street killer into a disciplined operator. Without the Skull, Rumlow is a more dangerous version of his teenage self; with the Skull, he is a strategic asset. This is not a story of nurture *creating* the pathology; it is a story of nurture *organizing* a pre-existing temperament into its most effective expression.

**Comorbid considerations.** Likely chronic-pain syndrome post-burn injury, untreated and personality-congruently denied. Probable subclinical TBI burden. No evidence of mood, anxiety, or substance disorder.

## 5. PERSONALITY — SYNTHESIS

**Core drives, in priority order:**

1. **To serve the Skull-line.** This is the master motive. Everything else is downstream. He needs an authoritarian object worthy of his violence; in the Skull he found it, and the bond has survived the Skull's death, body-swaps, daughter-succession, and proxy-incarnations (Hydra-Cap). Without this object he is unmoored.
2. **To inflict.** Cruelty is intrinsically rewarding. Mission and pleasure align in his work, which is why he is so productive.
3. **To possess.** Women, subordinates, terrain, the body of the enemy after the kill. Ownership-coded relating is his only relational dialect.
4. **To be feared.** The Crossbones persona is curated — name, mask, body language, voice register. He has solved the problem of social existence by becoming a symbol that requires no further negotiation.

**Worldview / operational code.** Hierarchical, social-Darwinist, contemptuous of liberal modernity, organized around the conviction that strength is the only legitimate currency and that the world's natural state is rule by the strong. He does not theorize this; he *inhabits* it. He regards Captain America not as wrong but as *soft* — a man who refuses to use what he has. This is the deep insult Rogers represents to him.

**Interpersonal pattern.** Vertical, never lateral. Up: devotional, disciplined, even capable of self-sacrifice (for the Skull, for Sin). Down: commanding, terror-cultivating, sometimes mentorial in a brutal mode. Peer-level: absent — he has no friends in the normal sense; Sin is partner and Skull-surrogate, not peer. With enemies: theatrical, taunting, sadistic — wants the kill to *mean something* to the victim before it lands. With women outside the hierarchy: predatory and possessive.

**Stress / escalation profile.** Baseline composure is exceptional. Escalators, in order of potency:
- Insult to or endangerment of the Skull-line (most dangerous trigger; produces tactical decompensation into rage).
- Being denied a kill that he has psychologically claimed (the long Diamondback grudge).
- Being treated as *small* or interchangeable — being made a mere thug when he experiences himself as a chosen instrument.

When escalated he does not panic; he becomes *more* violent and *less* patient, which paradoxically degrades his effectiveness because his usual edge is his patience.

**Pressure points (exploitable):**
- The Skull-bond is total and therefore brittle in a specific way: convincing him that his service has been *used* and *discarded* by the Skull-line would not produce remorse, but would produce a kind of identity vertigo that nothing else in his life can. He has no second narrative to fall back on.
- His grandiosity is invested in being *the* enforcer. Substitution — being shown a better, preferred enforcer — wounds him in a way physical injury does not.
- Diamondback specifically: the only person whose memory destabilizes him affectively. The unfinished possession.
- The face under the mask. He does not, behaviorally, appear to grieve it — but its unmasking before subordinates or the Skull-line would produce a humiliation response he has no defense against, because his defenses are all *outward*-facing.

**What he is not, in summary form.** He is not stupid. He is not impulsive in the disinhibited sense. He is not a true ideologue (he absorbs ideology; he does not generate it). He is not without loyalty (he has too much, to the wrong object). He is not haunted by his disfigurement, his victims, or his past. He is, in the precise clinical sense, *uncomplicated*: a successful, durable, sadistic antisocial personality who found the authoritarian movement that fit him and stayed in it for the rest of his life.

**The single most important analytic conclusion.** Rumlow is not a man whose violence expresses inner pain. He is a man whose violence *is* his inner life. Treatments, narrative arcs, or operational strategies premised on locating a wounded core to redeem or exploit will fail, because the core is not wounded — it is organized. The leverage against him is not psychological excavation; it is *displacement of the organizing object* (the Skull-line). Remove or corrupt that, and the structure has nothing to hold it.
