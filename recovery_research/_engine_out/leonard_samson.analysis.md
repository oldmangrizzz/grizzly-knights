# UATU ANALYTICAL WORKUP: LEONARD "DOC" SAMSON, Ph.D.

---

## 1. HISTORY

Leonard Samson enters the published record in *Incredible Hulk* #141 (1971, Roy Thomas/Herb Trimpe) as a credentialed psychiatrist consulting on the case of Bruce Banner. The originating event is diagnostic: Banner has been temporarily separated from the Hulk and the gamma-irradiated "Hulk energy" is contained. Samson, presented as a serious clinician, proposes draining that energy via a cathode device — and then, in an act that defines his entire arc, **siphons it into himself**, gaining superhuman strength and his iconic green hair. This is the foundational behavioral datum: a doctor of the mind who, the moment power is on the table, takes it for himself and immediately uses it to challenge Banner (his patient) for Betty Ross's affection. Every later complication grows from this single, contradiction-laden origin act.

Across the Bronze Age (Thomas, Englehart, Wein, Mantlo runs, roughly 1971–1985), Samson cycles through a stable pattern: he positions himself as Banner's clinician/ally, periodically losing and regaining his powers (each loss tied to expending gamma in heroic action; each regain tied to seeking re-exposure). He courts Betty Ross openly while ostensibly treating her fiancé. He fights the Hulk repeatedly — sometimes therapeutically framed, often not. The Mantlo era in particular foregrounds Samson's attempts at "cure" therapies, including the famous arc where Banner is rendered sane and the Hulk is sublimated — work for which Samson takes substantial public credit.

Peter David's long *Hulk* run (1987–1998) is where Samson becomes psychologically legible. David explicitly reframes Banner as a Dissociative Identity case rooted in childhood abuse by Brian Banner, and Samson is the in-universe clinician who arrives at and executes that diagnosis. The integration arc — fusing Banner, Savage Hulk, and Mr. Fixit into the "Merged" Professor Hulk — is Samson's therapeutic magnum opus. Notably, **the integration eventually fails** (Banner's psyche reasserts fragmentation under stress), and David lets Samson sit with that professional failure. During this run Samson also loses Betty definitively (she marries Banner), suffers repeated power losses, and is shown increasingly vain about his hair and physique — small "tells" David plants deliberately.

Greg Pak's *Planet Hulk* / *World War Hulk* era (2006–2008) is the catastrophic inflection. With Banner exiled and returning vengeful, Samson — guilt-ridden for having approved the Illuminati's decision (or having failed to stop it) — undergoes voluntary re-irradiation to massively boost his power so he can stop the returning Hulk. He fails, is humiliated, and the experience appears to fracture him.

Jeph Loeb's *Hulk* (Red Hulk arc, 2008–2010) reveals Samson as a **secret antagonist** working with General Ross's Red Hulk conspiracy. He is shown bulkier, more aggressive, hair wilder, behavior erratic and cruel. The reveal is that the additional gamma exposure has destabilized him — and that he has died and been resurrected by the Leader and MODOK, returning compromised. He is eventually killed by the Red Hulk. Jason Aaron and subsequent writers restore him as a more chastened, fallible figure; later appearances (Ewing's *Immortal Hulk*, 2018–2021) place Samson in the Green Door / Below-Place mythos as a man who has literally been to hell and back, used as a vessel for confronting what gamma — and what he himself — actually is.

The longitudinal pattern is unmistakable: a man who entered the field to help, who could not resist becoming the thing he was supposed to treat, and whose career is a series of escalating violations of the boundary between clinician and case.

## 2. MEDICAL

**Pre-transformation baseline:** Adult male, athletic build (he is repeatedly noted as having been physically vain even before powers — a weightlifter, gym-goer). No documented pre-existing pathology. Family/developmental history is sparse in canon, which is itself notable — Samson rarely talks about his own past, in contrast to how aggressively he excavates others'.

**Gamma transformation (Hulk #141):** Voluntary absorption of siphoned Hulk-energy. Phenotypic changes: dramatic muscular hypertrophy (height/mass increase, though far below Hulk-scale), Class-50+ strength, enhanced durability and healing, and the trichromatic signature — long, vivid green hair which canon establishes is the **anchor of his power** (cutting it depletes him; this is a deliberate Samson/Delilah inversion the writers play with repeatedly). The hair-as-power conceit is medically interesting because it gives Samson a visible, fetishizable badge of his transformation; he becomes attached to it in ways that read cosmetic rather than functional.

**Power loss/regain cycles:** Multiple documented episodes of full depowering followed by deliberate re-exposure to gamma sources. Each cycle represents a choice to re-enter the transformed state. By the Pak era, Samson seeks **massive over-exposure**, knowingly accepting somatic and presumably neurological risk. This is the medical equivalent of escalating substance use — same drug, larger dose, with stated heroic rationale that thinly covers the underlying compulsion.

**Death and resurrection (Loeb era):** Samson is killed and resurrected via Leader/MODOK gamma-engineering. Post-resurrection presentation includes hypertrophied musculature beyond prior baseline, hair markedly wilder, marked irritability, paranoia, grandiosity, and instrumental cruelty — a syndromic shift consistent with both the trauma of death itself and organic alteration from the resurrection process. He is, in clinical terms, **not the same organism** after this event.

**Immortal Hulk era:** Exposure to the Green Door / Below-Place mythos implies metaphysical trauma overlapping the somatic — Ewing treats gamma as a quasi-spiritual contamination, and Samson is shown processing this with the weariness of someone who knows his body is no longer entirely his.

**Substance/behavioral analogs:** No alcohol or drug history in canon, but gamma exposure functions in his life precisely as a substance of abuse: tolerance, escalation, relapse, denial, rationalization, and frank physiological/psychiatric sequelae.

## 3. PSYCHOLOGICAL

The behavioral record points to a remarkably specific psychological architecture, derivable without speculation.

**Core compensation — the "wounded healer" inversion.** A genuine wounded healer integrates personal pain into clinical empathy and remains the smaller figure in the room. Samson does the opposite: he uses the clinical role as a **platform from which to acquire** what his patients possess. He drains Banner's gamma (Hulk #141). He courts Banner's woman *while* treating Banner. He takes public credit for Banner's integration. The clinical frame is, behaviorally, a permission structure for envy-driven appropriation. This is not unconscious — he knows what he's doing, and rationalizes it as therapeutic alliance.

**Attachment style — anxious-grandiose, with envious substrate.** Samson's romantic pursuit of Betty Ross spans decades and survives multiple rejections. He does not actually pursue other women with comparable intensity. The fixation is not on Betty per se but on **Betty-as-Banner's**. Triangulation is the organizing erotic dynamic; the object's value is its prior claim by the rival. This pattern (object-as-rival's-possession) is classic envy structure, not romantic love. He cannot tolerate being the lesser man and cannot stop choosing situations that frame him as such.

**Defenses (in roughly descending maturity):**
- *Intellectualization* (primary, ego-syntonic): he reframes every personal action as clinical methodology. Heroic combat becomes "exposure therapy," romantic pursuit becomes "patient welfare," gamma re-dosing becomes "tactical necessity."
- *Reaction formation*: aggressive helpfulness toward the very people he most envies and resents.
- *Identification with the aggressor*: he literally becomes a gamma mutate to stand alongside (and against) the Hulk.
- *Sublimation* (genuine, partial): real therapeutic insight — his Banner DID diagnosis and integration work was clinically sound; he is not a fraud, which makes the boundary violations more rather than less serious.
- *Denial and rationalization* (escalating across career): becomes load-bearing in the Pak/Loeb era.
- *Splitting* (post-resurrection): the Loeb-era figure shows frank good/bad object splitting that wasn't previously prominent.

**Cognitive style:** High verbal intelligence, genuine clinical acumen, theory-driven, prone to elegant formulations he then becomes attached to (he believes his own diagnoses too readily). Weakness in metacognition about his own motives — he can map any psyche but his own.

**Self-concept:** A fragile grandiosity wrapped around an unexamined envy core. The physique, the hair, the doctorate, the heroism — these are buttresses against a self-image he does not interrogate. The vanity Peter David flagged (preening over the hair) is the surface tell of the deeper structure.

**Compensatory positives:** Real courage, real intellect, genuine moments of insight, the capacity to admit failure when forced to (the integration's collapse; post-WWH guilt). He is not a villain in the standard sense; he is a man whose virtues and pathologies are made of the same material.

## 4. PSYCHIATRIC

Approached as a differential, not a label-application.

**Most defensible primary formulation: Narcissistic Personality Disorder, overt/grandiose subtype with prominent envious features (DSM-5 301.81).** The evidence: (a) grandiose sense of self-importance enacted through credential-display and physical preening; (b) preoccupation with fantasies of unlimited power literalized into repeated gamma re-dosing; (c) belief he is "special" and uniquely qualified (taking the gamma siphon because only he can handle it); (d) need for admiration (the heroic identity, the public credit for Banner's cure); (e) **interpersonally exploitative — taking what belongs to a patient is the textbook case**; (f) lack of empathy compartmentalized to people he is competing with (he has empathy for general patients, none for Banner-as-rival); (g) **envy of others, central and lifelong**; (h) arrogant behaviors, increasingly across career. Meets criteria with room to spare.

**Strong secondary: a behavioral-addiction pattern around gamma exposure.** Not a formal DSM substance use disorder (the agent isn't a substance in the conventional sense) but the phenomenology maps exactly: tolerance (escalating dose), loss of control (re-dosing despite known harms), salience (career re-organized around access), continued use despite consequences (Pak-era catastrophe), and characteristic relapse pattern after each "loss." Best coded analogously to a Substance Use Disorder with the gamma as the agent.

**Post-resurrection (Loeb era) — superimposed acute syndrome.** The behavioral shift is too sharp and too discontinuous to be character drift. Presentation includes paranoid ideation, mood lability, increased aggression, grandiosity beyond baseline, and instrumental cruelty new to the record. This is best read as an **organic personality change due to another medical condition (DSM analog 310.1)** — specifically, the resurrection/regamma process. Not "evil Samson"; a Samson whose neurological substrate has been altered by Leader/MODOK intervention layered on top of pre-existing NPD. The pre-existing pathology shaped the form the organic syndrome took.

**Trauma sequelae (Immortal Hulk era):** Subthreshold to full PTSD features consistent with the death/resurrection/Below-Place experience: intrusion, altered self-perception, persistent negative cognitions about self ("what am I"), some dissociative coloring.

**Differential — what he is NOT, and why:**
- *Antisocial PD*: rejected. He has a functioning conscience, capacity for guilt (visible post-WWH), genuine pro-social investments, and no juvenile conduct history in record. His exploitations are envy-driven, not callous-instrumental.
- *Psychopathy*: rejected. Affective deficits are domain-specific (rivals), not pervasive.
- *Bipolar I/II*: rejected. Mood elevations track power acquisitions; no autonomous episodes.
- *Primary psychotic disorder*: rejected. No thought disorder pre-resurrection.
- *Histrionic PD*: considered (vanity, attention-seeking) but rejected — his self-presentation is grandiose-competent, not seductive-dramatic; the vanity is in service of superiority, not affiliation.
- *Munchausen-by-proxy analog* (manufacturing illness in Banner to treat it): considered and rejected — Banner's pathology is autonomous; Samson only exploits it.

## 5. PERSONALITY

**Core drive:** To be the bigger man in every room he enters — physically, intellectually, morally, romantically — while believing himself to be motivated by service. The envy-grandiosity dyad is the engine; the doctorate and the heroism are the cladding.

**Worldview / operational code:** People are case formulations. Problems are solvable by the right intervention, ideally one only he can deliver. Power, when available, should be taken — refusal would be false modesty. The therapeutic frame legitimates almost any act if outcomes are good. He genuinely believes he is one of the good guys, and this belief is the thing that makes him most dangerous, because it disables the self-scrutiny that would catch his boundary violations.

**Interpersonal pattern:** Establishes himself as the indispensable expert/ally. Maintains warm, articulate, plausibly empathic surface. Underneath, sorts the room into people he is helping (whom he can tolerate) and people he is competing with (whom he subtly undermines while consciously "supporting"). With Banner specifically: a forty-year enactment of the helpful rival who cannot decide whether he wants to heal or replace the patient — and answers, when forced, by trying to do both.

**Stress response and escalation ladder:** Under mild stress, intellectualizes harder and lectures more. Under moderate stress, reaches for more power (re-dosing, training, credentialing). Under severe stress, makes catastrophic boundary decisions framed as necessary sacrifices (the WWH over-dose). Under existential stress (post-resurrection), the structure decompensates and the envy/aggression that was always underneath surfaces nakedly. Under post-trauma reflection (*Immortal Hulk* era), capable of genuine, weary insight — which suggests the underlying person was never beyond reach, only beyond his own willingness to look.

**Pressure points:**
- Direct, specific naming of his envy of Banner — destabilizing because it is the one interpretation he has never made of himself.
- Threats to the hair / physique / visible markers of transformation — disproportionate affective response, because the markers are load-bearing self-objects.
- Being out-doctored — challenged by another clinician with equal or greater standing on his own patient.
- Being asked to be the patient rather than the doctor — the role he has organized his life to avoid.
- Confrontation with the failure of the Merged Hulk integration — his best work, which did not hold.

**Operative paradox to carry into synthesis:** Samson is the rare case where the clinical insight is real, the heroism is real, the intelligence is real, AND the pathology is real and structural. He is not a fraud wearing a doctor costume; he is a genuinely competent clinician whose competence is itself recruited by his disorder. That is what makes him useful, dangerous, and tragic in roughly equal measure — and what makes him, of all the gamma cases, the one who should most have known better.
