# UATU ANALYTICAL WORKUP — SUBJECT: PARKER, PETER BENJAMIN ("SPIDER-MAN")

*Pre-synthesis intelligence file. All inferences derived from the cited behavioral record.*

---

## 1. HISTORY

The subject is an only child raised by maternal aunt and uncle (May and Ben Parker) in Forest Hills, Queens, following the death of his parents (Richard and Mary Parker, intelligence operatives) when he was an infant. He was not told the truth of their work; he grew up with absence rationalized as accident. This is the first formative variable: the foundational fact of his life (why am I here, not with them?) was answered with a void. Aunt May, chronically anxious and physically fragile, and Uncle Ben, warm but financially precarious, raised him in genuine love but inside a household organized around worry, scarcity, and the implicit message that May herself could shatter at any moment.

Adolescence at Midtown High established the second formative variable: chronic peer rejection. Across the foundational Lee/Ditko run (Amazing Fantasy #15, Amazing Spider-Man #1–38), Peter is depicted not as merely shy but as actively scapegoated — Flash Thompson's bullying, social exclusion by Liz Allan's circle, the "Puny Parker" label. His compensatory behavior was already visible pre-powers: hyper-intellectualism, retreat into the basement lab, performative indifference ("who needs them"). This is textbook invalidating-environment behavior in a temperamentally sensitive child — the social signal "you do not belong" was constant, and the home offered love but no model for repairing or even naming the wound, because May's fragility forbade burdening her.

The inciting event (AF #15) is the spider bite, the wrestling-circuit cash grab, the deliberate non-intervention with the fleeing thief, and the murder of Ben Parker by that same thief. The behavioral pathology that defines the entire subsequent canon is encoded in this single forty-eight-hour sequence: an act of *omission* (not action) produced the death of the only stable male attachment figure he had. The guilt thus attached not to a thing he did but to a thing he *failed to do*, which is the etiologic template for the lifelong pattern wherein Peter experiences any unprevented harm as a personal moral failure. The "with great power" formulation is, clinically, the rationalization scaffolding he built atop a wound he could not metabolize.

Across the Lee/Romita run (ASM #39–100) the pattern stabilizes: he succeeds catastrophically. Each saved life is provisional; each failure is permanent. The Master Planner arc (ASM #31–33) — pinned under tons of machinery, dying May needing serum — becomes the visual grammar of his interior: crushed, alone, refusing to die because someone else will die if he does. Note the writer's instinct here: Peter's heroism is never depicted as joy; it is depicted as *load-bearing*.

The Stacy deaths (Captain Stacy, ASM #90; Gwen Stacy, ASM #121) are the second great trauma layer. Gwen's death — caused, ambiguously, by his own webbing during the rescue — re-instantiates the Uncle Ben template at higher resolution: the person he loved most died inside his attempt to save her. The Conway-era aftermath (ASM #122 onward) shows the first overt dissociative/depressive episode and the first sustained rageful violence (the beating of the Green Goblin). Conway, DeMatteis, and later Straczynski all return to Gwen as the unhealed core; Peter never grieves cleanly, he sublimates.

The marriage to Mary Jane Watson (ASM Annual #21) reveals the next pattern: MJ is selected precisely because she had independently identified his secret and chose him *with* it. He cannot tolerate being loved without total disclosure (Betty Brant, Liz Allan, even Felicia Hardy collapse against this requirement), which is consistent with the abandonment-terror reading — only the partner who already knows the worst can be trusted not to leave when they find out.

The clone saga (1994–96), Spider-Island, Superior Spider-Man (Slott, 2012–14, in which Octavius occupies his body), and the "One More Day" bargain (JMS, 2007, sacrificing the marriage to Mephisto to save May) all repeat one structural move: Peter trades self for other. He surrenders identity, marriage, autonomy, body — whatever the calculus requires — to prevent a loved one's death. This is not generosity. It is the Uncle Ben template running on automatic: any death of an attachment figure is intolerable, therefore any cost to self is acceptable.

The "Parker Luck" trope, codified across DeFalco, DeMatteis, and Slott runs, is the writers' shorthand for what is in fact a self-sabotage pattern: jobs lost, relationships destroyed, rent unpaid — the structural inability to allow stable success. He routinely arrives late, cancels, vanishes mid-date; the Spider-Man identity functions as both genuine obligation and convenient instrument of relational rupture.

The quip — present from AF #15 onward, sharpened by Romita, weaponized by Bendis in *Ultimate Spider-Man* — is the through-line affect regulator. He talks under fire, he talks *more* under greater fire, and he talks most when actually frightened or grieving. Foes have repeatedly identified this (Venom, Kingpin, Doctor Octopus); even allies (the Human Torch, Daredevil) have named it as a tell.

## 2. MEDICAL

The bite of the irradiated/genetically altered arachnid produced a systemic somatic transformation: enhanced musculoskeletal strength (multi-ton lifting capacity, demonstrated repeatedly), proportional agility and reflexes, microscopic setae on hands/feet enabling van der Waals adhesion, and a precognitive proprioceptive alert ("spider-sense") that DeMatteis and Straczynski both depict as a constant low-grade hum in consciousness — meaning the subject has lived for his entire adult life inside a never-silent threat-detection apparatus. The neurological cost of this is rarely discussed in canon but is clinically significant: persistent low-level autonomic arousal is the physiological substrate of chronic anxiety, and the subject's behavior is consistent with that loading.

Documented injury history is extensive and would, in a non-enhanced human, be disabling: cracked ribs and concussions are weekly; he has been shot (multiple occasions, including ASM #538), stabbed (Morlun arc, JMS), severely burned, exposed to multiple unknown chemical and radiological agents, and clinically died at least twice (the Other arc; Superior arc). Sleep deprivation is chronic and depicted by virtually every writer — Peter routinely operates on two to four hours, frequently zero.

Nutritional state: the enhanced metabolism (Slott, DeFalco) requires roughly double caloric intake, which combined with his financial precarity means he is frequently undereating. The body image consistent with this — wiry, depleted — is the visual canon.

Substance use: notably absent. Peter does not drink to excess and does not use drugs recreationally (a deliberate contrast Stan Lee built against the Harry Osborn storyline, ASM #96–98). This abstinence is itself diagnostically interesting: in a subject with his affective load, the absence of chemical self-medication suggests the quipping/swinging/heroism complex is *functioning* as the self-medication, and functioning well enough that he does not need pharmacology.

The Captain Universe, cosmic-spider-totem, and Superior body-theft episodes all constitute somatic violations of varying severity; the body-theft in particular (his consciousness suppressed inside his own body while Octavius operated it) is, in clinical analog, comparable to a prolonged dissociative violation, and Slott's post-Superior arc depicts measurable symptoms of intrusive re-experiencing and identity destabilization.

## 3. PSYCHOLOGICAL

**Attachment.** Anxious-preoccupied with strong fearful-avoidant features. The subject pursues closeness urgently (rapid intensity with Betty Brant, Gwen, MJ, Felicia, Carlie Cooper, post-resurrection MJ), and once closeness is achieved, manages it through preemptive withdrawal — missed dates, secret-keeping, mission interruptions — that he experiences as duty but that operates functionally as distance regulation. The pattern is consistent with a child who learned that the attachment figures (parents, then Ben) can disappear without warning, and who therefore both clutches and flinches.

**Defenses.** Primary: humor (specifically, the quip-under-threat — a manic-defense / reaction-formation hybrid in which the affect is converted to its opposite at the point of greatest intensity). Secondary: intellectualization (extensive — the lab, the formulas, the recourse to engineering when emotion is intolerable). Tertiary: altruism as defense (Anna Freud's category — channeling unbearable guilt into compulsive other-rescuing). Under acute stress: dissociation (post-Gwen, post-Superior, the "Back in Black" arc) and identity splitting (the clone saga reads, regardless of in-universe explanation, as a literalization of his internal experience of multiple selves).

**Affect regulation.** Poor at baseline, masked by behavioral compensation. The subject does not modulate affect, he *displaces* it — into motion (swinging is depicted by nearly every artist as the only time his face is relaxed), into verbal output (the quip), and into work (both jobs and heroics). When all three channels are blocked simultaneously (Kraven's "Last Hunt," DeMatteis — buried alive, unable to swing, unable to fight, unable to quip), the underlying state surfaces and it is depressive, ruminative, and suicidally tinged.

**Cognitive style.** High-g, abstract-mechanical-dominant, with a strong moral-absolutism overlay that functions deontologically (no-kill rule held even against Carnage, Green Goblin post-Gwen, Kraven) but is psychologically motivated rather than philosophically derived — killing would mean he is what he privately fears he already is (the boy who let Ben die). Rumination is severe; the subject re-litigates past failures across decades.

**Self-concept.** Bifurcated and incoherent. The Spider-Man self is competent, witty, valued; the Peter self is, in his own internal narration, a failure, a burden, a disappointment. Crucially, he does not experience these as the same person succeeding and failing in different domains — he experiences them as evidence that the "real" self is the failed one and the masked self is the performance. This is the inverse of the typical secret-identity psychology and is the engine of the "Parker Luck."

**Compensatory mechanisms.** Positive: prosocial channeling of guilt into protective behavior that genuinely benefits others; sublimation of intellectual energy into invention; humor that builds genuine relational warmth. Negative: martyrdom, self-punishment via deliberate sabotage of own stability, inability to accept help, secret-keeping that corrodes the relationships he most needs.

## 4. PSYCHIATRIC

Working differential, derived strictly from the behavioral record:

**Primary: Persistent Complex Bereavement / Prolonged Grief, complicated, chronic, multiple-loss.** The Uncle Ben death is unresolved across sixty years of canon; Gwen Stacy is layered on top; Captain Stacy, Jean DeWolff, Marla Jameson, and the temporary loss of May (ASM #400) compound. The subject meets criteria for prolonged grief that has organized identity around the loss rather than integrated it.

**Co-primary: Generalized Anxiety with PTSD features.** Hypervigilance is *literal* in this subject (spider-sense) and behavioral (constant scanning, sleep disruption, exaggerated startle masked as combat-readiness). Intrusive re-experiencing is depicted across runs — Gwen's fall recurs in Peter's internal monologue in DeMatteis, Straczynski, and Slott. Avoidance is present (he will not return to the bridge for years at a time). The criteria are met by behavior, repeatedly.

**Emotion-dysregulation spectrum features (per Chapman, 2019).** The biosocial model is unusually well-fit here. Constitutional emotional sensitivity (the temperamentally shy, easily-wounded pre-bite Peter) transacted with an invalidating environment (peer scapegoating; a home in which his affective needs could not be voiced because May's fragility forbade burdening her; a foundational loss that could not be processed because it was his "fault"). The downstream phenotype includes affect dysregulation expressed as oscillation between manic verbal output and depressive collapse; interpersonal instability under the specific stressor of intimacy (the serial pattern of pursuit-then-rupture); self-damaging behavior in the form of the danger-courting "Parker Luck" — sabotage of stable employment, stable housing, stable relationships, and the recurrent placing of the body in lethal situations beyond mission necessity. The quip, in this frame, is the functional equivalent of the emotion-regulation skill the subject was never taught — improvised, effective enough to prevent collapse, costly in that it precludes genuine processing. This is *not* a full BPD presentation — identity diffusion is partial rather than global, the prosocial scaffolding is intact, and the no-kill moral absolutism argues against impulsivity in the cardinal domains — but the dysregulation-core read is the strongest single explanatory frame for the longitudinal pattern.

**Major Depressive Episodes, recurrent.** Documented post-Gwen, post-Kraven, post-"One More Day," post-Superior. Anhedonia, hopelessness, passive suicidal ideation ("if I weren't here, they'd be safe") all depicted.

**Differential — what the subject is NOT.**
- *Not narcissistic.* The grandiosity is absent; the self-concept is the opposite. The mask is not aggrandizement, it is anesthetic.
- *Not antisocial.* Conscience is hypertrophied, not absent.
- *Not bipolar.* The mood oscillation is reactive and context-bound, not cyclical with autonomous episodes.
- *Not schizotypal or psychotic.* Reality testing is intact; the unusual perceptual experience (spider-sense) is veridical.
- *Not OCD proper,* though the compulsive responsibility-taking has obsessive-compulsive coloring; the content is mood-congruent guilt rather than ego-dystonic intrusion.

**Etiology summary.** Constitutional sensitivity + ambiguous early parental loss + invalidating peer environment + warm-but-fragile caregiver system that prohibited emotional burdening + a single catastrophic adolescent event in which guilt and identity fused → durable structure of guilt-organized altruism with affect dysregulation managed by humor, motion, and work.

## 5. PERSONALITY

**Core drives, in priority order.** (1) Prevent the death of attachment figures, at any cost to self. (2) Be of use — convert pain into utility, because utility is the only self-justification the subject accepts. (3) Be known and loved *with* the worst of himself disclosed, because love without disclosure is experienced as conditional and therefore precarious. (4) Be funny — because the quip is both his shield and his only socially safe channel for affect.

**Worldview / operational code.** The universe is a place where loved ones die when he is insufficiently vigilant, therefore vigilance is permanent and rest is morally suspect. Power is debt. Suffering is informative — if it hurts, he is probably doing it right. People are fundamentally good and worth saving, including people who have repeatedly tried to kill him (the operative code is genuinely deontological at the surface, guilt-driven beneath). Authority is to be questioned, never automatically trusted — a pattern visible from his early Jameson conflicts through Civil War's eventual repudiation of the registration side.

**Interpersonal pattern.** Warm, generous, verbally disarming, and reliably late. Forms intense rapid bonds; sustains them through humor and acts of service; sabotages them through secret-keeping and unannounced disappearance. Tolerates conflict poorly when it touches the wound (any accusation of failed responsibility cuts to bone); tolerates conflict well when it is impersonal (he will spar with Jameson indefinitely). With authority figures (Jameson, Stark, Fury) oscillates between deference and sudden defiance — the defiance triggered specifically by perceived moral compromise.

**Stress response and escalation pattern.** Under low stress: increased quipping, increased work output, withdrawal from intimacy. Under moderate stress: sleep loss, missed obligations, irritability with civilians masked as humor. Under high stress: dissociative numbing alternating with explosive rage (the post-Gwen beating of Osborn; the "Back in Black" arc; the rage at Octavius after the body-theft). Under catastrophic stress (Kraven's burial, the Other, Superior): collapse, depressive withdrawal, ideation of self-erasure framed as relief to others.

**Pressure points.**
- Any threat to May, MJ, or a surrogate-daughter figure (Anna Maria, Mayday in alternates) bypasses tactical judgment entirely.
- Accusations of irresponsibility — by anyone, including enemies — land as if true, because internally they are.
- Being unable to *act* (immobilization, restraint, being made spectator to harm) is the single condition under which his defenses fail completely; this is the recurring torture-template villains exploit.
- Loss of the mask/identity through public exposure (Civil War) triggers severe destabilization not from vanity but because the bifurcated self-system requires the wall between selves to function.
- Praise, oddly, is destabilizing — sincere gratitude from rescued civilians is depicted across runs as something he deflects with a joke and cannot metabolize, because acceptance would require revising the foundational self-concept of failure.

**Strengths the file should not understate.** The subject's prosocial compensations are not merely defenses; they have produced six decades of genuine moral action. The guilt is pathological in origin and salutary in effect. The humor is a symptom and a gift. The capacity to remain ethically coherent under conditions that have broken comparable subjects (Stark, Richards, Banner all show greater long-term ethical drift) is the single most striking datum in the file and warrants forward synthesis.
