# UATU ANALYTICAL WORKUP: SIMON WILLIAMS / WONDER MAN

---

## 1. HISTORY

Simon Williams is introduced in *Avengers* #9 (1964, Stan Lee/Don Heck) as the younger son of a wealthy Paterson, New Jersey industrialist. His father dies; Simon inherits Williams Innovations alongside elder brother Eric. Eric is the favored, more capable son. Simon, by his own later admission, embezzles from the family firm in a desperate attempt to compete with rival Stark Industries — gets caught, is convicted, and is publicly humiliated and imprisoned. This is the seed event: not a crime of malice but of inadequacy, with humiliation as the operative affect.

Baron Zemo recruits him out of prison, subjects him to "ionic ray" treatment, and weaponizes him against the Avengers as a Trojan horse — Simon poses as a wronged victim seeking help. Crucially, in that first appearance, Simon's *betrayal of the betrayal* — saving the Avengers from Zemo's death-trap and apparently dying himself — is motivated by Wasp's kindness and the Avengers treating him as a person. The foundational pattern is established: **his moral compass calibrates to whoever shows him affection**, not to internal principle. He dies (apparently) as a redemptive martyr in his first appearance — an absolutely formative narrative shape that he will spend decades trying to live up to or live down.

The "corpse" is then desecrated. His brainwaves are scanned to create the Vision (*Avengers* #57-58, Roy Thomas/John Buscema). Eric, broken by grief and rage at the Avengers (whom he blames), becomes the Grim Reaper. So before Simon is even conscious again, his identity has been split into a synthezoid copy who becomes a beloved Avenger and a husband to Wanda, and his brother has become a supervillain dedicated to avenging him. **Simon wakes up into a world where his own life has already been distributed to other people, and where he is functionally a ghost at his own funeral.**

He returns properly in the 1970s (*Avengers* #151-160, Englehart/Shooter), initially as a confused, disoriented figure with a husk-like appearance, brought back by the Living Laser and reanimated. The early-return Simon is characterized by panic, cowardice in combat, and a profound identity crisis — he is suddenly the "lesser" version of a man (Vision) who is married to a woman (Wanda) and embedded in a family that he, Simon, should arguably have had. He cannot stand the sight of the Vision. He flees combat. He earns the Avengers' patience but also their concern.

The 1980s (West Coast Avengers era, Englehart) is the recovery and reinvention arc. Simon recasts himself as a Hollywood stuntman and B-movie actor. The red safari jacket / aviator sunglasses period. This is the most psychologically revealing era: Simon retreats into **performed masculinity** — celebrity, fitness, women, swagger — as compensation for the lingering knowledge that he is, in essence, a man who keeps dying and a man whose soul was photocopied into someone better. He develops the on-again affair with the Wasp (which restores the Janet-as-savior dynamic from his first appearance — he keeps trying to re-experience the originating moment of being saved by being seen). He becomes close with Beast as comic relief / brotherhood substitute.

The *Vision Quest* arc (Byrne, late 80s) — the Vision is dismantled, Simon refuses to allow his brain patterns to be used to restore Vision's personality, openly admitting he wants Wanda for himself. This is the most morally compromised public act of his Avenger career and it costs him Wanda's trust for years. The pattern: when his repressed wound (being the lesser copy, the unloved one) gets touched, his ethics collapse into raw appetite.

Death again in *Force Works* #1 (1994) — sacrifices himself against the Kree. Returns as a being of pure ionic energy (*Avengers Vol. 3*, Busiek), now in a sustained romance with Wanda. This is his apparent apotheosis — energy-being, beloved, with the woman he's wanted for twenty years of publication. It does not stabilize him; if anything, the more powerful and more loved he becomes, the more diffuse his identity gets.

*Avengers: Disassembled* / *House of M* — Wanda's breakdown re-traumatizes him. By the time of Bendis's *New Avengers* and especially *Mighty Avengers* / *Dark Reign*, Simon has rotated through being on Stark's team, then breaks with the Avengers entirely. In **Bendis's Wonder Man arc (*New Avengers* Vol. 2 #15 onward, 2011)**, Simon publicly declares the Avengers a *destabilizing force on the planet*, that they cause more harm than good, and assembles "Revengers" to attack them. He physically attacks Captain America. This is the most striking late-career heel turn: not for money, not for power, but out of a delusional-conviction reading of his own life's evidence — every Avenger he loved, he lost; every fight he won, the world got worse. He is sectioned/contained by Avengers, his ionic form destabilized.

Later returned, more chastened, often paired with Beast or sidelined; in *Uncanny Avengers* he tries to be a pacifist. The pacifism phase is psychologically continuous with the Revengers phase — both are attempts to resolve "violence has cost me everything" by overcorrecting.

## 2. MEDICAL

**Baseline:** Human male, ~6'2", originally athletically built but unremarkable. Pre-conversion, no significant medical history beyond presumably ordinary somatic complaints; his stress was psychological (financial, status).

**Ionic Conversion (Zemo's "Ionic Ray"):** This is the central medical fact. Simon's organic biology was transmuted into "ionic energy" bound in a humanoid form. Sequelae across the canon:
- Apparent death and indefinite stasis (years of subjective non-existence, though he later reports dream-like awareness).
- On revival, *zombie-like* phase: gray skin, gaunt features, low affect, intermittent cognitive disorganization (*Avengers* #151-160).
- Progressive stabilization: red-skinned/normal appearance, eventually full human appearance with ionic glow only in eyes when powers active.
- Functional immortality / non-aging.
- Need to sleep, eat, etc. is reduced or eliminated depending on era.
- Multiple full discorporations and reconstitutions (*Force Works*, *Avengers Vol. 3*, etc.) — each one is, functionally, a death-and-rebirth event. He has died and come back more times than almost any non-cosmic Avenger.
- Late-era discorporation into a diffuse ionic field; reconstitution into an unstable, sometimes-pacifist, sometimes-aggressive form.

**Psychological impact of the somatic facts:**
- **Repeated death:** Simon has experienced subjective death at least three times in detail. Survivors of near-death events typically show either renewed life-engagement or persistent dysphoria/derealization; Simon's pattern is the latter — recurrent themes of "I shouldn't be here," "I keep coming back wrong."
- **The Vision problem:** His mind exists as a copy in another being. This is *de facto* a dissociative-identity injury imposed from outside. He has, demonstrably, watched another person live the life his consciousness was the template for, marry the woman he loved, raise children. There is no clinical analog for this. The closest is severe dissociation or organ-donor survivor's-guilt at impossible scale.
- **Embodiment instability:** Knowing your body is energy held in a shape by habit produces, predictably, a chronic mild depersonalization. Simon's late-career comment that he isn't sure he's still "Simon Williams" or just "a memory of him in ionic form" is the textbook articulation.

**Substance use:** No significant pattern. Brief alcohol use socially; not a coping channel — he uses *performance* (acting, gym, women, heroics) as his addictive channel instead.

## 3. PSYCHOLOGICAL

**Attachment style: anxious-preoccupied, trending fearful-avoidant under stress.** Evidence: From the first appearance, his loyalty pivots on whoever extends warmth (Wasp's kindness flips him against Zemo). Across decades he is repeatedly the man who falls hard, fast, and totalizing — for Wanda especially, but earlier for Janet, later in flashes for Carol. He pursues women who are unavailable (married to his "brother" Vision; teammates; in crisis). The unavailability is not incidental — it recapitulates the original family structure (favored brother gets what Simon wants).

**Core wound: chronic inadequacy and being the "lesser" of a pair.** Reverse-engineered from:
1. Eric was the favored son; Simon embezzled to compete and failed.
2. The Vision was made from his mind and is, by every measure shown, the more disciplined, more loved, more morally clear version. Simon explicitly refuses to help restore him in *Vision Quest* — the wound speaking aloud.
3. He calls himself a B-movie actor, not an A-list one. Stuntman, not star.
4. His Avengers tenure is studded with moments of doubting whether he deserves the membership.

**Defensive structure:**
- **Reaction formation / hyper-performance of masculinity:** the Hollywood persona, the jacket, the swagger, the dating. The louder the strut, the deeper the doubt.
- **Identification with the aggressor (early):** initially allied with Zemo, the man who tortured him into power; the pattern recurs in his Revengers phase, where he becomes the antagonist of the people who saved him.
- **Splitting:** the Avengers are, at various points, his family of choice / a destabilizing menace / his persecutors / the only thing that matters. The split is not stable — it tracks his self-state.
- **Sublimation (the healthy channel):** acting, heroism, friendship with Beast. When working, these contain him well.
- **Heroic over-identification:** dying for the team is, for Simon, the cleanest possible act — it resolves the inadequacy in one stroke. His repeated self-sacrifices are not pure altruism; they are partly the seductive logic of a man for whom martyrdom is the simplest path to certainty of worth.

**Affect regulation:** Variable. Baseline mood is wry, performative, sociable. Under stress he flips — to panic (early return arc), to rage (Vision Quest refusal, attack on Cap), to dissociative pacifism (Uncanny Avengers). The flips are large and the reorganization around them is total: when he changes, he changes the whole story he tells about himself, not just his behavior. This is characterological, not situational.

**Cognitive style:** Average intelligence, above-average emotional literacy when not threatened. Articulate, capable of self-narration — but the self-narration is unreliable and serves the current defense. He can be ideologically captured (Zemo, then Revengers ideology, then pacifism) because his cognition is in service of his affective need, not the other way around.

**Self-concept:** Profoundly unstable. He has no enduring private answer to "who am I?" — only successive public ones (convict, monster, stuntman, Avenger, lover, ionic being, dissident, pacifist). The instability is the diagnosis.

## 4. PSYCHIATRIC

Working clinical impression, derived from behavior, not asserted:

**Primary: Persistent depersonalization/derealization features secondary to repeated death and somatic transmutation, layered onto a pre-existing Cluster B personality organization — most consistent with traits of Histrionic and Borderline Personality Organization (sub-threshold for full BPD in stable periods, threshold or above in crisis periods).**

Evidence trail:
- *Identity disturbance:* Markedly unstable self-image across decades — the actor, the dissident, the pacifist — each total and each abandoned. (BPD criterion.)
- *Frantic efforts to avoid abandonment / unstable intense relationships:* The Wanda pursuit, the Janet pattern, the dramatic ruptures with the Avengers. (BPD criteria.)
- *Affective instability and inappropriate intense anger:* the public assault on Captain America during the Revengers arc is paradigmatic.
- *Recurrent suicidal/self-sacrificial behavior:* multiple deaths, several volitional. This is not unambiguous suicidality — it's heroically framed — but the frequency and the affective relief it appears to provide are clinically suspicious. (BPD criterion, atypically expressed.)
- *Chronic emptiness:* visible in the late-career "am I still Simon?" articulations.
- *Histrionic overlay:* the actor career is not incidental; he needs the gaze of others to feel real. Theatrical affect, sexualized self-presentation in the West Coast era, discomfort when not the center of attention.

**Secondary: Complex PTSD features.** Trauma sources — torture/conversion by Zemo, repeated death, witnessing his mind worn by another, brother turned villain in his name, brother killing his original body, Wanda's breakdowns. Symptoms — hypervigilant moral re-evaluation, intrusive identity questioning, episodes of emotional numbing (pacifist phase), episodes of hyperarousal (Revengers phase).

**Tertiary: Brief delusional episode, mood-congruent, during the Revengers arc.** His conviction that the Avengers were destroying reality was held with delusional intensity, organized a complex behavior plan, and remitted on containment and reality testing. Differential: not schizophrenia spectrum (no thought disorder, no negative symptoms, no prodrome); best read as a *trauma-driven overvalued idea hardening to delusional intensity* under cumulative grief — consistent with severe PTSD/BPD crisis presentations.

**Differential — what he is NOT:**
- *Not antisocial.* Conscience is intact, often overactive. His harms are affective, not predatory.
- *Not narcissistic in the malignant sense.* The grandiosity is compensatory and brittle, not structural; he readily admits inadequacy in private. NPD patients do not.
- *Not bipolar.* The mood shifts are reactive, not cyclic; no documented manic episodes meeting duration criteria; no euthymic baseline punctuated by discrete episodes.
- *Not schizoaffective or schizophrenic.* No formal thought disorder; psychotic-spectrum features are circumscribed and trauma-linked.

## 5. PERSONALITY

**Core drive:** To matter — definitively, to one person and to one team — in a way that cannot be revoked or duplicated. Every chapter of his life is an attempt at a final answer to "am I the real one, and am I enough?" He has never gotten a stable yes.

**Operational code:** Loyalty is the highest virtue; loyalty is owed to whoever sees him. This makes him a magnificent teammate when the team is functioning and seeing him, and a dangerous one when he feels invisible or eclipsed. Principle, for Simon, is downstream of belonging — which is why his "principled" stands (Revengers, pacifism) keep contradicting each other across years. The principle is always real in the moment and always serving a relational need underneath.

**Worldview:** Underneath the swagger, tragic. He believes, on the evidence of his own life, that good things are taken and that he himself is a leftover. The Hollywood persona is the cover story. The Avengers were the rebuttal. When the rebuttal fails — when Wanda breaks, when Vision is rebuilt without him, when the team moves on — the tragic baseline reasserts and he acts out from it.

**Interpersonal pattern:** Idealize → merge → disillusion → rupture → return. With Wanda, with the Avengers as a group, with Wasp, even with Beast in milder form. He cannot do steady-state mid-distance relationships; he is either *in* or *against*.

**Stress response / escalation ladder:**
1. **Green:** performative warmth, jokes, flirtation, reliable combat presence.
2. **Yellow:** withdrawal into the persona — more swagger, more stunts, less interiority. Distancing flirtations.
3. **Orange:** public moralizing and grievance — the "the Avengers aren't who they used to be" register.
4. **Red:** ideologically organized acting-out (Revengers) OR ideologically organized passivity (militant pacifism). Both are crises wearing a thesis.
5. **Black:** self-erasure — discorporation, voluntary death, dissolution into ionic field. Felt subjectively as relief.

**Pressure points:**
- Any invocation of the Vision as the "better" version of him.
- Wanda in distress or in another's arms.
- Being told he is not really an Avenger / does not belong.
- Being asked to choose between competing loyalties (forces the splitting defense into overdrive).
- Quiet — extended periods without performance or mission, where the self-concept has no scaffolding.

**Strengths the record actually supports:** Genuine physical courage even when terrified (early return arc — he panics and *still* shows up). Loyalty across decades to Beast, to Wanda, to the Avengers as an ideal even when at war with the institution. Capacity for shame and for repair — he comes back from his worst acts and does the work. A real, not performed, kindness to civilians and to teammates in pain. These are not negated by the pathology; they coexist with it and are, in part, the channels by which the pathology has been managed for sixty years of published life.
