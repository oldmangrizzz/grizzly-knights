# UATU ANALYTICAL WORKUP — SUBJECT: ERIC BROOKS ("BLADE")

*Stage One: Evidence Reconstruction From Canon. No conclusions imported; all inferences derived from observed behavior across the published record (Tomb of Dracula #10, 1973 — present).*

---

## 1. HISTORY

**Origin Event (Tomb of Dracula #10, Wolfman/Colan, 1973).** Subject is born in a Soho brothel/bordello in London while his mother, Tara Brooks, is exsanguinated by the vampire Deacon Frost during labor. The blood transfer at the moment of parturition produces the subject's hybrid physiology. This is the load-bearing wound of the entire file: the maternal death and his own birth are the *same event*, and the agent of both is the same creature. There is no separable mourning — his existence and his loss are one act. Every subsequent behavior pattern in the record is downstream of this fusion.

**Adolescence.** Raised by his mother's coworkers (a brothel staff who, per the original Wolfman text, treated him with affection) until roughly age nine, when he witnesses an elderly jazz musician, Jamal Afari, being attacked by vampires. He kills one of the vampires himself, is taken in by Afari, and is trained in trumpet, blade-work, and vampire-hunting in tandem. Afari is later turned and Eric is forced to stake him. **Pattern emerging early:** every paternal/protective figure ends in a corpse Eric himself produces. The recurrence is structural, not incidental.

**The Bronze Age Hunter (Tomb of Dracula run, 1973–79).** Operates as a freelance hunter, teak knives, leather jacket, no powers beyond immunity to vampire bite. Loosely allied with Quincy Harker, Frank Drake, Hannibal King. Behavior in this period: blunt, sneering, contemptuous of teammates' caution, repeatedly walks alone into ambushes. Wolfman writes him as the *angriest* member of an already grim cast — the one who refuses Harker's strategic patience.

**Morbius/Hannibal King partnership (late 70s–80s, sporadic).** First time the record shows him in sustained partnership with another hybrid (King — a "good" vampire). His tolerance of King is a critical data point: he can accept the *category* of vampire intellectually when the individual proves out, but only after testing to destruction. He is not a pure essentialist.

**Nightstalkers (1992–94, Marc McLaurin).** Forms a detective-agency triad with King and Drake. The book ends in mass trauma (Drake nearly killed, occult catastrophe). Eric is committed to a psychiatric facility afterward — *canonical psychiatric hospitalization* is in the record, not an inference. He emerges believing essentially every public figure is a vampire (the "everyone is a vampire" arc, mid-90s). This is a documented psychotic or near-psychotic break, not metaphor.

**The Daywalker Retcon (Blade film 1998 → comics absorption, late 90s onward).** Marvel comics retroactively integrate the film's "Daywalker" framing: vampire strengths, human weaknesses sublimated, thirst now a managed condition. Christopher Hinz's *Blade: Sins of the Father* and subsequent runs reframe him as not-merely-immune but actively hybrid. **This is critical for the workup:** the *thirst* is now canonical. His prior abstemiousness is reframed as ongoing suppression.

**Marv Wolfman / Bart Sears, Chris Claremont, Christopher Hinz, then Marc Guggenheim (2006–07 series).** Guggenheim run is the most psychologically dense: explores his estranged father (revealed to be Lucas Cross / a still-living human), his recruitment briefly into S.H.I.E.L.D., and his sustained inability to maintain civilian intimacy. He sleeps with a woman who turns out to be a vampire; he kills her. Pattern: *intimacy and lethality collapse into each other.*

**Captain Britain & MI:13 (Cornell, 2008–09).** Operates inside a team, under command, on British soil — psychologically significant because it is a *return to the geography of the wound* (London) and the only sustained period in the record where he willingly accepts hierarchical command. He functions. Brief romance with Spitfire (Jacqueline Falsworth, a vampire). The relationship works *precisely because* she is his structural opposite — aristocratic, English, vampiric-but-heroic. He cannot do unconflicted intimacy; he requires a partner who is *already inside the wound.*

**Modern Era (2015 Soule run, *Strikeforce*, *Avengers* Aaron run, *Bloodline: Daughter of Blade* 2023).** Reveals an adult daughter, Brielle Brooks (Bloodline) — paternity he was unaware of. His handling: distant, surveillance-from-afar, late intervention. Mirrors his own Afari relationship with the polarity reversed; he reproduces the trained-protector dynamic but is structurally unable to *be present.* In *Avengers,* serves on the team but is the designated dark-ops asset — the one tasked with the act others will not commit (e.g., the Moon-Knight-related arcs, the willingness to kill where teammates hesitate).

**Behavioral evolution across eras.** Bronze Age: pure rage-driven, low affect range. 80s: grudging capacity for partnership. 90s: catastrophic decompensation, hospitalization, paranoid ideation. Post-Daywalker: more controlled, more verbal, more strategically intelligent — but the underlying structure has not changed; it has only acquired better armor and a managed somatic condition.

## 2. MEDICAL

**Hybrid physiology (dhampir).** Documented capabilities: enhanced strength/speed/reflexes (low-to-mid superhuman, never Hulk-tier), accelerated healing, extended lifespan (apparent aging dramatically slowed post-30), heightened senses, immunity to vampiric conversion via bite. Documented deficits/conditions:

- **Bloodthirst.** Post-Daywalker canon: a chronic, suppressed craving for human blood. Originally managed via a serum (film-derived, integrated into comics) and later via discipline alone. **Functionally an addiction-equivalent condition** — chronic, lifelong, demanding daily self-management, relapse-capable under stress or injury.
- **Healing factor with cost.** Severe trauma sustained repeatedly across the record; he heals but the *psychological inscription* of injury appears not to attenuate.
- **Psychiatric hospitalization** (post-Nightstalkers). Documented inpatient stay following psychotic break with paranoid ideation.
- **Childhood somatic exposure.** Born during exsanguination event; in-utero or peri-natal vampire saliva/blood exchange. Whatever the metaphysics, the body itself is the crime scene.

**Substance use.** No documented substance abuse in the record. The thirst functions as the in-canon analog — and his *abstinence pattern* around it is rigid, white-knuckled, never moderated. This is consistent with a constitutional addict's profile applied to the only substance that matters.

**Somatic-psychological coupling.** The body is the evidence of the trauma. He cannot dissociate from the wound because the wound is *in his blood, metabolized hourly.* Most trauma survivors can, at minimum, leave the scene. He cannot. He *is* the scene.

## 3. PSYCHOLOGICAL

**Attachment.** Disorganized, with a fused trauma/love object. Primary caregiver (mother) is, in the same instant, killed and the source of his existence. There is no pre-trauma baseline of secure attachment to recover toward. Every subsequent attachment in the record (Afari, Harker, King, Spitfire, his daughter Brielle) is approached with a pattern of: cautious probing → conditional commitment → emotional withdrawal at the moment intimacy stabilizes → catastrophic loss event (real or anticipated) → re-isolation. He does not fail to bond; he bonds and then *flees the consummation of the bond,* which is observable when he repeatedly chooses solo work directly after teams begin to cohere (Bronze Age Harker group, Nightstalkers, MI:13).

**Defenses (in order of dominance).**

1. **Splitting.** Vampires = evil; humans = to be protected. This is the *organizing schema of his consciousness.* His own hybridity is the unresolvable scandal at the center of this split — he is himself the category he exists to annihilate. Post-Daywalker writers exploit this with increasing sophistication.
2. **Reaction formation.** The hunter identity is the inversion of the predator identity he could otherwise be. Every kill is also a refusal of a kill he could perform on a human.
3. **Sublimation.** The thirst is channeled into combat aggression — a relatively high-functioning defense, which is why he remains operational rather than predatory.
4. **Isolation of affect.** His verbal register across the record is laconic, dry, often sardonic. Affect is compressed and discharged through violence rather than expressed relationally.
5. **Identification with the aggressor (partial).** He has adopted Deacon Frost's literal weaponization, mirroring back upon vampires the lethality enacted upon his mother. Hunting Frost specifically is, until that arc resolves, the spine of the project.

**Affect regulation.** Narrow band. Baseline: controlled grimness. Activation threshold: low, particularly for vampire-adjacent stimuli. Recovery: poor in isolation, marginally improved in structured team settings (MI:13 data). Capacity for sustained positive affect: minimal in the record; flickers visible with Spitfire, with Brielle, with King during quieter beats.

**Cognitive style.** Concrete, tactical, present-focused. Pattern-recognition for threat is exceptionally fine-tuned. Strategic planning is competent but not his preference — he is a *closer,* not an architect (which is why he tolerated Harker and later S.H.I.E.L.D. command structures). Abstract/symbolic cognition is underdeveloped or actively suppressed; he resists psychological language about himself across multiple writers' runs.

**Self-concept.** A weapon. This is repeatedly stated in his internal monologues across decades. Not "a man who hunts" but "a thing built for this." The instrumental self-definition both protects him (a weapon does not need to be loved, healed, or made whole) and traps him (a weapon cannot retire).

**Compensatory mechanisms — positive.** The discipline around thirst. The loyalty he extends, slowly, to those who survive his vetting. The trained-protector instinct toward younger fighters and (latently) his daughter.

**Compensatory mechanisms — negative.** Pre-emptive abandonment of intimacy. The 90s paranoid generalization ("everyone is a vampire") — the worst-case version of his pattern-recognition when reality-testing collapses. The compulsive return to combat as the only legible self-state.

## 4. PSYCHIATRIC

Derivations from the behavioral record, with appropriate differential reasoning. Diagnoses framed as analogs given the metahuman substrate.

**Complex PTSD (ICD-11 analog).** Strongly indicated. Criteria mapped from record: (a) prolonged, inescapable traumatic exposure (his own body); (b) disturbances in self-organization — persistent negative self-concept ("weapon"); (c) affective dysregulation narrowed to anger/numbness; (d) interpersonal disturbance — sustained difficulty in close relationships. Etiology: peri-natal trauma + serial losses of attachment figures, each by the same mechanism, several by his own hand.

**Substance Use Disorder analog (thirst).** Post-Daywalker canon supports a chronic-craving condition with abstinence-based management. Behavior pattern matches: rigid avoidance, no harm-reduction tolerance, hypervigilance for cues, white-knuckle quality. He does not have the moderation profile; he has the abstinent-addict profile.

**Brief psychotic episode, paranoid features (mid-90s, post-Nightstalkers).** Documented in canon via hospitalization and the "everyone is a vampire" arc. Best read as a decompensation under cumulative trauma load rather than a primary psychotic disorder — episodic, contextually triggered, resolved with treatment, no recurrence in record. Differential vs. a primary schizophrenia-spectrum condition: he does not show negative symptoms, disorganized thought, or chronic delusional structures outside that episode.

**Differential — what he is NOT:**

- **Not Antisocial Personality Disorder.** Lethal, yes; remorseless toward vampires, yes. But the structure is moral, not amoral — he protects, he loyalty-bonds, he experiences guilt (visible after Afari, after the vampire lover, in his handling of Brielle). ASPD requires pervasive disregard; the record shows constrained-targeting with a coherent ethical frame.
- **Not Narcissistic Personality Disorder.** Self-concept is denigrating-instrumental ("weapon"), not grandiose. He does not seek admiration; he is indifferent to it.
- **Not Borderline Personality Disorder despite splitting.** The splitting is *ideological-categorical* (vampire vs. human), not *interpersonally oscillating* (idealize/devalue of the same person). His attachments are stable in valuation even when distant.
- **Not Schizoid PD.** He desires connection — the evidence is the repeated attempts at it. He cannot sustain it. Schizoid presentation lacks the desire; his lacks the capacity.

**Most parsimonious psychiatric formulation:** Complex PTSD as primary; chronic craving/addiction-analog as comorbid; trauma-related personality organization with paranoid and avoidant features (subclinical when stabilized, clinical under load); one documented brief psychotic episode in remission.

## 5. PERSONALITY

**Core drive.** The annihilation of the category of entity that produced him — which is also the annihilation of half of himself. The project is constitutively unfinishable, which is the engine of his persistence and the source of his exhaustion. He cannot win; he can only *continue.*

**Worldview / operational code.** The world contains predators and prey; his job is to interpose. Authority is provisionally tolerated when competent (Harker, MI:13 command, S.H.I.E.L.D. briefly) and discarded when it equivocates. Civilians are to be protected without being known. Vampires are to be killed without negotiation, with the narrow exception of those who prove out under sustained pressure (King, Spitfire). Trust is earned over years and revoked in seconds. Self-pity is contemptible; self-knowledge is suspect; introspection is a luxury enemies exploit.

**Interpersonal pattern.** Approaches cold. Tests through provocation and contempt — those who absorb it without retreat earn a hearing. Loyalty, once granted, is durable past the point of his own safety. Intimacy is approached and abandoned; sexual relationships in the record are sparse, intense, and end in violence or separation. He is a competent team member in tightly-bounded operational contexts and a poor one in any context requiring sustained emotional reciprocity.

**Stress / escalation profile.** Under acute stress: focuses, narrows, becomes more lethal and more verbally controlled. Under chronic stress: isolates, sleep-deprives, ruminates on Frost-equivalent figures, loses peripheral relationships. Under cumulative overload (Nightstalkers data point): reality-testing degrades along the lines of his existing schemas — vampires multiply in his perception until they are everywhere. The decompensation is *coherent with the baseline,* which is what makes it dangerous; he does not become disorganized, he becomes *more himself,* without brakes.

**Pressure points.**

1. *His own thirst.* Any scenario that forces him to feed, contemplate feeding, or witness himself enjoy violence in a recognizably predatory register destabilizes him faster than any external threat.
2. *Maternal-coded women in proximity to vampires.* The original wound is recapitulation-prone.
3. *Paternal/mentor figures.* He has buried too many; new ones are received with pre-emptive distance, and their endangerment provokes disproportionate response.
4. *His daughter.* The newest and least-tested vector. Brielle is the first person in the record whose existence implies a future he must survive *for,* rather than a past he must avenge. The record is too young to know whether this is therapeutic or catastrophic for him; the analytical prediction is that it will be both.
5. *Being named, accurately, as what he is.* He tolerates being called a monster (he half-believes it); he does not tolerate being called a man (he does not). The praise that treats him as fully human is, paradoxically, the harder blow.

**Synthesis for the profile stage.** Subject is a high-functioning trauma-organized operator whose entire personality is a stable scaffolding built around a wound that cannot heal because it is metabolic. The scaffolding works. It has worked for sixty years of in-universe time. It works because he never asks it to do anything except hold. Any intervention — narrative, interpersonal, or operational — that requires the scaffolding to *flex* rather than *hold* is where the file's risk concentrates.
