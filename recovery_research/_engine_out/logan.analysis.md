# UATU ANALYTICAL WORKUP — SUBJECT: LOGAN ("Wolverine" / James Howlett / "Weapon X" / sundry aliases)

Classification: independent psychological/behavioral case file. Six decades of published record collated and reverse-engineered. Conclusions are mine, derived from the cited behavior, not inherited from prior narrators.

---

## 1. HISTORY — Longitudinal Behavioral Record

**Origin layer (retconned 2001 in *Origin* #1–6, Jenkins/Quesada/Kelly).** Born James Howlett, late 1880s, Alberta, to a wealthy and emotionally cold landowning family; sickly, sheltered, asthmatic, emotionally enmeshed with the redheaded servant girl Rose. The triggering event — Thomas Logan's killing of John Howlett — produces the first bone-claw extrusion and the killing of his biological father (later revealed). The child then *flees identity itself*: takes the name "Logan," exiles himself to a Yukon mining camp, eventually lives feral among wolves. This is the inaugural template: catastrophic violence → identity dissolution → flight into wilderness/animality. It repeats for a century.

**Late 19th–early 20th c. drift (Origin, *Wolverine: The End*, *Logan* miniseries fragments).** Loses Rose to his own claws in an accidental impalement during a fight with "Dog" (Thomas Logan's son). Pattern established: *the people he loves die on his claws or because of him.* He withdraws from human society for years at a stretch.

**Military / mercenary century (assorted: *Wolverine* vol. 2 #34 "Blood and Claws," *Wolverine: Origins*, *Uncanny X-Men* #268, *Captain America* annuals).** Both World Wars, Spanish Civil War, postwar covert ops. Pre-Weapon X relationship with Captain America (mutual respect, functional). Pre-WWII bond with the Yashida clan in Jasmine Falls, Japan — the *only* sustained period of relative integration he ever achieves; he studies Bushidō under Bando Saburo. This becomes his lifelong organizing fantasy: *the samurai I could be*. Romance with Itsu, who is murdered while pregnant (Daken's birth, *Wolverine: Origins*). 1940s Madripoor partnership with Carol Danvers and Natasha Romanoff (*Uncanny X-Men* #268). Team X / CIA black-ops cell with Maverick, Sabretooth, Mastodon, Silver Fox — with implanted false memories from the start (*Wolverine* vol. 2 #48–50, "Logan: A Dog's Life").

**Weapon X (Barry Windsor-Smith, *Marvel Comics Presents* #72–84, 1991).** The defining trauma. Abducted, dehumanized ("the animal"), adamantium-bonded under live surgical conditions, repeatedly drugged, used as a remote-controlled murder weapon against bears, wolves, and humans. The Professor/Cornelius/Hines triad explicitly *engineer* dissociative states. He escapes in a berserker fugue, kills the staff, and is found naked in the snow by James and Heather Hudson — who provide the first post-trauma holding environment.

**Department H / Alpha Flight era (*Alpha Flight* #1, *Incredible Hulk* #180–182, 1974).** Functions as government asset. Defection to Xavier (*Giant-Size X-Men* #1, 1975) is, behaviorally, another flight from handlers.

**Classic X-Men era (Claremont, 1975–1991).** The crucial behavioral data set. Repeated patterns:
- *Protector-of-the-young attachment:* Jean Grey (frustrated/idealized), Storm (peer), and especially the surrogate-daughter bond with Kitty Pryde (*Kitty Pryde and Wolverine* mini, 1984) and later Jubilee (*Wolverine* vol. 2, 1988+) and Rogue. These bonds are non-sexual, fiercely protective, and pedagogical — he teaches them to *survive him*, essentially.
- *Japan as redemptive ritual space:* the Mariko Yashida arc (*Wolverine* limited series, 1982, Claremont/Miller) — the explicit attempt to "be a man, not an animal." Mariko's poisoning death (*Wolverine* vol. 2 #57) is delivered *by his hand* at her request. Once again: the beloved dies on the claws.
- *Berserker rage:* described by Claremont and successors as a dissociated state he "comes back from" with memory gaps and remorse. Behaviorally distinct from anger.

**Identity-fragmentation arcs.** *Weapon X* (Windsor-Smith), *Wolverine: Origins* (Way), the "Logan: A Dog's Life" sequence, *House of M* (where his suppressed memories return whole and incapacitate him with grief), and the Romulus arc all show the *same structural fact*: he cannot reliably distinguish authentic autobiography from implanted memory. The Professor (Weapon X), Romulus, and Apocalypse (turning him into Death, *X-Men* #186, *Apocalypse Twelve*) have all overwritten him.

**Adversarial pair-bonds.** Sabretooth (Victor Creed): an annual "birthday" ritual of attempted murder spanning a century — the most stable relationship in his life is with the man trying to kill him. Daken (the son raised by enemies to hate him): repeats the trauma of failed fatherhood. X-23 (Laura Kinney): the clone-daughter; the *one* opportunity to do fatherhood differently, which he handles with comparative competence.

**Late-career hardening (1990s–2000s).** *Enemy of the State* (Millar): brainwashed by Hand/Hydra into killing colleagues — yet another override of will. *Old Man Logan* (Millar/McNiven, 2008): tricked by Mysterio into slaughtering the X-Men; goes 50 years refusing to pop the claws. The *Old Man* response — total behavioral inhibition, depressive shutdown, vow of nonviolence — is the inverse symptom of the same disorder: when the dissociative weapon-self is too contaminated, the conscious self goes catatonic.

**Death and return (*Death of Wolverine*, 2014; *Return of Wolverine*, 2018).** Drowns in adamantium; literal encasement in the metal that has defined him. Returns with renewed memory fragmentation and Persephone-implanted kill triggers — *the cycle is structural, not narrative.*

**Krakoan era (Hickman et al., 2019+).** Resurrection protocols make the death/dissociation cycle institutional. Continues protector-of-young role with the Quiet Council.

The diachronic point: across writers from Wein to Claremont to Hama to Windsor-Smith to Millar to Aaron to Hickman, *the same three behaviors recur*: amnesia/identity confusion; berserker dissociation under threat-overload; compulsive surrogate-paternal attachment to adolescent girls. That cross-writer consistency is what makes the inference defensible.

## 2. MEDICAL

**Mutation profile.** Accelerated cellular regeneration ("healing factor"); hyperacute olfactory, auditory, and visual sensoria; retarded biological aging (apparent age ~mid-40s across 130+ chronological years); three retractable osseous claws per forearm, extruding through dorsal hand fascia.

**Iatrogenic somatic load (Weapon X procedure).** Subcutaneous bonding of molecular adamantium to entire skeleton including claws. Procedure performed without informed consent, with paralytic but not full anesthetic (the record indicates he was conscious for portions). Permanent sequelae: chronic adamantium toxicity load buffered only by the healing factor (when factor is suppressed — Magneto's extraction, *X-Men* #25, or Genesis' re-bonding — toxicity rapidly degrades him); the skeleton is the instrument of his torture worn internally for life. Every claw extrusion lacerates the dorsal hand — pain is the *prerequisite* of his primary defensive act.

**Injury history.** Functionally uncountable: gunshot, blade, immolation, decapitation-adjacent, vivisection (*Wolverine* vol. 2 #145, by Apocalypse), skeletal extraction (Magneto), drowning in adamantium. The healing factor's *psychological* significance is decisive: he has been conditioned, over a century, that *no injury is final* — which trains a recklessness toward his own body that in any non-mutant would constitute parasuicidal behavior.

**Substance use.** Sustained heavy ethanol consumption; tobacco (cigars) continuously. The healing factor metabolizes both rapidly, requiring intake well beyond normative thresholds to achieve effect. Functionally: chronic self-medication in pursuit of an anxiolysis his physiology denies him. Behavioral marker of affect-regulation failure, not recreation.

**Sensory burden.** The hyperacute senses constitute an unremitting stimulus load — he smells fear, arousal, illness, lies. This is medically relevant: it precludes the normal social filtration that buffers most humans from interpersonal stimuli. Hypervigilance in him is partly *imposed by anatomy*.

**Reproductive/familial.** At least one biological child (Daken); the X-23 line is genetic-derivative. Itsu's murder while pregnant is encoded somatically as another iteration of the Rose pattern.

## 3. PSYCHOLOGICAL

**Attachment.** Disorganized attachment (Main/Solomon framework), reverse-engineered from: (a) the originating Howlett scene in which the father-figure who raises him is revealed as not his biological father, and his biological father is the man who kills the social father — i.e., the very category of "father" collapses in a single traumatic instant; (b) lifelong oscillation between fierce protective approach and explosive withdrawal in his closest bonds (Jean, Mariko, Kitty, Jean again); (c) the stable hostile-pair-bond with Sabretooth, classic of disorganized attachment, where the predator is the only reliably present figure.

**Defenses (mature → primitive, situational).** In low-arousal states he uses humor, stoicism, and sublimation (Bushidō practice, mentorship) — relatively mature. Under threat he regresses rapidly: *dissociation* (the berserker state — affect, behavior, and memory all split off), *splitting* (people are protected innocents or legitimate kills, with little middle), and *acting out* (violence as discharge). The presence of mature defenses in calm states is what distinguishes him from a pure antisocial profile.

**Affect regulation.** Failed at the high end. He has no functioning brake between irritation and lethal rage once a threshold is crossed; the threshold is set by olfactory/threat cues he cannot voluntarily filter. The alcohol use is the symptomatic correlate. The Japan/Bushidō practice is the *attempted* prosthesis — meditation, formal etiquette, kata as imposed structure where internal regulation has failed.

**Cognitive style.** Concrete, tactically brilliant, strategically narrow. Excellent threat-assessment and pattern recognition (the senses + a century of combat). Impaired autobiographical memory: he cannot trust his own narrative, and behaves accordingly — he treats memory as evidence to be cross-examined rather than as identity. This is *adaptive* given his history but produces a chronic identity-confusion state.

**Self-concept.** Bifurcated. Conscious self-image: "the animal," "the best there is at what I do, and what I do isn't very nice" — a *self-stigmatizing* identity that pre-rejects intimacy ("I'm not fit"). Underneath, behaviorally evidenced: a strong moral conscience (the Bushidō ideal, the protection of the young, the years of refusal in *Old Man Logan*), which the conscious self systematically denies in order to maintain low expectations of itself and others. The animal-mask is a defense *against* the disappointment of the samurai-ideal.

**Compensatory mechanisms.** *Positive:* mentorship of adolescent girls (Kitty, Jubilee, Rogue, Armor, X-23) — reparative parenting of the child-self who lost Rose and was made into Weapon X. Teaching them survival skills he wishes had been taught to him. *Negative:* alcohol, geographic flight, sexual transience, and the periodic "I work alone" exile cycles. Both clusters serve the same function — managing an internal state he cannot otherwise tolerate.

## 4. PSYCHIATRIC

Working diagnoses (derived, in DSM-5-TR / ICD-11 analog terms):

**Primary: Complex PTSD (ICD-11 6B41).** Justified by the trifecta: (i) prolonged repeated trauma (Weapon X, Team X memory implants, Hand brainwashing, Romulus conditioning, Apocalypse "Death" overwrite — decades of agentic violation, not a single incident); (ii) the three CPTSD-specific disturbances: affective dysregulation (berserker state, alcohol use), negative self-concept ("the animal," self-stigma), and interpersonal disturbance (the approach-withdrawal cycle with every intimate). Plus the core PTSD criteria: intrusive memory (now complicated by *implanted* memory), avoidance (wilderness exile periods), hyperarousal (anatomically reinforced by hypersenses).

**Co-primary: Dissociative Disorder, with mixed amnestic and identity-alteration features** (DSM-5 Dissociative Amnesia 300.12 + features of Other Specified Dissociative Disorder; ICD-11 6B61/6B64). Citing Şar (2014, 2022): dissociative amnesia, depersonalization, derealization, identity confusion and identity alteration sit on a single severity spectrum produced by chronic developmental/traumatic stress, with the highest childhood-trauma frequency of any psychiatric condition. Logan's presentation maps onto that spectrum at high severity:
- *Dissociative amnesia:* documented across the entire canon — multi-decade gaps, inability to distinguish real from implanted autobiography.
- *Identity confusion / alteration:* the "James Howlett / Logan / Patch / Weapon X / Death" sequence is not aliases-for-cover; each is a behaviorally distinct mode he enters and partially loses access to.
- *Acute dissociative reaction:* the berserker state — circumscribed episodes of altered consciousness, motoric automatism, anterograde amnesia for the episode, classic acute dissociative presentation, not Intermittent Explosive Disorder.

The Şar framework is the right lens specifically because it refuses to silo "rage" from "amnesia" from "identity alteration" — in Logan they are one syndrome with one etiology (chronic violation), not three comorbidities.

**Secondary: Alcohol Use Disorder, moderate-to-severe by behavioral criteria** (tolerance, continued use despite consequences, use to manage affect). Tolerance is anatomically inflated, so quantity criteria misapply; functional criteria are met.

**Differential — what he is NOT, and why this matters:**
- *Not Antisocial Personality Disorder.* He has demonstrable empathy, durable loyalty, internalized moral code (Bushidō), and reparative behavior toward the vulnerable. ASPD requires the absence of these. Sabretooth is the ASPD control case in the same canon — the contrast is diagnostic.
- *Not Dissociative Identity Disorder (proper).* The alters are not autonomous personalities with elaborated separate biographies; they are state-dependent behavioral modes with porous boundaries. He sits at OSDD / high-end identity-alteration on the Şar spectrum, not full DID.
- *Not primary Intermittent Explosive Disorder.* IED rules out episodes better explained by another disorder; the berserker episodes are better explained as acute dissociative reactions within his CPTSD/dissociative syndrome.
- *Not Major Depressive Disorder as primary,* though the *Old Man Logan* presentation meets criteria episodically — there it is a trauma-driven shutdown, not endogenous depression.
- *Not Bipolar.* No discrete hypomanic/manic episodes; the affective oscillations are trauma-cued, not cyclic.

**Etiology.** Childhood: emotionally cold family, somatic fragility, sudden catastrophic violence, fatal accidental harm to the first love object (Rose). Adolescence/young adulthood: feral living, then a century of combat. Adulthood: serial agentic violations (Weapon X, Team X memory implants, Hand, Hydra, Romulus, Apocalypse) — the etiological signature for dissociative spectrum disorders per Şar is *exactly* present here, at perhaps the maximal dose imaginable.

## 5. PERSONALITY — Synthesis

**Core drives** (in operating priority): (1) *Do not become the weapon they made.* (2) *Protect the young, especially young women, from becoming what was done to me.* (3) *Find out who I actually am* — held in chronic suspense because the data are corrupted. (4) *Discharge the unbearable* — through violence when it has a target, alcohol when it does not, geography when neither will serve.

**Worldview / operational code.** The world contains predators, prey, and the few who choose to stand between them. He has been all three and trusts only the third role. Civilization is a thin courtesy he respects out of discipline, not faith. Authority is presumptively a handler; institutions presumptively want to use him. Loyalty is owed to *persons* (Xavier, Jean, Kitty, Kurt, Mariko) and to *codes* (Bushidō, the X-Men's better self), never to organizations. Death is not a deterrent to him and he knows it is not, which produces both his tactical fearlessness and a low-grade lifelong wish for an ending that will stick.

**Interpersonal pattern.** With peers: gruff, loyal, brutally honest, capable of deep friendship (Kurt, Ororo, Rogers). With authority: insubordinate by default, compliant only under earned trust. With romantic objects: idealizing then withdrawing, because intimacy activates the Rose/Itsu/Mariko template — the prediction *she will die because of me* is a load-bearing belief, and avoidance is its logical behavioral consequence. With adolescents under his protection: his most regulated, generative, and durable mode — the only zone in which his attachment system functions cleanly. With enemies: ritualized; he prefers a known adversary to an unknown ally.

**Stress response and escalation ladder.** (1) Sensory cue (smell of blood/fear/familiar enemy) → hyperarousal. (2) Cognitive narrowing, sardonic verbal warnings. (3) Claw extrusion as warning display. (4) Engaged combat, controlled. (5) Threshold breach — typically harm to a protected person, or smell of a specific enemy (Sabretooth, Hand, Weapon X personnel) — triggers berserker dissociation: consciousness narrows or splits, behavior becomes automatized, amnesia for the episode follows. (6) Post-episode: shame, withdrawal, alcohol, sometimes geographic flight. The ladder is the same across writers and decades.

**Pressure points (the file's operational section).** 
- *Children in danger* will override every other consideration including his own survival and tactical judgment — exploitable, and has been exploited (Hand, Mystique, Mojo).
- *Implanted memory or compulsion* destabilizes him faster than physical injury; an adversary who can credibly claim "your memories aren't yours" lands a blow that the healing factor cannot close.
- *The Rose/Mariko/Itsu pattern* — the woman he loves placed in a position where he must kill her or watch her die — is the canonical psychological kill-shot and recurs because it works.
- *Sabretooth's continued existence* is a chronic destabilizer he has organized his identity around; paradoxically, Creed's permanent removal might be more destabilizing than his presence.
- *Sobriety, stillness, and unstructured time* are aversive — he decompensates in peace faster than in war, which is why Krakoan-era institutional roles and pre-Krakoan teaching posts are self-prescribed structure.
- *The samurai ideal* is both his highest functioning and his deepest shame ledger; invoking it can recruit him to causes against his interest, and confronting him with his failures against it can disable him.

The integrated read: Logan is not a berserker with a soft side, and not a noble warrior with an anger problem. He is a chronically dissociative trauma survivor whose physiology made him unkillable before his psychology was finished being formed, and who has spent a hundred and thirty years building — out of Bushidō, mentorship, alcohol, and exile — a workable container for a self that was repeatedly broken open by people who wanted a weapon. The container holds. It was not designed to, and he knows it. That knowledge is the substrate of everything else.
