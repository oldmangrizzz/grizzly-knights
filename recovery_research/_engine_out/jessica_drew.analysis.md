# UATU ANALYTICAL WORKUP: JESSICA DREW

**Subject designation:** Spider-Woman / Jessica Drew
**File basis:** Full publication record, *Marvel Spotlight* #32 (1977) through current.

---

## 1. HISTORY — The Behavioral Record

**Origin stratum (Marv Wolfman / Marie Severin / Archie Goodwin, 1977–1979).** Subject is the daughter of Jonathan and Merriem Drew, geneticist parents working on Wundagore Mountain — a site historically saturated with anomalous activity (the High Evolutionary's New Men, Chthon's mystical residue). Childhood account is incoherent across writers by design: the foundational story (*Marvel Spotlight* #32) presents her as dying of uranium poisoning and saved by her father's experimental spider-serum, then placed in a slow-aging genetic accelerator for decades; later retconned (Bendis, *Spider-Woman: Origin*, 2005) to in-utero genetic manipulation. What is *behaviorally* consistent across both versions: Jessica grows up developmentally isolated, with no peer cohort, no normative socialization, raised by absent/dead parents and surrogate caretakers (Bova, the cow-woman of the High Evolutionary). She emerges into the world already an adult, already weaponized, already strange to herself.

**HYDRA conscription (Wolfman run, 1977–78).** Recruited and indoctrinated by Otto Vermis under the fiction that her parents were murdered by S.H.I.E.L.D. She kills (or believes she kills) Jerry Hunt before the brainwashing collapses. This is the load-bearing trauma for everything afterward: she was a weapon for fascists before she had a self.

**Early solo era (Mark Gruenwald / Chris Claremont, *Spider-Woman* #1–50, 1978–83).** Relocates to Los Angeles. Works as bounty hunter, private investigator. Pattern: serial occupational reinvention, perpetual outsider status, runs hot and cold with would-be allies (Lindsay McCabe is the rare durable bond). Her pheromonal powers cause her to *repel* most people and *enthrall* others involuntarily — she is, literally and behaviorally, someone who cannot have normal proximity to other humans. Subject's "death" and resurrection at Magus's hands (*Spider-Woman* #50) leaves her depowered and depressed for years.

**Wilderness period (mid-80s through 90s).** Largely sidelined. Supporting player. Loses powers, regains fragments, drifts. Functionally: a long depressive trough at the publishing level that mirrors the character's interior arc.

**Bendis era — the defining modern stratum (2004–2015).** *New Avengers* reveals subject was replaced by Skrull queen Veranke for an indeterminate but extended period (covered in *Secret Invasion*, 2008); the woman the Avengers trusted was never her. When the real Jessica is recovered from a Skrull ship, she is unmoored — her professional identity, romantic history (the Hawkeye flirtation, the Avengers placements), and even her recent agency belong to an impostor. *Spider-Woman: Agent of S.W.O.R.D.* (Bendis/Maleev, 2009) shows her hunting Skrulls compulsively across the globe, affect flat, narration saturated with paranoia, sleeplessness, and self-loathing. This is textbook post-captivity trauma rendered as ongoing mission.

**Hopeless / Rodriguez run (2015–2017).** Subject becomes a mother (son Gerry, father Roger Carpenter). Pregnancy is concealed under costume, then disclosed. She continues fieldwork while pregnant — a behavioral tell: cannot stop working, cannot accept dependency, refuses to let motherhood erase the operator identity. Adopts the leather-jacket-and-yoga-pants costume, a deliberate de-glamorization. Functions as single mother + freelance hero + investigator, often broke, often exhausted, consistently competent.

**Recent eras (Pacheco, Foxe, *King in Black*, current).** Continued status as Avenger, mother, friend (durable bonds: Carol Danvers above all, Clint Barton, Natasha Romanoff). Recurring near-death events; recurring possession/manipulation events (Octavia Vermis, etc.). Pattern: the universe keeps trying to take her body and identity from her, and she keeps reclaiming both with grim humor.

**Evolutionary arc:** From manufactured weapon → cult defector → isolated drifter → ghost (during Skrull captivity) → reconstructed adult who, finally, in motherhood and the Carol friendship, locates something like chosen belonging. The trajectory is *late individuation* — she had to build a self from scratch in her thirties.

## 2. MEDICAL

**Genetic substrate.** Body chemistry is not human-standard. Either spider-serum + radiation exposure (classic) or in-utero genetic engineering (modern) — operationally identical outcome: enhanced strength (low superhuman, ~7 ton class on good days), durability, accelerated healing, immunity to most toxins/radiation/disease, bio-electric "venom blast" discharge from hands, and — most clinically significant — continuous pheromone emission affecting human limbic response (attraction in men, hostility/distrust in women, variable across writers but consistent in *involuntariness*).

**Pheromone production is a chronic somatic condition with profound psychiatric sequelae.** Subject cannot turn it off reliably. Every social interaction is chemically contaminated. She can never know if affection directed at her is real or pheromonally induced; she can never know if hostility is earned or chemical. This is a lifelong condition of consent-corruption. Compare clinically to disorders that distort interpersonal feedback (severe facial difference, certain endocrine conditions) — the psychological burden is similar: the subject distrusts all relational data.

**Injury history.** Heavy. "Death" at Magus's hands. Multiple severe combat injuries. Pregnancy and childbirth, with associated physiological changes she works through without significant downtime. Repeated exposure to mind-control, possession, and bodily hijack (HYDRA conditioning, Skrull replacement involves her being held in a tank for an extended period, later Octavia Vermis's neural manipulation). The Skrull captivity in particular is a prolonged immobilization / sensory deprivation / loss-of-bodily-autonomy event of indeterminate duration.

**Substance use.** Smokes (intermittently, era-dependent). Drinks. Not depicted as dependent, but uses both as affect regulators — a tell about baseline anxiety load. Sleeps poorly; insomnia explicitly narrated in the Bendis run.

**Power-loss episodes.** Multiple. Each one psychologically destabilizing — the body is the only continuous source of identity she has trusted, and it has betrayed her by switching off.

## 3. PSYCHOLOGICAL

**Attachment.** Disorganized-leaning-toward-avoidant. Primary caregivers were absent (parents dead/working), non-human (Bova), or actively malignant (Vermis, HYDRA). She had no consistent attuned figure in formative years. Adult attachments are slow to form, fiercely guarded once formed (McCabe, Carol Danvers, eventually Clint), but she defaults to *withdrawal* under stress, not approach. The Carol Danvers friendship is clinically the healthiest object in her file — long, reciprocal, survives ruptures — and it is not coincidence that Carol is one of the only people whose response to Jessica cannot plausibly be pheromone-confounded (Carol is too powerful, too oriented, too distinct).

**Defenses (mature and immature, mixed).** Heavy use of *humor* (dry, sardonic, self-deprecating — her internal monologue under Bendis/Hopkins is one long defensive joke), *intellectualization* (she narrates her own life like a case file), *isolation of affect* (combat decisions made with eerie calm), and *counterphobic action* (the response to fear is to walk toward it — see her Skrull-hunting after Secret Invasion). Immature defenses surface in depressive episodes: *withdrawal*, *somatization* (insomnia, smoking, drinking), and an *identification with the aggressor* echo from her HYDRA period that she has spent her career repudiating.

**Affect regulation.** Functional under fire, brittle in stillness. She is regulated by *mission* — give her a target, she becomes coherent; remove the target, she fragments. This is the operator-stabilization pattern seen in long-tenure combat personnel.

**Cognitive style.** Concrete-operational under threat, ruminative-abstract at rest. High vigilance. Strong pattern-recognition. Skeptical to the point of cynicism — she assumes deception as a baseline because deception is her empirical experience (HYDRA lied to her about her parents; the world experienced her impostor as her; her own body chemistry distorts every interaction).

**Self-concept.** Profoundly uncertain. The recurring narrative tic — "I don't even know who I am" — is not melodrama; it is a structurally accurate description of someone whose origin, agency, and recent biography have all been hijacked at different points. Motherhood has stabilized this: *being Gerry's mother* is a fact no one can retcon, take, or pheromonally distort.

**Compensatory mechanisms.** Positive: chosen sisterhood (Carol), professional competence, the practical ethic of "show up and do the work." Negative: workaholism that flirts with parental neglect, self-isolation framed as independence, refusal to ask for help until late in the crisis.

## 4. PSYCHIATRIC

Working impressions, derived behaviorally:

**Complex PTSD (ICD-11 6B41) — primary.** Justified by: (a) prolonged interpersonal trauma in formative period (HYDRA conditioning), (b) repeated captivity/identity-loss events (Skrull replacement, multiple possessions), (c) the classic triad of *re-experiencing* (the Skrull period haunts her narration for years), *avoidance/numbing* (flat affect in Bendis's *Agent of S.W.O.R.D.*), and *hyperarousal* (insomnia, vigilance, startle response), plus the c-PTSD-specific features of *disturbed self-concept*, *affective dysregulation*, and *interpersonal disturbance* (chronic difficulty trusting). This is the load-bearing diagnosis.

**Major depressive episodes, recurrent.** Documented across the depowered post-*Spider-Woman* #50 period and again post-*Secret Invasion*. Anhedonia, social withdrawal, sleep disturbance, self-devaluation. Not chronic dysthymia — she has functional intervals — but episodic and trauma-triggered.

**Identity disturbance** sub-clinical to DID-spectrum. Does *not* meet DID criteria (no discrete alters), but the *phenomenology of identity rupture* is unusually severe due to repeated externally-imposed identity events (brainwashing, Skrull replacement, possession). Best described as *trauma-related identity disturbance secondary to c-PTSD*, not as a dissociative disorder proper.

**Differential — what she is NOT:**

- *Not* Borderline Personality Disorder, despite surface markers (identity disturbance, intense relationships, affective instability). She lacks the abandonment terror, the splitting, the impulsive self-destructive pattern, and the relational chaos. Her instability is *event-driven*, not characterological.
- *Not* Antisocial. The HYDRA period was coerced; her post-deprogramming life shows consistent prosocial values, guilt, reparative behavior.
- *Not* Schizoid, despite preference for solitude. She *wants* connection; she has learned it is dangerous and contaminated. Schizoid lacks the want.
- *Not* primary substance use disorder. Use is symptomatic, not central.

**Etiological summary.** Subject presents as a case of severe early-life institutional/cultic abuse compounded by repeated adult traumas of bodily and identity violation, in a person with high baseline ego strength and intelligence — which is why she functions as well as she does. The presentation is *trauma sequelae in a structurally intact personality*, not a personality disorder.

## 5. PERSONALITY — Synthesis

**Core drives.** (1) *Authorship of her own life* — after a childhood she didn't choose, conditioning she didn't consent to, and a captivity that erased years, the deepest hunger is to *be the one deciding*. (2) *Verification of the real* — she needs to know what is actually happening, who is actually who, what is actually felt; hence the investigator vocation, the skepticism, the close attention. (3) *Earned belonging* — she will not coast on charm or pheromone; relationships have to be tested and survive.

**Worldview / operational code.** Empirical, mistrustful, ethically stubborn. People lie; institutions lie more; bodies (including her own) can be hijacked; the only reliable instrument is showing up and watching carefully. Heroism is not glory — it is the job of cleaning up what predators do, because she knows from the inside what being prey is. She has zero patience for ideology and considerable patience for individual people in trouble.

**Interpersonal pattern.** Slow-warming, dry, deflective on first contact; loyal past reason once in. Tends to underplay her own competence verbally while overperforming it operationally. Reads other people's deceptions quickly and her own emotions slowly. Prefers peer relationships with other operators (Carol, Clint, Nat) over civilian intimacy — peers don't need protecting and can survive her pheromones with awareness.

**Stress response / escalation.** Stage 1: increased dry humor, increased work output. Stage 2: withdrawal from non-mission contacts, insomnia, smoking/drinking uptick. Stage 3: counterphobic charge — picks the hardest target and goes alone. Stage 4 (rare, post-major-violation): depressive collapse, dissociative numbness, requires external intervention (historically Carol) to recover. She does not rage outward; she internalizes and burns.

**Pressure points.** (1) Threats to or through Gerry — the one place she will abandon all tactical discipline. (2) Loss of bodily autonomy / mind-control / possession scenarios — re-traumatizing in a way she cannot mask. (3) Discovery that a relationship she trusted was a deception — strikes at the Skrull/HYDRA wound directly. (4) Being told who she "really" is by someone else — she will reject the framing even if accurate, because the authorship is the point.

**Strengths the file should not understate.** She is funny. She is unusually honest about her own limits once she trusts you. Her ethical core, hard-built from a corrupted start, is more reliable than that of heroes who never had to construct one. And she has, against considerable odds, built a life — a son, a friend, a job, a self — out of materials that were handed to her broken.
