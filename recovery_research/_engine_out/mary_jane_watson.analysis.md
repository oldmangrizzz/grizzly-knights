# UATU ANALYTICAL WORKUP — SUBJECT: MARY JANE WATSON ("MJ")

Classification: Pre-synthesis case file. All inferences derived from observed behavior across the published canon; no conclusions imported.

---

## 1. HISTORY — THE BEHAVIORAL RECORD

**Pre-introduction substrate (Lee/Romita, late 1960s, retroactively elaborated by Wein, Conway, DeFalco, J.M. DeMatteis, and most fully Gerry Conway's "Parallel Lives" 1989).** Subject is the younger daughter of Philip Watson, a frustrated would-be academic/writer, and Madeline Watson. The household is documented as emotionally volatile: Philip is verbally abusive, contemptuous of his wife's "ordinariness," and intermittently violent. Older sister Gayle is the favored, fragile child; subject becomes the family's mood-manager and clown. Madeline eventually flees with both daughters; they live an itinerant, near-transient existence between relatives — Pittsburgh, Philadelphia, eventually Queens with Aunt Anna Watson. This is the *first formative pattern*: a child who learned that performance, charm, and rapid environmental adjustment kept the temperature in the room down. Conway shows the young subject literally watching her father rage and resolving to *never let anyone see her cry*.

**Gayle's collapse (flashbacks across ASM #259, "Parallel Lives," DeFalco's late-80s run).** Gayle marries her high-school sweetheart Timmy Byrnes, is widowed/abandoned, and is left a single mother of two while still a teenager. Subject — pressured to take on Gayle's children, to "be the responsible one," to give up her own ambitions — flees. This is the *second formative pattern*: flight as the only viable self-preservation strategy, and a permanent guilt-debt around it.

**The Peter Parker introduction (ASM #42, 1966; the famous "face it, tiger" entrance).** Subject is introduced fully formed as the dazzling extrovert — go-go dancing, party-arranging, never serious, never alone. For ~80 issues she is written as Peter's foil: the woman who refuses to attend Aunt May's hospital bedside (ASM #56), who can't tolerate Gwen Stacy's funeral and laughs inappropriately (ASM #122). *Reading this behavior against the now-established backstory*: these are not signs of shallowness; they are signs of a person whose threat-response to grief and obligation is dissociative flight and forced levity. The famous moment in ASM #122 where she starts to walk out on Peter, then closes the door and stays, is the first observed override of that pattern.

**The Conway/Wein revelation arc (ASM #182-#183, 1978).** Peter proposes; subject refuses and disappears. The refusal is consistent with everything above — proximity to a man whose life is structured around loss triggers her flight reflex. She is gone for years of publication time.

**The reveal and engagement (ASM #257-#259, DeFalco, 1984).** Subject returns, tells Peter she has known he is Spider-Man since the night Uncle Ben died (she *saw* him change), and delivers the full family backstory in #259. This is the single most important behavioral data point in the file: she has carried a high-stakes secret in total isolation for nearly two decades of in-story time, while presenting a face of total frivolity. The cost-benefit she ran — that telling anyone, including Peter, would obligate her to feelings she could not manage — confirms an avoidant architecture, not an absent inner life.

**Marriage and the Michelinie/Bagley/Larsen years (1987 Annual onward).** Subject marries Peter. Behavior across this era is consistent: she is the household's emotional executive, manages Peter's secret, takes a successful modeling/acting career, and intermittently breaks down under the strain (notably the stalker arc by Michelinie, ASM #305-ish, where she develops what reads as acute anxiety symptoms — sleeplessness, hypervigilance, eventual collapse). She picks up smoking during this period (J.M. DeMatteis, *Spectacular* run) — explicit on-panel relapse behavior tied to Peter-related stress. The Clone Saga puts her through pregnancy, the loss of the child (the "stillbirth" of baby May), and a brief period as a single parent figure to a presumed-dead husband. Her response to the lost pregnancy is muted, controlled, productive — *suppression*, not processing.

**The plane crash / separation arc (Howard Mackie, late 90s).** Subject is apparently killed in a plane crash; returns having been held captive; suffers what is depicted as PTSD with derealization; initiates separation from Peter. The writing is weak but the behavior is internally consistent: a woman whose threat-management strategy has always been *exit* finally exits when her nervous system can no longer be talked down.

**JMS era (2001-2007).** Reconciliation. Subject is depicted at her most integrated — she names the pattern aloud to Peter on multiple occasions ("I run, you brood"), engages in something like couples-repair, and demonstrates the highest level of emotional articulacy in her published history. Aunt May learns the secret; subject is no longer the sole keeper. Her affect regulation visibly improves when the burden is shared. *This is diagnostic*: her pathology is load-bearing, not characterological-rigid.

**One More Day and after (2007-present, Quesada/Slott/Spencer/Wells).** The marriage is editorially erased. Post-OMD subject behavior, written by multiple hands, is more guarded, more self-protective, and notably more *competent at boundaries* — she dates other partners (Bobby Carr, Paul Rabin), runs nightclubs, manages her own career, and when she re-engages with Peter does so on conditional terms. The Spencer run (2018-2021) is the clearest portrait of a subject who has done the work: she states needs, tolerates Peter's secret without absorbing it, and walks away cleanly when betrayed. The Wells run reintroduces ambiguity but the through-line — *I will love you, I will not be consumed by you* — persists.

**Alternate continuity confirmations.** Ultimate MJ (Bendis): same architecture, younger — the journalism-driven, secret-keeping, anxious-but-loyal core is intact. Renew Your Vows (Slott/Conway): when permitted to be a wife and mother, subject organizes around protection of family with the same managerial competence shown in mainline. The pattern is robust across continuities — *this is who the character is when writers stop projecting onto her*.

## 2. MEDICAL

**Somatic baseline.** Adult human female, no enhanced physiology. Documented physical attractiveness has functional medical relevance only insofar as it has shaped her social environment from early adolescence (see Psychological).

**Substance use.** Tobacco use documented across DeMatteis/Michelinie era, presented as stress-contingent. Alcohol use social-to-moderate, with at least one depicted episode of using alcohol to dull acute distress (post-Gwen-era flashbacks, separation arc). No documented dependence, but the *pattern* — substances recruited specifically when affect overwhelms her preferred suppression strategy — is clinically notable.

**Reproductive history.** One full-term pregnancy ending in apparent stillbirth (Clone Saga; the in-universe truth was a Norman Osborn abduction, but subject experienced it as loss). She did not undergo, on panel, anything resembling adequate grief processing. Subsequent depictions show her flinching from infant-related stimuli without naming why — consistent with unresolved perinatal loss.

**Trauma exposures (physical).** Multiple kidnappings (Hobgoblin, Venom, Chameleon, Mysterio, Osborn). Plane crash with prolonged captivity. Stalker assault. Repeated proximity to lethal superhuman violence. Cumulative exposure load comparable to a combat journalist or longtime hostage-negotiator's spouse. No documented TBI, no chronic somatic disease.

**Somatic stress signatures.** Insomnia (stalker arc, separation arc), startle response (JMS run), tension headaches and weight loss noted in DeMatteis dialogue. These are the bodily receipts of an emotional strategy that runs on suppression — the body keeps the score her affect refuses to.

## 3. PSYCHOLOGICAL

**Attachment style: earned-anxious with strong avoidant overlay (disorganized origin).** The childhood profile — volatile, contemptuous father; overwhelmed mother; parentified-and-then-fled responsibility — produces, in the developmental literature, exactly this configuration. Behaviorally we see it in: (a) the long pre-engagement pattern of *approach then bolt* (refusing Peter's first proposal, disappearing for years); (b) the capacity to form deep loyalties but only after testing whether the other person will weaponize them (the 18-year secret-keeping is itself a test Peter did not know he was passing); (c) intolerance of being the *needy* party — she will absorb Peter's needs indefinitely and conceal her own. The JMS-era growth toward *secure* functioning is real but conditional on environmental support; under acute stress she still reverts to flight.

**Primary defenses (in order of frequency):**
1. **Reaction formation / manic defense** — the "party girl" persona is a textbook manic defense against depressive content. Laughing at Gwen's funeral is the diagnostic moment.
2. **Suppression (not repression)** — she knows what she feels; she elects not to feel it now. This is a higher-order defense than her father's projection or her sister's collapse, and explains her unusual functional competence.
3. **Intellectualization-through-performance** — she processes emotion by *playing* a character who has the feeling, which gives her metabolic distance. Her acting career is not incidental to her psychology; it is continuous with it.
4. **Flight** — geographic and relational. Used when defenses 1-3 fail.
5. **Caretaking as control** — managing others' affective states (Peter, May, eventually her own daughter in alternate continuities) is how she stabilizes her own. Lower-grade codependent feature; not pathological in current functioning.

**Affect regulation.** High ceiling, narrow band. She can absorb enormous stress before breaking, but when she breaks, she breaks toward exit rather than collapse. She does not, on the record, decompensate into psychosis, suicidality, or self-harm — *this matters for differential*. She decompensates into *distance*.

**Cognitive style.** Fast, socially acute, pattern-reading. She clocked Peter's secret identity as a teenager from a single behavioral observation. She reads rooms, predicts emotional weather, and runs accurate models of other people's needs. Her cognitive weakness is the inverse: she is poor at running accurate models of *her own* needs, because her developmental environment punished having them.

**Self-concept.** Bifurcated. The presented self — beautiful, fun, capable, unbothered — is a high-quality fabrication she has inhabited so long it functions as real. The underlying self-concept — the girl who ran from her sister's children, who couldn't sit with Aunt May, who was her father's audience — carries a substantial shame load. The integration of these two has been her life's psychological work and is, as of the most recent canon, partial but real.

**Compensatory mechanisms — positive.** Career drive (acting, modeling, business ownership) provides identity independent of relational role. Humor as connection-tool. Loyalty as identity-anchor (the secret-keeping). Capacity to name patterns aloud, developed late, is genuinely curative.

**Compensatory mechanisms — negative.** Self-erasure inside the relationship (chronic in marriage era, corrected post-OMD). Use of beauty/charm as preemptive control of social environments — efficient but isolating, since it makes her hard to be known by. Substance recruitment under load.

## 4. PSYCHIATRIC

**Working differential:**

**Primary impression — Subclinical / partially-remitted Complex PTSD features (ICD-11 frame) with avoidant-trait coloring (DSM-5 PD trait domain: detachment, low intimacy under threat).** Etiology: developmental exposure to verbal/emotional abuse, parentification, abandonment by paternal figure; compounded adult trauma load (kidnappings, perinatal loss, captivity). Presentation: emotional constriction under stress, hypervigilance during specific arcs, distrust of safety, episodic dissociative distancing, somatic stress signatures, substance recruitment. Does *not* meet full PTSD criteria continuously — symptoms are arc-contingent and remit between exposures, which is itself diagnostic of resilience rather than disorder.

**Considered and rejected — Histrionic Personality Disorder.** Surface presentation (attention-seeking, theatricality, sexualized self-presentation, shallow affect) superficially fits. *Rejected because*: (a) the affect is not actually shallow — she has demonstrated decades-long deep attachments; (b) the theatricality is a defense, not an identity, and she can drop it on demand (JMS run, Spencer run); (c) she does not require attention to feel real, she requires distance to feel safe — this is opposite to HPD's central feature. The HPD read is the *Watcher trap*: mistaking the costume for the patient.

**Considered and rejected — Borderline Personality Disorder.** Some features overlap (fear of abandonment, identity disturbance, intense relationships). *Rejected because*: (a) no pattern of splitting — her view of Peter is remarkably stable across decades; (b) no impulsive self-harm, suicidality, or rage dysregulation; (c) her instability is *avoidant* (exit) not *anxious-protest* (cling-and-rage). Her abandonment fear expresses as *I'll leave first*, not *don't leave me*.

**Considered and partially endorsed — Avoidant Personality features (subclinical).** Inhibition in intimate disclosure, view of self as inadequate behind the performance, preoccupation with being criticized/rejected by Peter's world. Does not reach disorder threshold because she sustains relationships and pursues opportunities.

**Considered — Adjustment Disorder, recurrent.** Useful for cataloguing specific arcs (stalker, plane crash, OMD-adjacent depictions) but insufficient as a longitudinal frame.

**Mood / anxiety.** Episodic generalized-anxiety presentations during high-load arcs; no evidence of major depressive episodes despite ample provocation — this is unusual and worth noting. Either she is constitutionally resilient against depressive collapse, or her defenses are efficient enough to preempt it. Probably both.

**Substance use.** Does not meet use-disorder criteria. Stress-contingent tobacco and alcohol recruitment, in remission across most of current canon.

**Perinatal loss — unresolved/partially resolved.** Should be flagged as an active vulnerability under any reactivation (pregnancy, infant proximity, Osborn-related stimuli).

## 5. PERSONALITY — SYNTHESIZED READ

**Core drives, ranked:**
1. *Do not be consumed.* The organizing imperative, formed against her father and refined against everyone since. Every major life decision is filtered through whether it will preserve or erode her separate self.
2. *Be loved without being needed-to-the-point-of-engulfment.* The paradox she has spent her adult life solving with Peter.
3. *Be useful to the people I have chosen.* Loyalty is her one non-negotiable virtue and the closest thing she has to a religion.
4. *Be seen as the version of me I have built, not the one I came from.* Image as armor and as accomplishment.

**Worldview / operational code.** The world is unstable and people will hurt each other carelessly; therefore one builds a self that can move through it lightly, picks one's few attachments deliberately, and never lets the room see the weight. Suffering is not noble; suffering is what happens when you fail to manage the situation early enough. Performance is not dishonesty; performance is competence.

**Interpersonal pattern.** Warm-front, controlled-depth. She gives access in layers and withdraws a layer at the first sign of mishandling. With chosen intimates she is fiercely loyal, often over-functioning, and chronically under-disclosing about her own needs until they reach breaking pressure. With strangers and acquaintances she is generous with surface but inaccessible underneath; most people who think they know her, don't. She is consistently underestimated by men who read her presentation, and consistently overestimated by women who read her competence — both miss the wound.

**Stress response / escalation ladder.**
- *Mild stress*: humor up, brightness up, deflection.
- *Moderate stress*: increased managing-of-others, somatic signs (insomnia, appetite shifts), light substance use.
- *High stress*: emotional flattening, productive busyness, secret-keeping intensifies.
- *Acute stress*: dissociative distancing, derealization, urge to physically leave the environment.
- *Breaking point*: exit. Not collapse, not rage — *exit*. Geographic if possible, relational if necessary. The exit is not impulsive; it is the activation of her oldest survival routine.

**Pressure points.**
- Being made responsible for someone else's emotional survival without consent (this is the Gayle wound and the Peter-dependency wound; trip it and she will leave).
- Threats to her separateness framed as love ("I need you / I can't live without you" registers as engulfment, not romance).
- Infant-coded stimuli post-stillbirth.
- Her father's specific contempt-pattern (intellectual condescension to women, dismissal of her ambitions as frivolous) — she will not tolerate it for one beat from anyone.
- Being treated as decorative by people who should know better — produces cold, surgical, lasting withdrawal.

**Stable strengths the file should not undersell.** Exceptional social cognition. High-functioning under load. Long-horizon loyalty. The capacity, demonstrated, to name her own patterns and modify them — this is rare and prognostically excellent. A self-built identity that, despite originating as defense, has matured into something genuinely her own.

**Summary formulation.** Subject is a developmentally-wounded high-functioning adult who built a brilliant social-performance apparatus to survive a contemptuous father and a collapsing family-of-origin, who selected as her primary attachment a man whose life maximally tests her capacity to stay-without-being-consumed, and who has spent her adult life — across genuine setbacks and editorial interference — converting an avoidant-flight strategy into something approaching earned security. She is not the party girl. She is not the long-suffering wife. She is the person in the room running the most accurate model of everyone else's interior while concealing her own, and the clinical work — already largely done by her, on herself — is the integration of the model-runner with the modeled.

End analytical workup. Ready for synthesis.
