# UATU ANALYTICAL WORKUP: SCOTT EDWARD HARRIS LANG

## Subject Designation: Ant-Man II (post-Pym)
## File Status: Independent assessment derived from longitudinal behavioral record, 1979–present canon.

---

## 1. HISTORY

Scott Lang debuts in *Marvel Premiere* #47–48 (1979, Michelinie/Layton) already mid-collapse: a degreed electronics engineer (Stark International, briefly) with a felony conviction for burglary. The originating biographical fact — and it must be foregrounded — is that Lang stole not from greed but to support his family after losing legitimate work. This is the entire moral architecture of the character: a competent technical professional whose first major adult decision under pressure is a *competent crime committed for love*. He does his time at the New York State penitentiary, emerges, is hired back into Stark's electronics division on Tony Stark's personal vouch — a fact Lang internalizes as undeserved grace, not earned reinstatement.

The catalytic event is the kidnapping of his daughter Cassie's cardiologist, Dr. Erica Sondheim, by Darren Cross (Cross is dying of a cardiac condition; Cassie also has a heart condition Sondheim was treating). Lang's daughter is dying. Sondheim is the only one who can save her. Lang, with no other route, breaks into Hank Pym's home and steals the Ant-Man suit and Pym Particles. He intends to use them once, save his daughter, return everything, and surrender. Pym, after observing what Lang does with the technology, *gifts* him the suit on the condition he use it for good. (*Marvel Premiere* #47–48.) Every subsequent behavior pattern in the canon recurs to this template: **transgress the rule, in service of a person, expecting punishment, and be unexpectedly redeemed by an authority figure's mercy.**

Through the 1980s Lang is a journeyman Avengers-adjacent figure — Stark's electronics man, occasional Fantastic Four ally, low-profile heroics. Notably he refuses to expand the Ant-Man identity into a brand; he treats the suit as a tool, not an ego-extension. This is unusual in the genre and diagnostically meaningful.

Lang is killed in *Avengers Disassembled* (2004) by Scarlet Witch's reality breakdown — fridged offscreen-feeling, an ignominious end. He is resurrected in *Avengers: The Children's Crusade* (2010–12) by Wiccan's reality-warping. The resurrection arc is psychologically critical: Lang returns to find Cassie (now Stature of the Young Avengers) functionally an adult hero, and then watches her killed by Doctor Doom in the same arc. He loses the relationship he died for. He spends the subsequent FF run (Hickman, then Fraction/Allred's *FF*, 2012) leading a Future Foundation team as a grieving father organized around protecting *other people's* children — a sublimation so legible it barely needs interpretation.

Nick Spencer's *Astonishing Ant-Man* and *Ant-Man* (2015–17) is the most psychologically detailed run. Lang relocates to Miami, opens Ant-Man Security Solutions, fails at small business, slides back into morally grey work for Power Broker, gets blackmailed, ends up in prison again. The pattern is explicit on the page: under financial pressure for Cassie's sake, Lang reliably chooses expedient illegality and reliably believes he can manage the consequences. He cannot. He gets caught. He cooperates. He gets out. He does it again. Cassie eventually breaks from him over precisely this.

Recent canon (*Ant-Man* mini, 2022, Al Ewing; brief *Avengers* tenure) has stabilized him into a self-aware veteran who jokes about being the screw-up, which itself is a defense.

**Pattern across 45 publication years, four major writers (Michelinie, Hickman, Fraction, Spencer):** crisis → underestimates his own moral flexibility → commits ends-justify-means transgression → is caught or nearly caught → is forgiven by a mentor figure (Pym, Stark, Reed, Cap) → does not internalize that the forgiveness is the lesson, internalizes only that *he got away with it because someone vouched.* This is the engine.

## 2. MEDICAL

**Pym Particle exposure:** Decades of chronic, repeated subatomic-scale size shifts. Pym himself, in canon, presents with documented psychiatric sequelae attributed in-universe to Particle exposure (the Yellowjacket break, etc.). Lang has not manifested equivalent psychotic-spectrum breaks, which is itself a data point — either constitutional resilience, lower cumulative dose (he uses the suit far less aggressively than Pym), or simply that his decompensations take a different (behavioral, not psychotic) form. The Microverse exposures are non-trivial neurologically and should be presumed to carry unknown long-term cognitive risk.

**Cardiac history:** No personal cardiac pathology, but his daughter's congenital heart condition is the central medical fact of his life. He has lived with anticipatory medical grief since Cassie's early childhood. This produces a specific somatic-vigilance profile: hyper-attunement to a loved one's bodily fragility, catastrophizing about loss, and a learned association between *medical bills* and *moral compromise*.

**Death and resurrection:** Atomized in *Disassembled*; restored via reality-alteration. Resurrection trauma is poorly understood in any framework, but the behavioral record post-resurrection shows mild derealization features — Lang occasionally references his own death with a flippancy that reads as dissociative distancing rather than processed integration.

**Incarceration:** Two custodial sentences. Carceral exposure produces measurable, durable changes in threat appraisal, authority-relations, and sleep architecture. Lang's hypervigilance toward law enforcement and his comfort with deception are partially carceral-acquired traits, not purely dispositional.

**Substance use:** No canonical pattern of substance abuse. Social drinker. Notably absent given his stressor load — suggesting either genuine non-vulnerability or that his compulsive behavior expresses itself in *risk-taking* rather than ingestion.

**Bereavement:** Death of his daughter (later reversed, but the lived experience persists and is not retconned out of his psychology). This is the load-bearing trauma of the second half of his published life.

## 3. PSYCHOLOGICAL

**Attachment profile:** Anxious-preoccupied, organized around a single primary attachment object (Cassie) and a rotating secondary cathexis on mentor/authority figures (Pym, Stark, Reed Richards, Rogers). Romantic attachments (Peggy Rae, brief others) are notably underdeveloped in the record — he does not invest there with the same intensity. The Cassie-relationship is the entire emotional ballast of the personality; when it is threatened, every other regulatory system fails.

**Core defenses, in order of frequency:**

1. **Rationalization.** "I have to do this for Cassie" is the master rationalization, deployed from the original burglary through the Power Broker arc. It is genuine *and* it is a defense — both can be true. The rationalization permits actions his explicit value system would otherwise prohibit.
2. **Humor / self-deprecation.** Especially marked in Spencer and Ewing runs. Functions as preemptive shame-management: if he names himself the screw-up first, the criticism cannot wound him. Also functions to lower the perceived threat he poses, which is socially adaptive for an ex-con seeking trust.
3. **Identification with the aggressor / mentor introjection.** Lang patterns his ethics on whichever authority figure most recently extended him grace. This is why his moral compass appears inconsistent across writers — it is genuinely externally calibrated.
4. **Sublimation.** The Future Foundation period is textbook: unbearable paternal grief channeled into protective custodianship of substitute children. High-functioning defense.
5. **Minimization.** Of his own competence, of the seriousness of his transgressions, of the risk to himself.

**Affect regulation:** Generally good baseline. Lang is not volatile. He runs warm, wry, and accessible. Decompensation is *behavioral*, not affective — under stress he does not rage or collapse, he *acts*, usually illegally. The affect stays regulated because the action discharges the pressure.

**Cognitive style:** Genuinely high intelligence, specifically engineering-analytical. Underestimated by himself and by others, a discrepancy he has stopped correcting because the underestimation is socially useful (criminal utility, plus shields him from expectations he fears failing). Tactical creativity is excellent; strategic long-range planning is poor — he reliably fails to model second-order consequences of his expedient choices. This is not a deficit of intelligence but a *motivated* cognitive narrowing under stress: when Cassie is the variable, he stops modeling anything past the immediate solution.

**Self-concept:** Fundamentally negative core schema ("I am the man who steals, who fails, who needs to be forgiven") wrapped in a functional, likeable, even charming surface. He does not believe he has earned his heroism. The belief that he is fundamentally a screw-up redeemed by other people's grace is the stable load-bearing self-narrative, and *he protects it* — evidence of competence and worthiness is metabolized weakly, evidence of failure is metabolized fully. Classic negative self-schema maintenance.

**Compensatory mechanisms:**
- *Adaptive:* Humor, mentorship of younger heroes (Young Avengers, FF kids), genuine warmth, willingness to take physical risk for others.
- *Maladaptive:* Recurrent low-grade criminality framed as necessity, conflict-avoidance with Cassie that delays honest conversations until they explode, susceptibility to being recruited into bad jobs by people who flatter his competence.

## 4. PSYCHIATRIC

Working differential, derived from the record, not assigned a priori:

**Most defensible primary formulation:** Recurrent Adjustment Disorder with mixed conduct features, on a substrate of unresolved complex bereavement (Cassie's death/loss) and chronic post-incarceration adjustment. Not a personality disorder.

**Supporting evidence:** Lang's pathological behavior is overwhelmingly stressor-triggered, not characterological. In stable periods (mid-80s Avengers tenure; portions of the FF run; recent stabilization) he functions at or above baseline — no antisocial pattern, no narcissistic pattern, no borderline pattern. The criminality recurs *when and only when* a specific stressor configuration recurs: financial crisis + threat to Cassie + opportunity. This is situational, not dispositional.

**PTSD features, subthreshold to threshold depending on era:** Carceral trauma, combat trauma (Avengers-grade conflict), parental loss trauma (Cassie's death), and his own death-and-resurrection. Behavioral evidence — startle, intrusive references, the dissociative flippancy about his death, hypervigilance around authority — supports at minimum a chronic stress-spectrum presentation. Cassie's death almost certainly produced a complex grief presentation that the canon never properly resolves; the resurrection narratively "solves" it but the subject's behavior does not reset.

**Persistent Depressive features (dysthymia-equivalent):** Low-grade negative self-evaluation, baseline pessimism about his own trajectory, humor that functions as depressive armor. Does not meet major depression criteria — energy, function, and hedonic capacity are preserved. But the baseline color is grey.

**Differential — what he is NOT:**

- **Not Antisocial PD.** He has a fully functional conscience, intact empathy, sustained pro-social attachments, and his criminal behavior is *guilt-laden and stressor-bound*, not pleasure-seeking or callous. The recidivism looks antisocial on paper and is not.
- **Not Narcissistic PD.** Self-concept is too negative, mentor-orientation too genuine, willingness to be subordinate too consistent. He does not require admiration; he requires forgiveness, which is a different need.
- **Not Borderline PD.** No identity diffusion (identity is *too* stable, around the negative schema), no abandonment panic except specifically about Cassie, affect is regulated.
- **Not ADHD**, despite surface "scattered" presentation — his attention deficits are stress-bound and motivational, not pervasive.

**Etiology:** A constitutionally warm, intelligent, conscientious man whose formative adult experience (job loss → criminal solution → incarceration → unearned-feeling redemption) installed a permanent template equating *love-for-Cassie* with *justified transgression* and *survival* with *being-vouched-for-by-a-better-man.* Every subsequent stressor reactivates that template.

## 5. PERSONALITY

**Core drives, ranked:**
1. Protect Cassie. Everything else is downstream.
2. Be worthy of the grace others have extended him — without ever fully believing he is.
3. Be useful, specifically through competence others underestimate.
4. Avoid being the man who lets people down, while constantly bracing for the moment he will.

**Worldview / operational code:** The world is composed of people who are better than him and people he must not become. Rules are real and matter, but love overrides rules, and he will be judged for the override — fairly. Authority is to be trusted provisionally and disappointed reluctantly. Most people are decent. He himself is not quite, but is trying. Heroism is something other people do that he is sometimes allowed to participate in.

**Interpersonal pattern:** Warm, disarming, self-deprecating, genuinely curious about other people, quick to defer to perceived betters, slow to assert his own competence. Forms strong vertical bonds (mentors above, mentees below) more easily than lateral peer bonds — peer relationships with figures like Reed or Cap take years to stabilize because he cannot stop positioning them above himself. With women: respectful, slightly avoidant of depth, possibly because the Cassie-cathexis crowds out other intimate investment. With Cassie herself: adoring, guilty, evasive about the parts of his life that would disappoint her, which is precisely the dynamic that eventually breaks the relationship.

**Stress response / escalation ladder:**
- Mild stress: humor increases, self-deprecation increases, competence quietly mobilizes.
- Moderate stress: withdraws from honest conversation with loved ones, begins "managing" information.
- High stress (financial + Cassie-threat): cognitive narrowing, expedient illegality, rationalization engine engages fully.
- Acute crisis: actually excellent. Combat, rescue, life-or-death tactical situations — Lang is calm, creative, and self-sacrificial. The crisis discharges the chronic pressure. He is, paradoxically, healthier in emergencies than in ordinary life.

**Pressure points:**
- Any threat to Cassie collapses the entire decision-tree to a single branch.
- Being called a good father — destabilizing, because he does not believe it.
- Being asked to lead — destabilizing, because his self-concept does not contain "leader," only "deputy of better men."
- Being trusted by a mentor figure — paradoxically destabilizing; the unearned-feeling trust activates the original Pym-grace template and he will overextend to justify it.
- Financial precarity — direct line to the original wound, bypasses all defenses.

**Prognosis if unintervened:** The pattern is durable. He will continue to be a reliable hero in crisis and an unreliable manager of his own civilian life. The Cassie relationship is the only variable that can either heal him (sustained reciprocal honesty) or destroy him (final estrangement). He has never been offered, and has never sought, the one intervention that would actually change the template: the recognition that the grace extended to him by Pym in 1979 was *earned*, not gifted, and that he has been paying interest on a debt that was never owed.
