# UATU ANALYTICAL WORKUP — SUBJECT: MALCOLM DUCASSE

*Stage 1: Pre-Synthesis Intelligence File*

---

## 1. HISTORY — The Behavioral Record

Malcolm Ducasse is a comparatively young figure in the published canon, originating in *Alias* (Bendis/Gaydos, 2001) as a peripheral background fixture — the next-door neighbor in Jessica Jones' Alias Investigations building — before being elevated to a major supporting character across *The Pulse* (2004-2006), *New Avengers*, and most substantively across Brian Michael Bendis' and Kelly Thompson's *Jessica Jones* runs (2016-2020). His arc is one of the most documented portrayals of addiction, exploitation, and post-trauma reconstruction in the modern Marvel record.

**Formative Period — Pre-Kilgrave.** Background indicates an intact, middle-class upbringing (parents present, religiously observant household referenced in *Jessica Jones* vol. 2). He arrives in New York as a young adult with stated intentions of becoming a social worker — a vocational choice that is itself diagnostically meaningful (see §3). Pre-trauma, he is positioned as conscientious, idealistic, somewhat naive, and prosocial.

**The Kilgrave Compromise (*Alias* #24-28, "Purple"; *Jessica Jones* vol. 2 #1-6, "Uncaged").** The single most catastrophic event in Malcolm's biography. Kilgrave (Zebediah Killgrave, the Purple Man) used his pheromonal mind-control to coerce Malcolm into administering and submitting to repeated heroin use, weaponizing him as a surveillance asset on Jessica Jones over an extended period. The exploitation has three distinct trauma vectors: (1) chemical dependency installed without consent, (2) bodily autonomy stripped, (3) instrumentalization against someone he came to love as a friend. He emerges physiologically addicted, psychologically shattered, and carrying chronic guilt for actions he did not choose.

**The Sobriety Era and Adoption by the Jones-Cage Orbit.** Post-Kilgrave, Jessica essentially absorbs Malcolm into her chosen family. He gets clean, enrolls in NA, finishes coursework, and reinvents himself first as Jessica's part-time nanny/assistant (*New Avengers* era), then as her business partner at Alias Investigations (*Jessica Jones* vol. 2-3). The recovery is presented as durable but never frictionless — he attends meetings consistently, sponsors others, and his sobriety is referenced as an ongoing project rather than a closed file.

**The Junior Investigator Arc (*Jessica Jones* vol. 2, 2016-2018).** Malcolm functions as Jessica's apprentice, often the moral check against her self-destructive methodology. Bendis writes him as the conscience of the office: the one who insists clients be told the truth, who pushes back on Jessica's drinking, who maintains relationships when Jessica torches them. Notably, in the "Return of the Purple Children" arc, his confrontation with Kilgrave's surviving offspring forces re-engagement with the original trauma — he holds steady, but the writing shows him sweating it.

**The Thompson Run (*Jessica Jones: Blind Spot*, *Purple Daughter*, 2018-2020).** Malcolm is given more autonomous space. He runs cases independently, manages a complicated relationship (with Kara Lindstrom/"the girlfriend whose secrets he doesn't probe"), and faces a relapse-risk arc during the *Purple Daughter* storyline when proximity to Kilgrave's residue re-traumatizes him. He does not relapse, but the writing makes the temptation explicit.

**Pattern of Evolution.** The longitudinal trajectory is unusually clean for the medium: victim → addict → recovering addict → assistant → partner → independent operator. What persists across all eras and both major writers is (a) reflexive caretaking, (b) a tendency to subordinate his needs to others', (c) discomfort with conflict expressed not as avoidance but as mediation, and (d) a continuing low-grade vigilance around purple-coded stimuli.

## 2. MEDICAL

**Substance Use Disorder, Opioid Type — Iatrogenic/Coerced Onset.** Heroin dependency installed via Kilgrave coercion (*Alias* #24-28). Physiological withdrawal documented in early *Pulse* appearances; sustained remission from approximately 2005 onward in publication time, maintained through 12-step participation. The coerced etiology is medically and psychologically distinct from elective-onset SUD — there was no prodromal pattern of seeking, no pre-existing risk profile in the record — yet the neurobiological dependency, once established, follows the standard course. He carries the lifelong recovering-addict status accurately.

**No documented chronic somatic conditions.** No superhuman enhancement, no augmentation, no recurring injury pattern. He is a baseline-human civilian who occasionally takes incidental injuries in proximity to Jessica's caseload (bruisings, one notable concussion in *Jessica Jones* vol. 2 #10-ish range).

**Neuroendocrine residue of mind-control exposure.** While not a formal diagnosis in any medical universe, the canon depicts somatic stress responses to purple-coded triggers (color cues, Kilgrave's voice on recording, the Purple Children) — autonomic activation consistent with conditioned trauma response. This is medically relevant because it intersects with relapse risk: stress is the dominant relapse driver in opioid SUD, and Malcolm's stressor is recurrent and unavoidable given his professional proximity to Kilgrave's orbit.

**Sleep.** Background depictions show irregular hours, which is occupational rather than pathological, but pertinent to relapse-vulnerability calculus.

## 3. PSYCHOLOGICAL — Reverse-Engineered from Behavior

**Attachment Style — Anxious-Preoccupied with Earned-Secure Trajectory.** Evidence: his repeated pattern of organizing his life around another person's needs (Jessica throughout, his girlfriend, NA sponsees), his difficulty leaving relational systems even when they cost him (he stays in Jessica's orbit through her worst behavior), and his over-availability as a caretaker. However, across the Thompson run, he demonstrates increasing capacity to set limits, maintain a separate relationship, and tolerate disagreement without rupture — markers of movement toward earned-secure functioning. Inference: pre-Kilgrave attachment was likely secure-leaning; trauma destabilized it into anxious patterning; sustained corrective relationship with Jessica and recovery community has gradually rebuilt secure base.

**Primary Defenses.**
- *Reaction formation / altruistic surrender* (A. Freud): the survivor of weaponized harm becomes the protector. His choice to pursue social work pre-trauma is one data point; his post-trauma re-engagement with that helper identity (sponsorship, partnering with a victim like Jessica, gentling clients) is the more diagnostic one. The wound is metabolized by becoming structurally incapable of being the perpetrator.
- *Sublimation*: investigative work as channeled vigilance. The hypervigilance installed by Kilgrave is repurposed into pattern-recognition for cases.
- *Intellectualization* (mild): he frames his recovery in NA's language with some frequency, which is functional but also distancing.
- He notably does *not* use denial, splitting, or projection at clinically significant levels — which is part of why he reads as the "healthy" character.

**Affect Regulation.** Above average. He absorbs Jessica's eruptions without escalating, de-escalates clients, and tolerates ambiguity. Anger is present but channeled — see his cold, contained fury in Purple Children sequences rather than explosive discharge. The regulation appears genuinely consolidated, not suppressed (no evidence of somatic leak, no displaced aggression).

**Cognitive Style.** Concrete-practical with strong interpersonal/social intelligence; not a deductive prodigy in the Sherlock mode, but high in mentalization — he reads people accurately and fast. This is consistent with both his vocational pull toward social work and his survival adaptation under Kilgrave (where reading the controller's mood was protective).

**Self-Concept.** Fragile in one specific compartment — the "what I did under Kilgrave" file — and durable everywhere else. He explicitly does not believe the addiction defines him, and he has done the work to separate coerced action from chosen identity. But the file is not closed; it is filed. Proximity to Kilgrave-coded material reopens it.

**Compensatory Mechanisms.**
- *Positive*: the helper identity, sobriety practice, the chosen family with Jessica/Luke/Danielle.
- *Negative-risk*: over-functioning for others as identity-stabilizer (codependence vulnerability), occasional minimization of his own needs in service of Jessica's chaos.

## 4. PSYCHIATRIC

**Primary Diagnosis — Opioid Use Disorder, In Sustained Remission (DSM-5 equivalent).** Specifier: in a controlled environment is *not* applicable; remission is volitionally maintained in a high-stress occupational context. Etiology is anomalous (coerced onset) but the disorder, once installed, meets full criteria for the active phase and remission course.

**Secondary Diagnosis — Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder, Chronic, with Partial Remission.** Derivation:
- *Criterion A (exposure)*: prolonged coercive control, violation of bodily autonomy, forced participation in harm to others — qualifies on multiple stressor axes.
- *Criterion B (intrusion)*: behavioral evidence of triggered response to Purple Man stimuli (Purple Children arc).
- *Criterion C (avoidance)*: mild — he does not refuse engagement with Kilgrave-coded material, but his discomfort is visible.
- *Criterion D (negative cognition/mood)*: present early-post-trauma, substantially remitted; residual guilt-cognitions ("I was complicit") still detectable.
- *Criterion E (arousal)*: well-regulated baseline, elevation under specific triggers.

PTSD presentation is partial-remission, treated/managed rather than active.

**Differential — What He Is NOT.**
- *Not* Complex PTSD in the full Herman sense: the trauma was bounded (months to a year), not developmental, and his pre-trauma attachment was secure enough to anchor recovery. C-PTSD would predict more pervasive identity disturbance and affect dysregulation than he shows.
- *Not* Dependent Personality Disorder, despite the caretaking pattern. DPD requires submissive clinging across domains and inability to make decisions independently; Malcolm makes independent professional decisions, holds positions against Jessica, and runs his own cases. The caretaking is ego-syntonic vocation, not pathological dependence.
- *Not* Major Depressive Disorder: no anhedonia, no sustained low mood, no vegetative signs in record.
- *Not* a Cluster B presentation of any kind: no entitlement, no instability of self, no manipulation, no impulsivity above baseline.

**Comorbid risk factor (subclinical): codependent relational pattern.** Not a DSM diagnosis but a clinically meaningful pattern given his recovery model and his proximity to a partner (Jessica) whose drinking and self-destructive behavior structurally invite over-functioning.

## 5. PERSONALITY — Synthesis

**Core Drives.** (1) To be useful — operationalized as helping people whose autonomy has been compromised. (2) To remain the person Kilgrave did not make him — sobriety and ethical conduct as ongoing self-authorship against an installed alternative. (3) To belong to a chosen family that knows the worst of him and stayed.

**Worldview / Operational Code.** People are damaged but recoverable; the work is in showing up consistently. Truth-telling is load-bearing — he believes lies metastasize, which is the recovering-addict epistemology applied universally. He distrusts power asymmetries reflexively (trauma-derived) and is unusually attuned to consent in mundane interactions. He does not believe in clean people or clean cases; he believes in honest ones.

**Interpersonal Pattern.** The stabilizer in volatile rooms. Reads affect rapidly, adjusts tone, mediates without performing neutrality. Forms few close attachments but those he forms are durable and high-investment. Warm without being effusive; the warmth is operationally reliable rather than performative — a quality clients and colleagues note and trust over time.

**Stress Response / Escalation Ladder.**
1. *Low stress*: dry humor, increased caretaking of others, more NA-language.
2. *Moderate stress*: withdrawal into work, longer hours, sleep deficit.
3. *High stress* (Kilgrave-coded): autonomic activation, intrusive cognition, *active* relapse risk that he meets with meeting attendance and disclosure to sponsor/Jessica rather than concealment. Critically, his stress response is disclosure-oriented rather than concealment-oriented — a hard-won inversion of the addict's default.
4. *Catastrophic stress*: not yet observed at full ceiling in canon; the closest approximation (Purple Daughter) showed strain without decompensation.

**Pressure Points.**
- Any framing that implies his coerced actions were "really him."
- Being placed in a position to harm someone non-consensually (including via deception).
- Watching Jessica self-destruct without being able to intervene — this taxes the caretaker-as-identity structure.
- Purple-coded sensory material; Kilgrave-adjacent persons (the Purple Children, in particular, as they are both victims and stimuli).
- Being thanked excessively — surfaces unworthiness cognitions tied to the coerced-actions file.

**Operational Summary.** A high-functioning trauma survivor whose recovery is real, tested, and load-bearing for the people around him; whose vulnerabilities are narrow, identified, and managed; and whose stabilizing influence on a volatile professional ecosystem is the most underwritten thing about him on the page. The signal across 20 years of canon is consistent: he is one of the few characters in the Marvel record whose primary arc is not power but the slow, unglamorous construction of an integrated self after that self was stolen.
