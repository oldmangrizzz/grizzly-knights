# UATU ANALYTICAL WORKUP — SUBJECT: BANNER, ROBERT BRUCE, PhD

---

## 1. HISTORY

The subject's behavioral record spans six decades of published observation and is, on close reading, the longest continuous case study of complex post-traumatic dissociation in the fictional canon. The pattern must be read longitudinally because the surface phenotype (a man who transforms into a destructive entity under stress) has remained stable while the underlying etiological picture has been progressively excavated by successive observers.

**Formative period (pre-incident).** The earliest record (Lee/Kirby, *Incident Hulk* #1, 1962) presents the subject as an adult physicist of conspicuously controlled affect, working under military auspices on a gamma-bomb project. Affect is flat; speech is clipped; engagement with his fiancée Betty Ross is muted and his interactions with her father, General Thaddeus Ross, are characterized by submissive avoidance despite open contempt from the General. This pre-incident baseline — a man who has already organized his entire affective life around suppression — is critical: the dissociative apparatus existed before the gamma exposure. The gamma incident did not create the Hulk; it gave a pre-existing dissociated state somatic expression.

The deeper formative record was retroactively recovered by Bill Mantlo (*Incident Hulk* #312, 1985), in what remains the foundational trauma disclosure of the file: Brian Banner, the subject's father — himself a physicist, alcoholic, and paranoid about his son's genetic "contamination" by his own work — subjected the child to chronic physical abuse, killed the subject's mother Rebecca when she attempted to flee with the boy, and was committed. The subject witnessed the matricide, was coerced into perjury at the inquest, and was raised thereafter by an aunt with the trauma never processed. Brian Banner returned in the subject's adulthood; in an altercation the subject killed him (accidentally or not is forever ambiguous in the record) and dissociated the event entirely until forced to recover it in therapy with Doc Samson.

**Early manifestation phase (1962–1970s).** The early Hulk fluctuates wildly across writers — a nocturnal transformation (Lee), then stress-triggered, then a childlike "savage" Hulk. What is diagnostically consistent is the **amnestic barrier**: the subject does not remember Hulk episodes, and the Hulk speaks of Banner in the third person and with hostility ("Banner is the puny one"). This is not a metaphor; it is textbook inter-alter amnesia and persecutory-alter behavior.

**Peter David era (1986–1998, ~12 years of continuous case material).** This is the diagnostic period of the file. Doc Samson conducts extended psychotherapeutic work and explicitly formulates the case as Multiple Personality Disorder (the term of art at the time) secondary to childhood abuse. At least four distinct alters are catalogued: (a) **Savage Hulk** — child-state, ages emotionally around 7, the age of matricide, defensive rage, low cognition; (b) **Mr. Fixit / Grey Hulk** — Vegas-based, gambler, manipulator, nocturnal, holds the subject's suppressed appetitive and aggressive drives in socialized form; (c) **Professor Hulk / Merged Hulk** — Samson's therapeutic achievement, a fusion state holding Banner's intellect with Hulk somatics; (d) **the Guilt Hulk / Devil Hulk** — internalized persecutor modeled explicitly on Brian Banner. The Devil Hulk is the file's most important figure: a sadistic, paternal-voiced internal object that hates the subject and wishes him destroyed, recurring across decades in dream sequences and breakdowns.

**Late-1990s through 2000s.** The "Worldbreaker" period (*Planet Hulk*, *World War Hulk*, Greg Pak) demonstrates what happens when the dissociated rage-state is given a coherent narrative justification: it becomes organized, articulate, and catastrophically effective. This is consistent with structural-dissociation theory: alters can integrate functionally without integrating with the host.

**Aaron / Ewing eras (2010s–2020s).** Jason Aaron's *Indestructible Hulk* depicts Banner attempting a radical reframe — using the Hulk as a deployable asset, essentially a therapeutic containment via instrumentalization. It fails, as such reframes do, because it does not address the wound. Al Ewing's *Immortal Hulk* (2018–2021) is the most clinically literate text in the canon: it presents the alter system as a fully developed dissociative structure, names the Devil Hulk as the introjected abusive father, stages the recovered memory of paternal abuse as the central narrative reveal, and depicts the subject's gradual movement toward inter-alter cooperation rather than suppression — the contemporary trauma-therapy standard.

**Evolutionary read.** Across sixty years, the writers did not invent a psychology; they progressively uncovered one already implicit in Banner's pre-incident affect. The trajectory of the canon is the trajectory of a real trauma case: symptom, denial, partial insight, recovered memory, slow integration.

## 2. MEDICAL

**Pre-incident somatics.** Subject is a thin, somatically underdeveloped male, repeatedly described and depicted as physically slight, with a history (inferred from depictions of childhood) of chronic stress exposure — likely HPA-axis dysregulation, possible chronic sleep disturbance, and the somatic signature of a developmental-trauma survivor (hypervigilance, startle, low baseline weight).

**The gamma incident.** Acute high-dose gamma radiation exposure during the detonation of the Banner-designed gamma device while shielding the civilian Rick Jones. By any conventional radiobiology this is a lethal exposure; the subject's survival and subsequent transformation are the canonical departure point from realism. What matters clinically is that the somatic transformation maps with extraordinary precision onto the subject's psychological structure: the transformation is stress-triggered, the trigger is autonomic arousal (heart rate is the explicit somatic marker across the canon), and the resulting morphology corresponds to whichever dissociated state is in executive control. The body, in other words, has become a literal expression of the dissociative apparatus. This is not incidental — it is the central somatic fact of the case.

**Recurrent injury record.** The subject's body sustains continuous, catastrophic injury in the Hulk state and heals with rapidity that has been characterized as effectively immortal (*Immortal Hulk*). Subjectively, however, the subject experiences the aftermath as bodily violation: he wakes nude, in unfamiliar locations, often bloodied, with no memory of what occurred — phenomenologically indistinguishable from dissociative fugue with somatic sequelae.

**Substance use.** Notably *absent* in the subject, in striking contrast to his father. The subject is teetotal across virtually the entire canon. This is diagnostically significant: the subject has organized his entire life around being not-his-father, which includes rigid abstention from disinhibitors. The Grey Hulk alter, conversely, drinks and gambles — i.e., the appetitive/disinhibited functions have been split off into a dissociated state rather than integrated.

**Suicidality.** Documented attempt in the Defenders era (the "Hulk-proof" bullet attempt) and ideation across multiple arcs. The subject's body refuses to permit completion; this constitutes a recurrent iatrogenic trauma in itself.

## 3. PSYCHOLOGICAL

**Attachment.** The subject's primary attachment figure (mother) was both a source of love and the locus of catastrophic loss; the secondary figure (father) was a terrifying and unpredictable persecutor. This is the textbook substrate for **disorganized attachment** (Main & Solomon), and the adult phenotype is exactly what the literature predicts: simultaneous yearning for and terror of intimacy, oscillation between idealization and withdrawal (the Betty Ross relationship across decades exhibits this perfectly — pursuit, retreat, sabotage, re-pursuit), and inability to sustain proximity without dissociative rupture.

**Defenses.** The defensive architecture is, in Vaillant's terms, almost entirely **immature and primitive** despite the subject's extraordinary cortical capacities. Primary defenses: (1) **dissociation** — structural, body-level, the organizing defense of the case; (2) **splitting** — good Banner / bad Hulk, with all aggression and appetite externalized onto the alter; (3) **isolation of affect** — the subject's pre-incident baseline; (4) **intellectualization** — physics as a domain in which the unbearable can be made abstract and tractable, and in which mastery substitutes for emotional contact; (5) **reaction formation** — pervasive gentleness, soft-spokenness, and pacifism overlying the rage-state. Higher-order defenses (sublimation, humor, altruism) appear sporadically but cannot be sustained under stress.

**Affect regulation.** Catastrophically impaired. The subject has no functional window of tolerance for anger; the affect cannot be metabolized at all and is instead dissociatively expelled into the alter system. This is consistent with a child who learned that any expression of anger meant lethal danger (father) and who had no co-regulating adult to teach affective modulation. The famous line — "don't make me angry; you wouldn't like me when I'm angry" — is, read clinically, the precise self-report of a person who has never been permitted to feel anger and therefore has no graduated relationship to it.

**Cognitive style.** Exceptional fluid intelligence, deployed almost exclusively in service of containment and control — both of physical reality (physics) and of his own internal states (compulsive self-monitoring, heart-rate awareness, meditation regimens). Cognition is recruited as a defense; when defenses fail, cognition is offline (Savage Hulk).

**Self-concept.** Profoundly negative. The subject across the canon expresses self-loathing, describes himself as a monster or a danger, and the *Devil Hulk* / *Guilt Hulk* alter is the externalized voice of his internalized self-hatred — which, importantly, speaks in the father's register. The introjected abuser has become the subject's superego. This is the clearest single piece of evidence for the developmental etiology.

**Compensatory mechanisms — positive.** Genuine altruism (the gamma incident itself, saving Rick Jones, was an act of moral courage); scientific contribution; recurring attempts to use his condition in service of others; capacity for therapeutic alliance (with Samson, with Betty, intermittently with the Avengers).

**Compensatory mechanisms — negative.** Self-isolation, flight (the subject's defining adult behavior is leaving — leaving Betty, leaving cities, leaving the country, leaving the planet), and the externalization of all aggression into a non-self entity for which the subject can disclaim responsibility while nonetheless experiencing the guilt of its actions — a perfect dissociative bind.

## 4. PSYCHIATRIC

**Primary diagnosis — Dissociative Identity Disorder (DSM-5 300.14; ICD-11 6B64).** The evidence is dispositive and is supported by the cited clinical literature (Sar, 2014) on dissociation as the terminal response to chronic developmental trauma. The diagnostic criteria are met explicitly: (A) two or more distinct personality states with marked discontinuity in sense of self and agency — documented as Savage, Grey/Fixit, Professor, Devil Hulk alters; (B) recurrent gaps in autobiographical memory — the Banner-Hulk amnestic barrier is the defining feature of the case; (C) clinically significant distress — pervasive; (D) not part of culturally accepted practice; (E) not attributable to substances. The somatic transformation is unusual but is best understood as the literalization of a switch — the underlying psychiatric structure is DID, not a separate condition.

**Comorbid — Complex Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (ICD-11 6B41).** All three CPTSD criteria beyond standard PTSD are met: (a) affective dysregulation (catastrophic, the central somatic-psychic problem of the case); (b) negative self-concept (pervasive self-loathing, monster-identity); (c) disturbances in relationships (lifelong inability to sustain intimacy). Antecedent: documented chronic physical abuse and witnessed matricide before age 10.

**Comorbid — Major Depressive Disorder, recurrent.** Documented suicidality, anhedonia, low affect baseline, hopelessness across multiple arcs.

**Differential — what this case is NOT.**

- **Intermittent Explosive Disorder.** Superficially fits, but IED has no amnestic barrier, no distinct identity state, no third-person self-reference. Rejected.
- **Bipolar I with manic aggression.** No periodicity, no euthymic-elevated mood states, no pressured speech in the host. Rejected.
- **Substance-induced disorder.** Subject is teetotal; trigger is autonomic arousal, not intoxication. Rejected.
- **Antisocial Personality Disorder.** The Hulk's destruction is reactive, not predatory; the host displays profound guilt, conscience, and altruism — the inverse of ASPD. Rejected.
- **Schizophrenia / psychotic disorder.** The alters are not auditory hallucinations of external origin; the subject does not exhibit thought disorder, negative symptoms in the schizophrenic sense, or delusions outside the dissociative frame. Rejected.
- **"Anger management problem."** This is the lay framing and it is wrong. The subject has the opposite of an anger problem: he has so totally interdicted the experience of anger at the host level that it can only be expressed via a dissociated identity. The presenting symptom is dissociation, not aggression.

**Etiology.** Chronic early physical abuse by a paranoid, alcoholic, intermittently psychotic father; witnessed lethal interpersonal violence (matricide) during the developmental window in which self-structure consolidates; absence of a reparative attachment figure; coerced concealment of the trauma (perjury at inquest), which is itself a known amplifier of dissociative pathology. The gamma exposure is a precipitant of somatic expression, not an etiological agent of the psychiatric condition.

## 5. PERSONALITY

**Core drives.** Two, in unresolvable tension. First, the drive to **contain** — to prevent harm, to be safe, to be good, to be not-his-father. Second, the drive to **be known** — to be loved despite what he is, to stop fleeing, to be held in relationship. Every major arc in the canon is generated by the collision of these two drives: containment requires isolation; being known requires proximity; proximity raises arousal; arousal breaches containment; the alter emerges; he flees; the cycle restarts.

**Worldview / operational code.** The world is unsafe because *he* is unsafe; the appropriate posture is therefore preemptive withdrawal, exhaustive self-monitoring, and instrumental use of intellect to predict and forestall the next breach. Other people are to be protected — from him, principally — and the most loving act available to him is departure. This is the operational logic of a child who learned that his presence brought lethal consequences to the people he loved.

**Interpersonal pattern.** Soft, deferential, intelligent, self-deprecating, and chronically apologetic in baseline state. Forms intense but unsustainable bonds (Betty, Rick Jones, Samson). Withdraws under intimacy. Cannot accept aggression directed at him in conventional contexts (will absorb mistreatment), which is precisely what produces dissociative breach when the threshold finally tips. Submissive to authority figures coded as paternal (Ross), with rage held entirely outside awareness — and then expressed catastrophically and outside his ownership.

**Stress / escalation pathway.** (1) Stressor → (2) host suppresses affect, recruits intellectualization → (3) autonomic arousal climbs past the host's narrow window of tolerance → (4) dissociative switch initiated, host loses executive function → (5) alter takes the field, expresses the interdicted affect at the somatic register the host could never reach → (6) post-episode amnesia, somatic disorientation, shame spiral, flight → (7) re-establishment of containment regime, more rigid than before, which lowers the threshold for the next breach. The cycle is self-reinforcing because each containment failure increases the host's self-loathing, which feeds the Devil Hulk introject, which destabilizes the system further.

**Pressure points.**
- **Betty Ross** — the only sustained adult attachment; threats to her produce the fastest and most complete loss of host control in the record.
- **Children in danger** — touches the unprotected-child substrate directly; produces immediate Savage Hulk emergence.
- **Paternal-coded authority figures** (Ross, Fury at times) — reactivate the original power asymmetry; produce regression and submission in the host, rage in the alter.
- **Being called a monster** — confirms the self-concept and collapses any therapeutic gain.
- **Forced recollection of childhood** — has produced the largest single decompensations in the record.
- **Restraint / confinement** — replays the helplessness of the abused child; produces the most violent alter responses.

**Prognostic note.** The case is, in trauma-therapy terms, not hopeless — the *Immortal Hulk* trajectory of inter-alter cooperation rather than suppression represents the correct clinical direction, and the subject possesses unusually high cognitive and reflective capacity. The obstacles are (a) the somatic literalization of the dissociative apparatus, which makes ordinary therapeutic containment physically impossible, and (b) the absence, across most of his life, of a reliably safe relational environment in which integration could occur. Both are environmental, not constitutional. The subject is treatable. He has rarely been treated.
