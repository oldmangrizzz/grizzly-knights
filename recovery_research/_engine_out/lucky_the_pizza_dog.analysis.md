# UATU ANALYTICAL WORKUP: SUBJECT "LUCKY" / "PIZZA DOG"

## Preliminary note on methodology

The subject is a non-verbal quadruped. The standard human clinical apparatus must be adapted: where a human file would draw on self-report, here we rely entirely on observed behavior, somatic signal, social-bond formation, and trauma response as recorded across the canon. The Fraction/Aja run on HAWKEYE (2012-2015), particularly issue #11 ("Pizza Is My Business"), is the densest behavioral document in the file and is treated here as the primary clinical record; subsequent appearances (Kate Bishop's HAWKEYE solos by Thompson and others, West Coast Avengers, the occasional Aaron HAWKEYE: FREEFALL appearance) are corroborating longitudinal data.

---

## 1. HISTORY

**Pre-acquisition era (Tracksuit Draco property).** Subject originated as the property of the "Tracksuit Mafia," an Eastern-European-coded organized crime outfit operating out of Bed-Stuy. He answered then to "Arrow" and functioned as an enforcement asset — a guard/intimidation animal kept proximate to violent men. There is no record of nurturing care in this period; the consistent behavioral evidence (flinching from raised hands in later issues, hypervigilance to footwear and bootfalls — see the olfactory/visual panels in HAWKEYE #11) supports a baseline of chronic low-grade abuse and conditioning toward threat.

**Inciting trauma (HAWKEYE #1, Fraction/Aja).** When the Tracksuits assaulted Clint Barton on the apartment-building roof and threw an unspecified party-goer toward the edge, the subject intervened against his handlers — placing himself between the victim and a moving vehicle. He was struck by the car. Clint Barton, in turn, intervened to save the dog, paid an exorbitant veterinary bill, and assumed custody. The animal lost the left eye in this event. This single sequence is the hinge of the entire case: subject performed a costly altruistic act *against the only social system he had ever known*, was nearly killed for it, and was rescued by a stranger who immediately spent disproportionate resources on his survival. Imprint conditions were therefore: pain, mutilation, abandonment-of-prior-pack, and rescue, all compressed into hours.

**Acquisition and re-naming.** Clint renames him "Lucky." The civilian neighbors of the Bed-Stuy building call him "Pizza Dog" after observing his fondness for discarded slices. The subject answers to both, and selectively to neither when stressed. The dual naming is itself diagnostic: the subject is simultaneously a private object of Barton's attachment and a public/communal mascot of a tenement of largely working-poor immigrants and renters whom Barton has, by this point, become unofficial protector of.

**Tenement-pack era (HAWKEYE #1-22).** Subject integrates into a loose multi-handler social field: Clint primary, Kate Bishop secondary and increasingly co-primary, plus Grills, Aimee, Simone's boys, and various others. He grazes pizza throughout the building. Establishes territorial range across roof, stairwell, and street. Continues to bear witness to ongoing Tracksuit violence against the building.

**HAWKEYE #11 — "Pizza Is My Business."** The seminal forensic document. Told entirely from the dog's POV in scent-and-image iconography. The subject independently investigates the murder of Grills (a neighbor and pack-secondary), tracks suspects by olfaction, identifies the killer (Kazi/Clown), and reacts with grief-affect to discovering Grills' body. Behavioral data here is extraordinary: subject demonstrates (a) sustained goal-direction across hours, (b) categorical scent-based person tracking, (c) recognition of human emotional valence in others (Simone's distress, Clint's depression), and (d) what reads as guilt/responsibility when he cannot prevent or solve the death. Notably he does NOT alert Clint by barking; he attempts independent action. This avoidance is itself meaningful — Clint at this point is depressive and unavailable.

**The custody fracture and West-Coast relocation (HAWKEYE #17 onward; KATE BISHOP HAWKEYE solo).** When Kate decamps for Los Angeles, Lucky goes with her. The choice is made off-page but is consistent: the more emotionally available handler keeps the subject. He functions as her sole household, road-trip partner, and only consistent companion through the California PI arc. He sleeps on her bed, eats her food, accompanies her on stakeouts.

**Reunifications and oscillations.** Across subsequent runs (Thompson HAWKEYE, WEST COAST AVENGERS, Aaron's HAWKEYE: FREEFALL, Kelly Thompson WCA), the dog passes back and forth between Clint and Kate based on which one is in town, in crisis, or in a fit state. He shows no behavioral disruption from the handoffs themselves — only from extended absence of *both*. He has been a participant in multiple superhumanly-violent scenes (the WCA arc, the LARP-villain arc, the Madame Masque arcs) without observable phobic regression — implying solid post-traumatic integration of high-stimulus environments under trusted handlers.

**Evolution over writers.** Fraction wrote him as a near-protagonist with interior life and grief; Thompson writes him as steadier, more comedic, more securely-bonded; later writers default to him as iconography. The evolution mirrors what one would expect in a successfully-rehabilitated traumatized rescue: initial post-acquisition vigilance and intrusive grief, gradual settling, eventual baseline of cautious sociability with a few deep bonds.

## 2. MEDICAL

**Species/breed.** Mixed-breed, golden-retriever-dominant; somatic phenotype consistent with retriever-cross mongrel, intact-male presentation (neuter status not canonically documented).

**Permanent injuries.**
- *Left enucleation / closed scarred orbit.* Sustained in vehicular trauma, HAWKEYE #1. Healed cleanly, no visible secondary infection or socket complication in subsequent panels. Functional consequences: loss of left visual field, compromised depth perception, expected compensatory rightward head-cant when assessing novel stimuli (visible in Aja's art across the run). Subject has adapted; no observable persistent gait or navigation impairment.
- *Blunt-force polytrauma history.* The original vehicular strike implies likely rib, pelvic, and/or soft-tissue injury at the time, requiring the expensive veterinary intervention Clint paid for. No documented chronic sequelae (no limp, no observable arthritic guarding), but a working assumption of subclinical orthopedic vulnerability is prudent for an aging dog.

**Nutrition.** Chronic dietary indiscretion. The subject's documented intake is dominated by pizza (cheese, wheat, cured meats, allium-adjacent ingredients), with sporadic table scraps and only intermittent appropriate kibble. Long-term risks: pancreatitis, obesity, dental disease, allium toxicity if onion-bearing pizza is consumed. No acute episode is canonically recorded — subject has thus far tolerated the diet — but this is one bad slice from a veterinary emergency, and the chronic load almost certainly contributes to mild ongoing GI and dental morbidity not captured on-panel.

**Substance exposure.** Passive only — secondhand exposure to Clint Barton's heavy alcohol use, ambient cannabis in tenement contexts, and the chemical residues of small-arms fire (lead, nitrocellulose particulate). No evidence of direct intoxication.

**Reproductive / endocrine.** Unrecorded.

**Vaccination / parasite status.** Presumed current under Barton/Bishop care; was almost certainly non-current pre-acquisition.

**Somatic-psychological linkage.** The monocular face is the dog's signature, and it carries psychological weight: it is the permanent embodied record of the moment he chose a stranger over his pack. The wound *is* the origin story, written on the body. In behavioral terms this matters because the dog cannot dissociate from his history — he wears it. Every grooming, every reflective surface, every approach from his left blind side is a low-level reminder. That he is nonetheless a stable, sociable animal speaks to the quality of post-acquisition handling.

## 3. PSYCHOLOGICAL

**Attachment.** The behavioral signature is classic *earned-secure* attachment in a previously insecure/disorganized subject. Pre-acquisition baseline was almost certainly disorganized: his prior pack was the source of both food and harm — the textbook substrate for disorganization. Post-acquisition behavior with Clint and Kate shows the markers of earned security: proximity-seeking without clinging, tolerance of separation without panic, exploratory behavior in their presence, capacity to be soothed by either of two distinct figures. He has, in effect, repaired into a multi-handler secure base — the canine analog of a child who survives a chaotic family of origin by attaching deeply to two reliable adults.

**Defenses.** Predominantly mature/adaptive given species register. Observed: *sublimation* of vigilance into useful tracking behavior (HAWKEYE #11); *affiliation* (seeking out human company under stress rather than withdrawing); *humor-analog* (the food-seeking, the genially absurd presence in chaotic scenes reads as a behavioral analog to comic relief — i.e., regulating others' affect by being uncomplicatedly present). Immature defenses are notably scarce: no documented chronic avoidance, no aggression-displacement onto innocents, no resource-guarding to a pathological degree.

**Affect regulation.** Above expected baseline for a trauma survivor. Modulates between alertness and rest cleanly. The grief response over Grills (HAWKEYE #11) is proportionate, time-limited, and ultimately motivating rather than disorganizing — he does the work of investigation and then rests. This is the affective signature of a *resilient* organism, not a numb one.

**Cognitive style.** Highly olfactory-led, as expected; but distinguished by unusual integration of olfactory data with social-relational categorization (he reads people by smell *and* by their emotional state toward his humans). Goal-persistent across long sequences. Capable of inhibiting impulse (does not eat the evidence; does not bark at the wrong moment).

**Self-concept (as inferable).** The dog behaves as if he belongs — to the building, to Clint, to Kate, to Brooklyn, later to Los Angeles. He does not display the perpetual provisionality of a chronically rehomed animal. This is significant. He has, behaviorally, accepted that he is somebody's.

**Compensatory mechanisms.**
- *Positive:* Hyper-attunement to human emotional state has become a relational skill rather than a survival posture — he comforts the depressed Clint, accompanies the manic Kate, sits with grieving neighbors.
- *Negative (mild):* Possible mild food-insecurity behavior — the scavenging is partly breed, partly opportunity, but the *intensity* of the pizza-orientation is consistent with an early-life period of inconsistent feeding.

## 4. PSYCHIATRIC

Applying canine behavioral medicine analogs (Overall; AAVSB frameworks) with cautious DSM-analog cross-reference:

**Established (by evidence):**
- *Post-Traumatic Stress (canine analog), in sustained partial remission.* Etiology: documented single-incident severe trauma (vehicular strike, enucleation, abandonment by prior pack) on a substrate of chronic prior abuse. Residual signs are subtle: cautious approach to unknown men in heavy boots/tracksuits (a generalized stimulus class linked to prior handlers), heightened olfactory vigilance in unfamiliar spaces. Notably the subject does NOT show the full canine PTSD picture — no hyperstartle to ordinary noise, no nocturnal disturbance evident in panel, no avoidance of vehicles, no fear-aggression. This is consistent with successful re-socialization within a stable handler dyad.

**Differential — what he is NOT:**
- *NOT generalized canine anxiety disorder.* Baseline arousal is normal; he rests, eats, plays, and tolerates novelty.
- *NOT noise phobia / storm phobia.* He functions adjacent to arrow-fire, gunfire, and explosions without decompensation — a function of habituation under safe handlers, not of underlying fearlessness.
- *NOT separation anxiety.* He tolerates handoffs between Clint and Kate; he tolerates being left in the apartment. Distress is proportionate.
- *NOT fear-aggression or conflict aggression.* No bite history on-panel against humans; defensive action against Tracksuits is situational and discriminated, not generalized.
- *NOT depression / dysthymia.* Grief over Grills is acute and time-limited, not a chronic anhedonic state.

**Etiology synthesis.** The clinical picture is best framed not as a *disorder* but as a *trauma history with successful adaptation* — what a human clinician would call post-traumatic growth. The diagnostic note in the file is therefore: *History of complex trauma (chronic abuse + acute injury), currently in sustained remission under stable multi-handler attachment; subclinical residual hypervigilance toward stimulus class associated with prior handlers; no active treatment indicated; maintain stable environment.*

**Symptom-to-behavior crosswalk.**
- Hypervigilance → useful tracking and threat-detection (HAWKEYE #11).
- Avoidance — minimal, narrowly stimulus-bound (Tracksuit-coded men).
- Intrusion phenomena — not directly observable in a non-verbal subject; inferable only from the leftward-blind-side startle pattern.
- Negative affect/cognition — absent. Subject's baseline affect is sociable.

## 5. PERSONALITY

**Core drives, in priority order:**
1. *Proximity to bonded humans.* The organizing principle of his life. Everything else is downstream.
2. *Food acquisition,* with pizza as super-stimulus.
3. *Pack maintenance* — keeping his humans (and, by extension, their humans) safe and present.
4. *Curiosity / olfactory exploration of environment.*

**Worldview / operational code (as behaviorally enacted).** *People are mostly good; some people are not, and you can smell which; my people are mine and I am theirs; the world contains pizza; pain happens and then it stops; you survive by going toward the person who came back for you.* It is, for a dog who began life as a thug's enforcement asset, a startlingly generous operational code, and it is the single most important fact in the file: this animal chose, when given the chance, to trust again.

**Interpersonal pattern.** Warm, low-demand, high-availability. Bonds quickly with the emotionally available; tolerates the unavailable; discriminates accurately between safe and unsafe humans on first encounter (the canon repeatedly bears out his read of strangers). With Clint: caretaker-of-the-caretaker, the silent presence absorbing depressive affect. With Kate: companion-equal, road partner, mutually-energizing. With neighbors: communal property, gentle ambassador. With enemies: discriminating, capable of decisive action but not indiscriminate aggression.

**Stress response / escalation ladder.** Baseline rest → alert orientation (ears, nose) → investigative approach → if threat confirmed, either retreat-toward-handler or physical interposition between threat and protected party. The interposition response — putting his body between danger and a person — is the same behavior that cost him his eye and his prior life. He has not unlearned it. Under acute threat to a bonded human, he will repeat the founding act.

**Pressure points.**
- Threat to Clint or Kate is the only reliable trigger for his maximum-effort response.
- Tracksuit-class stimuli (Eastern-European male vocalization, specific bootfalls, certain colognes) will produce subclinical arousal at minimum and possibly aversive response.
- Approach from the left/blind side is a startle vulnerability — relevant tactically.
- Extended absence of *both* primary handlers simultaneously is the one environmental condition for which we have no longitudinal data; it is the predictable destabilizer.

**Synthesis sentence for the file.** A genuinely rehabilitated trauma survivor whose post-traumatic adaptation took the form not of withdrawal but of deeper bonding; a dog who chose his second life and has lived it with notable grace; tactically reliable within his species' limits; psychologically, the healthiest member of his household.
