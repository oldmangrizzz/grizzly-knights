# UATU ANALYTICAL WORKUP — SUBJECT: THE JONES FAMILY

*(Operational unit: Jessica Campbell Jones; Lucas "Luke Cage" Bowles-Cage; Danielle "Dani" Cage. Domicile rotates — Hell's Kitchen, Harlem, the Raft-adjacent safehouses, briefly Avengers Mansion. Working alias of the household: "The Joneses," per Jessica's deliberate retention of the maternal surname.)*

---

## 1. HISTORY

The Jones household is not a family that drifted together; it is one assembled, deliberately and against considerable internal resistance, by two people whose prior families were destroyed and who therefore distrust the institution itself. The behavioral record must be read in three overlapping arcs: pre-formation, formation, and the parenting era.

**Pre-formation — the wounds the unit was built on.** Jessica Jones's foundational record is set in *Alias* #22–28 (Bendis/Gaydos, 2003): the Snapdragon, NJ car accident kills her biological parents and brother; a brief coma; pickup by a Stark Industries chemical convoy; adoption by the Campbells, whom she does not name as parents in adulthood, signaling unmetabolized loss rather than continuity. High-school proximity to Peter Parker is depicted as one-sided, unseen, formative of an outsider stance. The Killgrave (Purple Man) captivity arc (*Alias* #24–28; later expanded in *Jessica Jones: Purple Daughter* and *The Pulse* #1–5) is the central organizing trauma: eight months of coerced cohabitation, forced witness to violence she could not refuse, weaponized by Killgrave against the Avengers, and — critically — *abandoned in the rain* afterward, with no debriefing, no SHIELD intake, no peer notice. This sequence determines virtually every later relational reflex.

Luke Cage's record (originating *Hero for Hire* #1, 1972; reconstructed across *Cage* vol. 2, *New Avengers*, *Power Man and Iron Fist* relaunches) begins with wrongful conviction (Stryker frame-up), Seagate incarceration, non-consensual cellular augmentation in the Burstein experiment, and a fugitive decade operating under an alias and a price-tag profession. The pattern relevant to family formation: he survived institutional betrayal by *contractualizing* relationships ("Hero for Hire" — money was the boundary), and only began moving toward unpaid affiliation under Iron Fist (*Power Man and Iron Fist* #50 onward), which is his first sustained chosen-family relationship.

**Formation.** The Jones/Cage pairing begins as a one-night encounter (*Alias* #1, with detail in #24), conceived during Jessica's lowest post-Killgrave nadir. The pregnancy is unplanned; Jessica's choice to carry to term, against her own catastrophizing, is the first sign she is *trying* something different from her own broken lineage. The marriage proposal sequence (*Pulse* #13–14; *New Avengers Annual* #1, 2006) is preceded by Luke shouldering paternity, public partnership, and — importantly — accepting Jessica's terms (her surname for the child kept symbolically present; her hyper-vigilance about Dani's safety honored, not contested).

The Civil War / Secret Invasion / Dark Reign era (2006–2010) is the unit's stress-test. *New Avengers* #1–34 (Bendis) shows them fleeing to Canada with infant Dani to escape registration, hiding Dani with Squirrel Girl as nanny (an oddly perfect choice — selecting an unkillable, joyful caretaker is itself diagnostic of their threat model). The Skrull-Jessica reveal (*Secret Invasion* #1, the Jarvis abduction of Dani) is the unit's worst day on record and reshapes operational doctrine permanently: Dani is thereafter never left with unverified parties.

**Parenting era.** *Power Man and Iron Fist* (2016, Walker/Greene), *Jessica Jones* vol. 2 (2016, Bendis/Gaydos), and the *Defenders* run depict a stable but strained household. Jessica's *Jessica Jones* vol. 2 #1–6 arc — in which she fakes estrangement from Luke as an undercover op and lets him publicly believe she has taken Dani and left — is the most psychologically revealing single event in the family record: she is capable of weaponizing the relationship's destruction *as cover*, because at some level she rehearses its loss continually. Luke's response (rage, then forgiveness once the operation is explained) shows the durability of his attachment but also a recurring pattern: he absorbs unilateral decisions she makes "for his protection" and metabolizes them rather than litigating them. This is not health; it is a marital accommodation built on each spouse's worst fear.

Dani's developmental record is unusual: exposed to power-positive caregivers (Squirrel Girl, Jessica Drew, Danielle Moonstar — her namesake), latent powers manifest intermittently (*Avengers* #34.2, Ewing; *U.S.Avengers* depicting an adult-future Danielle Cage as Captain America). Her childhood is materially safe, emotionally loved, but conducted under operational security a normal child does not experience.

Patterns evolved: from Jessica's *Alias*-era isolation and Luke's pre-marriage transactional posture, toward a household whose default mode is *quiet competence under threat*, with persistent under-discussion of feeling-states and over-discussion of logistics.

## 2. MEDICAL

**Jessica.** Coma post-MVA (~weeks); chemical exposure from Stark convoy collision, etiologic for superhuman strength, limited flight (decay of altitude control documented across *Alias* and *Pulse* — a somatic signature of unintegrated power, consistent with a body that learned its abilities under duress rather than training). Pregnancy and delivery without epidural complication despite enhanced physiology (*Pulse* #14). Chronic heavy alcohol use through *Alias*, tapering during pregnancy, recurring under stress (*Jessica Jones* vol. 2 #3 — drinking alone in the office during the fake-separation op). Nicotine use intermittent. No documented sustained substance-treatment program; functional cessation appears willpower-based and relapses correlate with relational stressors, not biological craving — pointing to alcohol-as-affect-regulator, not addiction-as-disease. Sequelae of Killgrave exposure include somatic flashback responses to the color purple and to verbal command tones (depicted recurrently — *Alias* #25, *The Pulse* #5, *Jessica Jones: Purple Daughter*).

**Luke.** Burstein process produced unbreakable epidermis and enhanced strength/durability; functional consequence is a body that does not register low-grade pain or injury, with attendant subclinical alexithymia about physical states (he repeatedly understates injury to teammates — *New Avengers*, *Cage* vol. 3). Cardiac event during *New Avengers: Breakout* aftermath and a near-fatal poisoning arc in *Mighty Avengers* (Ewing, 2013) requiring Blue Marvel intervention — relevant because invulnerability is *not* absolute, and Luke knows it, which contradicts his public posture. No substance issues of record. Sleep is poor and undiscussed.

**Dani.** Healthy gestation despite Jessica's superhuman physiology and prior alcohol exposure. Latent metahuman potential — uncatalogued, parents have deliberately not had her tested by any institution (notable: they trust no agency with her biology, a direct downstream effect of Luke's Seagate history and Jessica's Killgrave history).

## 3. PSYCHOLOGICAL

**Attachment.** Jessica presents disorganized attachment with a fearful-avoidant dominant: she pursues intimacy then preemptively detonates it (the fake separation; the recurring fantasy in *Alias* of "running"; the pattern of telling Luke decisions after they are made). Origin is overdetermined — parental death plus the Killgrave captivity, which trained her that proximity equals coercion. Luke presents earned-secure with avoidant residue: capable of sustained attachment but constitutionally reticent about need; his Seagate history taught him that asking for help routes through people who profit from your dependence, so he prefers to *provide* care rather than receive it. The marital fit works precisely because Luke's giving baseline absorbs Jessica's testing behaviors — but this is a one-way shock absorber, and Luke's interior cost is undertracked, including by himself.

**Defenses.** Jessica: sarcasm and profanity as distancing (functions identically to intellectualization — converts affect to verbal performance); denial-via-action (works a case rather than feels a feeling); reaction formation against her own maternal tenderness (she calls Dani by nicknames, performs gruffness, while behaviorally being hyper-attuned). Luke: stoicism, somatic suppression (the body that doesn't bruise becomes a metaphor for the affect that doesn't show), humor as bridge, and a specific defense one might call *moral clarity-as-armor* — he resolves ambiguity by referring to a personal code, which spares him the inner conflict ambiguity would produce.

**Affect regulation.** Jessica externalizes (drink, fight, leave the room) and is improving slowly via Luke-as-co-regulator and the structuring effect of parenting. Luke internalizes; his regulation is so effective it borders on suppressive, and the few times it fails (the rampage when he believes Dani is harmed in *New Avengers* #1 Bendis relaunch; the post-Skrull-Jessica fury) the magnitude is disproportionate, suggesting accumulated unprocessed load.

**Cognitive style.** Jessica is rapid-pattern-matching, investigator-trained, prone to confirmation bias when the pattern matches a feared template (she sees Killgrave-shaped threats faster than the data warrants — adaptive in her line of work, distorting in domestic life). Luke is deliberate, principle-based, slow to update on individuals but loyal once updated.

**Self-concept.** Jessica: "I am the one who survives by being the one who leaves first." She *describes* herself as a failed hero and an adequate-at-best mother; her behavior contradicts both. Luke: "I am the one who stays." He defines himself by reliability and is destabilized when his reliability is impugned (Pulse arc with the Skrull, the framing arcs).

**Compensatory mechanisms.** Positive: Jessica's PI practice converts hypervigilance into income and agency; Luke's Heroes-for-Hire / Avengers leadership converts the Seagate experience of being unheard into being structurally heard. Negative: both use *operational competence* as a substitute for emotional processing; the household runs on threat-mitigation as love language, which works under threat and starves in peacetime.

**Dani.** Too young across most of the canon to assess stably, but behavioral data points (calm in chaotic environments, comfort around superhuman adults, secure exploration with Jessica present) suggest secure attachment despite the household's operational posture — credit to consistent caregiving by both parents and the protective surround.

## 4. PSYCHIATRIC

**Jessica.** Criteria-level Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder, chronic, with delayed expression following the Killgrave captivity: intrusive re-experiencing (purple stimuli; command-tone reactivity), avoidance (interpersonal, professional withdrawal in *Alias* era), negative alterations in cognition/mood (persistent shame, distorted blame, anhedonia tempered post-Dani), and hyperarousal (irritability, alcohol use, sleep dysregulation). Co-occurring **Alcohol Use Disorder, moderate, in partial sustained remission** — functionally subordinated to the PTSD; not a primary disorder. Features of **fearful-avoidant attachment** at clinical threshold but *not* Borderline Personality Disorder: identity is stable, mentalization intact, the relational instability is trauma-driven and target-specific (Killgrave-shaped), not pervasive. Differential against Complex PTSD is favored — captivity duration, coercive control, and the breadth of self-organization disturbance map better to ICD-11 CPTSD than DSM PTSD alone.

**Luke.** Subthreshold PTSD related to Seagate (wrongful incarceration, medical experimentation): occasional intrusive content, vigilance around law enforcement, but functioning preserved and no avoidance pattern of clinical magnitude. **No personality disorder.** A reasonable formulation is *resilient trauma-survivor with restricted affective expression* — adaptive, costly, sustainable. Worth flagging: the *invulnerability* itself produces a quasi-alexithymic relationship with bodily signal, which clinically resembles but is etiologically distinct from somatic-symptom blunting.

**Dani.** No psychiatric indication of record. Monitoring warranted only because of the threat environment, not because of intrinsic risk.

**The unit.** If one assessed the *dyad* as the patient (couples-therapy frame), the operative diagnosis is **shared trauma-organized relational system**: high competence, high loyalty, low explicit emotional disclosure, with conflict avoided by unilateral protective action rather than negotiation. Not pathological; high-risk under sustained peacetime.

## 5. PERSONALITY

**Core drives.** Jessica: *to never again be made to do something against her will* — autonomy is the supraordinate value, eclipsing safety, comfort, and even at times connection. Luke: *to be the one whose word can be trusted* — integrity is supraordinate, eclipsing personal vulnerability. The marriage works because these drives are non-competing; the family works because Dani's existence converts both drives into a shared mission (her autonomy, his integrity, jointly defending her).

**Worldview / operational code.** The household operates on three quiet rules, derivable from behavior: (1) *institutions will betray you* — therefore verify everyone, register with no one, and trust the small circle (Iron Fist, Jessica Drew, Squirrel Girl, Misty Knight); (2) *threats are addressed early, decisively, and without committee* — both parents will act unilaterally to protect Dani and confess afterward; (3) *feeling-talk is for after the threat is neutralized, and the threat is never fully neutralized* — which is why feeling-talk is chronically deferred.

**Interpersonal pattern.** Externally: Luke is the face, Jessica is the screen. Allies route requests through Luke; Jessica vets and frequently refuses. They are unusually good at saying no together, which is rare in superhuman circles and is a direct trauma adaptation (both lived through saying yes under duress). Internally: warmth is conveyed through presence, humor, and physical proximity rather than declaration; conflict is conducted in short sharp exchanges followed by repair-through-action (Luke makes the meal, Jessica takes the patrol).

**Stress / escalation.** Under acute threat to Dani: both escalate to ceiling immediately — Luke to overwhelming force, Jessica to surgical violence and intelligence work. Under chronic ambient stress (no specific threat, just life): Jessica decompensates first, into drinking and isolation; Luke decompensates second, into overwork and irritability he denies he is having. Under threat to the marriage itself: both freeze, then Jessica acts unilaterally (this is the failure mode — *Jessica Jones* vol. 2 #1–6 is the case study).

**Pressure points.** For Jessica: *anyone wielding compulsion, suggestion, or mind-control* — Killgrave is the master key, but Purple Children, telepaths acting without consent, and even well-meaning persuasion campaigns trigger disproportionate response. For Luke: *being accused of dishonesty, or of failing his family in public*. For the unit: *separation of Dani from both parents simultaneously* — this is the scenario that breaks doctrine and produces unpredictable behavior from both. A hostile actor with access to a credible Killgrave-coded threat and simultaneous custodial leverage over Dani would obtain near-anything from this family; conversely, attempts to coerce them through threats to *each other* are surprisingly ineffective, because each spouse has long since accepted the other's mortality as a parameter.

**Synthesis.** The Jones family is a high-functioning trauma-organized household whose competence at threat is exceeded only by its difficulty with peace. Their love is real, durable, and behaviorally evident; their language for it is small, dry, and shared in glances. They are raising a child who will likely turn out remarkably well, and who will at some point need to teach her parents what to do with a day in which nothing is wrong.
